What is 360-Feedback?

Overview

360-Feedback is Bayzat's multi-rater performance evaluation tool that enables organizations to collect comprehensive feedback about employees from multiple sources. Unlike traditional top-down reviews, 360-Feedback gathers input from managers, peers, direct reports, and the employee themselves, providing a holistic view of performance, competencies, and development areas.

The feature integrates with Bayzat's Performance Management system, allowing HR administrators and managers to design customized review cycles, configure rating scales, and generate actionable insights that support employee growth and organizational development.

Key Benefits

  • Comprehensive Perspective: Collect feedback from multiple sources to eliminate bias and provide balanced performance insights
  • Customizable Evaluation: Configure rating scales from 3 to 10 points with custom labels and descriptions to match your organization's evaluation framework
  • Development-Focused: Identify strengths and development areas through structured, multi-dimensional feedback
  • Flexible Configuration: Include optional 'Not Applicable' (N/A) options for competencies that don't apply to specific roles
  • Performance Integration: Seamlessly connects with goal reviews and performance evaluations within Bayzat

Technical Foundation

Rating Scale Configuration

360-Feedback relies on customizable rating scales that must be configured in Settings > Performance > Rating Scale. Key specifications:

  • Scale Range: Choose between 3 to 10 rating points
  • Default Scale: System provides a 5-point scale by default
  • Custom Labels: Each rating point can have a custom label (e.g., "Exceeds Expectations", "Meets Expectations")
  • Descriptions: Add detailed descriptions to each rating point to ensure consistent interpretation
  • N/A Option: Optionally include a "Not Applicable" option for competencies that may not apply to all roles
Best Practice: Define clear, behaviorally-anchored descriptions for each rating point to reduce rater bias and improve feedback consistency across reviewers.

User Journey

Typical Use Case Scenarios

Scenario 1: Annual Performance Review

Organization conducts comprehensive 360-degree reviews annually for all employees. Each employee receives feedback from their manager, 3-5 peers, and completes self-assessment. Results inform performance ratings, compensation decisions, and annual development plans.

Scenario 2: Leadership Development

High-potential employees identified for leadership roles undergo 360-degree assessment focused on leadership competencies. Feedback from direct reports, peers, and senior leaders provides insights into leadership effectiveness and areas for development before promotion.

Scenario 3: New Manager Transition

Employee recently promoted to management role receives 360-degree feedback after 90 days. Feedback from direct reports, peers, and manager helps new leader understand management effectiveness and adjust approach during critical transition period.

Scenario 4: Project-Based Feedback

Cross-functional project team members provide 360-degree feedback to each other at project completion. Feedback focuses on collaboration, communication, and contribution to project success, informing future team assignments.

Initial Setup

Setting up the 360-feedback feature requires careful configuration to ensure the review process aligns with your organization's performance management goals. This section guides you through the essential setup steps.

Prerequisites

Before beginning setup, ensure you have:

  • Administrator permissions for Performance Management module
  • Defined competency framework or performance criteria to evaluate
  • Identified review cycle schedule (annual, bi-annual, quarterly)
  • Stakeholder alignment on rating methodology and scale
  • Communication plan for rolling out 360-feedback to employees

Step 1: Configure Rating Scale

The rating scale is the foundation of your 360-feedback system. Configure it first before creating review templates.

  1. Navigate to rating scale settings
    Settings → Performance → Rating Scale
  2. Click 'Edit Rating Scale' to begin configuration
  3. Select number of rating points
    • Choose between 3 and 10 rating points
    • Common scales: 3-point (Below/Meets/Exceeds), 5-point (1-5 scale), 7-point (detailed gradations)
    • Consider: More points provide granularity but may reduce inter-rater reliability
  4. Decide on 'Not Applicable' (N/A) option
    • Check box to include N/A option
    • Useful when competencies don't apply to all roles or situations
    • Recommended for comprehensive competency frameworks
  5. Customize labels for each rating point
    • Click on each rating point to edit label
    • Use clear, descriptive labels (e.g., "Exceptional", "Exceeds Expectations", "Meets Expectations")
    • Ensure labels are understood consistently across organization
  6. Add descriptions for each rating point
    • Provide detailed description of what each rating means
    • Include behavioral indicators or performance examples
    • Helps ensure consistent interpretation by all reviewers
  7. Save rating scale configuration

Rating Scale Best Practices

Scale TypePointsBest ForConsiderations
Simple3Quick assessments, clear distinctionsLess nuance, easier consensus

Detailed7-10Precise differentiation, high-stakes decisionsRequires clear definitions, may reduce reliability
Recommendation: Start with a 5-point scale if unsure. This provides sufficient granularity while remaining easy to understand and apply consistently. The default 5-point scale is pre-configured and can be customized to match your organization's terminology.

Step 2: Define Review Templates

After configuring the rating scale, create review templates that define what competencies and questions will be evaluated.

  1. Navigate to review templates
    Settings → Performance → 360-Feedback → Templates
  2. Click 'Create New Template'
  3. Enter template details
    • Template name (e.g., "Annual 360 Review", "Leadership Assessment")
    • Description of template purpose
    • Target roles or employee groups
  4. Add competency categories
    • Create sections for different competency areas (e.g., Technical Skills, Leadership, Communication)
    • Organize questions logically by category
    • Consider role-specific vs. universal competencies
  5. Add review questions
    • Write clear, specific questions for each competency
    • Assign rating scale to each question
    • Include option for written comments
    • Mark questions as required or optional
  6. Configure question visibility
    • Specify which questions appear for self-assessment
    • Specify which questions appear for peer review
    • Specify which questions appear for manager review
    • Some questions may be role-specific
  7. Save and activate template

Step 3: Set Up User Roles and Permissions

Ensure appropriate users have correct permissions to participate in 360-feedback process.

  1. Review default permission groups

  3. Identify review administrators

    • Document escalation process for issues

Step 4: Configure Notifications and Reminders

Set up automated communications to keep review process on track.

  4. Configure completion notifications

Edge Cases

Rating Scale Boundaries

When working with rating scale limits, be aware of the following edge cases:

  • Minimum Scale (3 points): While functional, a 3-point scale may provide limited granularity for detailed performance assessments. Consider whether this meets your evaluation needs.
  • Maximum Scale (10 points): A 10-point scale offers high granularity but may be difficult for reviewers to differentiate between adjacent points. Ensure clear descriptions for each level.
  • N/A Option Usage: When N/A is enabled, ensure reviewers understand when it's appropriate to use this option versus providing an actual rating.

Configuration Changes

⚠️ Impact of Rating Scale Changes

Changes to rating scales affect future reviews only. Historical review data remains unchanged and continues to display using the rating scale that was active at the time of the review.

Recommendation: Plan rating scale changes carefully and communicate changes to all users involved in the performance review process.

Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

What is the 360 Feedback feature?
360 Feedback is a performance evaluation tool that allows organizations to collect comprehensive feedback on employees from multiple sources, including peers, managers, and direct reports, using customizable rating scales.
Who can configure rating scales?
Only users with Admin permissions for Performance Management can configure and modify rating scales.
Can I use different rating scales for different types of reviews?
The system uses a single rating scale configuration. All performance reviews within the organization will use the same rating scale settings.

Rating Scale Configuration

How many points can my rating scale have?
Rating scales can have between 3 and 10 points. The default configuration is a 5-point scale.
Can I change the rating scale after reviews have been completed?
Yes, but changes only apply to future reviews. Completed reviews will continue to display the rating scale that was active when they were conducted.
What is the N/A option and when should I use it?
The N/A (Not Applicable) option allows reviewers to indicate when a particular criterion doesn't apply to the employee being reviewed. This is optional and can be enabled or disabled in the rating scale settings.
Can I customize the labels for each rating point?
Yes, each rating point can have a custom label and description to align with your organization's performance evaluation criteria.

Best Practices

What's the recommended number of rating points?
A 5-point scale (the default) is commonly used as it provides sufficient granularity while remaining easy for reviewers to use. However, the optimal number depends on your organization's evaluation needs and culture.
Should I include the N/A option?
Include the N/A option if your reviews contain criteria that may not apply to all employees. This prevents forced ratings on irrelevant criteria.
How detailed should my rating descriptions be?
Provide clear, specific descriptions for each rating level to ensure consistency across reviewers. Include behavioral examples or performance indicators where possible.

Glossary of Terms

Glossary

360 Feedback
A comprehensive performance evaluation method that gathers feedback from multiple sources including peers, managers, direct reports, and sometimes external stakeholders, providing a well-rounded view of an employee's performance.
Rating Scale
A standardized measurement tool used to evaluate and score employee performance across various criteria. In Bayzat, rating scales can be configured with 3-10 points.
Rating Point
An individual level within a rating scale, representing a specific performance level. Each point can have a custom label and description.
N/A (Not Applicable)
An optional rating scale option that allows reviewers to indicate when a particular evaluation criterion does not apply to the employee being reviewed.
Performance Management
The Bayzat module that encompasses tools and features for managing employee performance, including reviews, goal setting, and feedback mechanisms.
Admin
A user role with elevated permissions that can configure system settings, including rating scales and performance management features.
Review Cycle
A defined period during which performance reviews are conducted, typically aligned with organizational evaluation schedules (e.g., annual, semi-annual, quarterly).
Reviewer
