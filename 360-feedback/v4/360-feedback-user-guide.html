What is 360 Feedback?

Overview

360 Feedback is Bayzat's performance evaluation tool that enables organizations to collect comprehensive feedback about employees from multiple sources. Unlike traditional top-down reviews, 360 Feedback gathers insights from peers, direct reports, managers, and other stakeholders to provide a well-rounded view of employee performance and development areas.

The feature allows HR administrators and managers to design custom surveys, select participant groups, and collect structured feedback using configurable rating scales and question types. This multi-dimensional approach helps identify strengths, development opportunities, and blind spots that might not be visible in traditional performance reviews.

Key Benefits

  • Holistic Performance View: Gather feedback from multiple perspectives to understand how employees are perceived across different working relationships
  • Flexible Survey Design: Create surveys using AI-assisted question generation or manual customization with various question types
  • Customizable Rating Scales: Configure rating scales from 3-10 points with custom labels and descriptions to match your evaluation criteria
  • Structured Feedback Collection: Organize feedback by participant groups (up to 4 per review) for targeted insights

Who Uses This Feature?

HR Administrators
Configure rating scales, create survey templates, manage review cycles, and oversee the overall 360 feedback process
Managers
Initiate 360 reviews for team members, select participants, and access feedback reports to support performance discussions
Employees
Participate as reviewers by providing feedback on colleagues, and receive consolidated feedback reports for their own development

Business Value

360 Feedback supports organizational development by:

  • Providing objective, multi-source performance data for more informed talent decisions
  • Identifying leadership potential and development needs across the organization
  • Fostering a culture of continuous feedback and transparency
  • Supporting succession planning with comprehensive competency assessments
  • Reducing bias by incorporating diverse perspectives in performance evaluations

Setting Up Rating Scales

Rating scales are configured separately and apply to performance reviews and 360 feedback:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Performance → Rating Scale
  2. Click Edit Rating Scale
  3. Select the number of rating points (3-10 points)
  4. Choose whether to include a "Not Applicable" (N/A) option
  5. Customize labels for each rating point (e.g., "Exceeds Expectations", "Meets Expectations")
  6. Add descriptions for each rating level to guide reviewers
  7. Save the rating scale configuration
Default Configuration: The system uses a 5-point rating scale by default. Customize this based on your organization's performance evaluation methodology.

Managing Participant Groups

When creating 360 reviews, you can select up to 4 participant groups per review:

  • Self-review: Employee evaluates themselves
  • Manager review: Direct manager provides feedback
  • Peer review: Colleagues at similar levels provide input
  • Direct report review: Subordinates evaluate leadership (if applicable)

Select participant groups based on the feedback perspectives needed for comprehensive evaluation.

Survey Question Types

Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

What is 360 Feedback and who should use it?

360 Feedback is a comprehensive performance evaluation method that collects feedback about an employee from multiple sources: managers, peers, direct reports, and self-assessment. HR administrators and managers use this feature to gather well-rounded performance insights for employee development, performance reviews, and succession planning.

Do I need special permissions to create 360 reviews?

Yes, you need access to the Performance module and appropriate user role permissions. Typically, HR administrators and managers with performance management rights can create and manage 360 reviews. Contact your system administrator if you cannot access the Performance section in the sidebar navigation.

How many people can participate in a 360 review?

Each 360 review can include up to 4 distinct participant groups. Within each group, you can include multiple individuals. For example: Group 1 (Managers), Group 2 (Peers - 5 people), Group 3 (Direct Reports - 3 people), Group 4 (Self-assessment).

Can I schedule 360 reviews to run automatically?

No, automated scheduling is not currently available. All 360 reviews must be manually initiated by an administrator. You'll need to track review cycles externally and manually create reviews when needed. See limitation reference: TSSD-363, JRV-1912.

Survey Creation and Management

Can I edit a survey after creating it?

You can edit surveys only before any responses have been submitted. Once participants begin responding, the survey becomes locked to maintain data integrity. Plan your surveys carefully and use the preview function before launching.

What types of questions can I include in surveys?

360 Feedback surveys support multiple question types including rating scales, open-ended text responses, and multiple choice questions. Rating scale questions require that you first configure a rating scale in Settings → Performance → Rating Scale.

How do I create surveys quickly?

Bayzat offers AI-assisted survey creation to help you generate relevant questions based on competencies and evaluation criteria. You can also create surveys manually by adding questions one by one, or use templates if available.

Can participants save partial responses and return later?

This depends on your survey configuration. However, it's best practice to encourage participants to complete surveys in one session to ensure responses are properly saved. Incomplete sessions may not retain partial data.

Reporting and Results

Where can I view 360 review results?

Results are available within the 360 Feedback section of the Performance module. However, note that comprehensive reporting features are limited (see limitation TSSD-2128). You may need to export data and create custom reports externally for detailed analysis.

Are participant responses anonymous?

Anonymity settings depend on how you configure the review. Clearly communicate to participants whether their responses will be anonymous or attributed. This should be established during survey setup.

Can employees see their own 360 feedback results?

Access to results depends on your organization's configuration and permissions. Typically, HR administrators and managers can view results, and you can choose whether to share results with the employee being reviewed.

Technical Issues

Why can't I access the Performance module?

If the Performance menu item in the sidebar doesn't work or returns a 404 error, the Performance module may not be enabled for your organization. Contact your Bayzat account administrator or Bayzat support to verify module activation and your user permissions.

What should I do if a participant didn't receive the survey invitation?

Verify that the participant's email address is correct in their employee profile, they have an active Bayzat account, and appropriate permissions. Check spam/junk folders. If issues persist, you may need to manually share the survey link.

Glossary of Terms

360 Feedback Terminology

360 Feedback
A comprehensive performance evaluation method that collects feedback about an employee from multiple sources including managers, peers, direct reports, and self-assessment. Also known as 360-degree review or multi-rater feedback.
Participant Group
A category of reviewers who provide feedback in a 360 review. Common groups include managers, peers, direct reports, and self-assessment. Each 360 review can include up to 4 participant groups.
Rating Scale
A numerical scale (3-10 points) used to grade responses in performance surveys. Must be configured in Settings → Performance → Rating Scale before use in surveys. Can include an optional "Not Applicable" (N/A) option.
Survey
A structured questionnaire containing multiple questions used to collect feedback from participants. Surveys can include rating scale questions, open-ended text questions, and multiple choice questions.
Review Subject
The employee who is being evaluated in a 360 review. Feedback is collected about this person from various participant groups.
Reviewer / Participant
An individual who provides feedback about the review subject. Reviewers are organized into participant groups based on their relationship to the subject.
Self-Assessment
A participant group where the review subject evaluates their own performance. This provides a comparison point between self-perception and others' feedback.
Multi-Source Review
Another term for 360 feedback, emphasizing that feedback comes from multiple sources rather than a single manager or supervisor.
Survey Immutability
The rule that surveys cannot be edited or modified once participants have begun submitting responses. This ensures data integrity and fairness in the evaluation process.
Anonymous Feedback
Responses where the reviewer's identity is not disclosed to the review subject or other participants. Anonymity settings are configured during survey setup.
Performance Module
The Bayzat module that contains 360 Feedback, performance reviews, goal management, and related features. Must be enabled for your organization to access 360 Feedback.
AI-Assisted Survey Creation
A feature that uses artificial intelligence to generate relevant survey questions based on competencies, job roles, and evaluation criteria, speeding up the survey creation process.
Question Type
The format of a survey question. Common types include rating scale (numerical scoring), open-ended text (free-form responses), and multiple choice (select from predefined options).
Response Collection Phase
The period during which participants can submit their feedback. Once this phase begins, the survey becomes locked and cannot be edited.
Competency
A specific skill, behavior, or attribute being evaluated in a performance review. Examples include leadership, communication, technical skills, and teamwork.
Evaluation Cycle
The recurring period during which performance reviews are conducted, such as annually, semi-annually, or quarterly. In Bayzat, 360 reviews must be manually initiated for each cycle.
Feedback Report
A summary of responses collected from all participants in a 360 review. Note: Comprehensive reporting features are limited in the current version (see known limitations).
N/A (Not Applicable)
An optional rating scale response indicating that a particular question or criterion does not apply to the review subject's role or situation.
Preview Mode
A function that allows survey creators to view how the survey will appear to participants before launching it for response collection.
User Role Permissions
Access rights that determine which users can create, manage, view, or participate in 360 reviews. Typically managed by HR administrators in Settings → Users & Roles.
