Attendance Management

Track employee work hours, shifts, and location with automated attendance management

What is Attendance Management?

Overview

The Attendance module is a comprehensive time and attendance tracking system that enables organizations to monitor employee work hours, manage shift schedules, and maintain accurate attendance records. The system supports mobile app-based check-ins, biometric device integrations, and geo-fencing for location verification.

Key Benefits

  • Real-time check-in/check-out tracking via mobile app with geo-fencing
  • Flexible shift scheduling with static and dynamic shift support
  • Automated notifications 10 minutes before check-in/out times
  • Comprehensive attendance reports with daily, custom, and location views
  • Attendance regularization workflow for correcting missed entries

Product Foundation Overview

How Attendance Fits

Attendance is a transactional tracking system that records employee check-ins/check-outs and validates them against configured work timings. Daily attendance feeds into payroll calculations, overtime, and leave records.

Mental model: Work Timing (rules) → Employee Check-in → Validation → Attendance Record → Payroll/Reports

Attendance records are the source of truth for working hours. Changes to work timings affect how future check-ins are validated.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before configuring attendance:

  • Check-in method? — Mobile app, biometric devices, or both?
  • Location verification? — Enable geo-fencing? What radius?
  • Identity verification? — Require facial recognition with liveness detection?
  • Work centers needed? — Single office or multiple locations with different shifts?

Related Features

  • Work Timings — Define schedules, thresholds, and overtime rules that validate check-ins
  • Work Centers — Physical locations with their own shift schedulers
  • Biometric Integration — Physical devices for attendance tracking via API/CAMS
  • Payroll — Attendance data feeds into salary calculations
  • Leave Management — Approved leave affects expected attendance

Flexible Work Timings

What are Flexible Work Timings?

Flexible work timings allow employees to have control over their daily working hours. Instead of a fixed schedule (like 9-to-5), employees can choose when to start and end their workday, as long as they work the required number of hours.

Benefits

  • For Employees: Better work-life balance, reduced commuting time, accommodate personal commitments
  • For Employers: Increased productivity, higher job satisfaction, reduced absenteeism

How Extra Hours Work

Employees are expected to fulfill the required working hours within the start and end time limits. If an employee logs more hours than expected, the extra time is captured as extra hours on Time and Pay Adjustments. No action is taken if fewer hours are logged.

Note: Employees can check in before the start time limit with no system restrictions.

Overtime Policy Setup

What is an Overtime Policy?

An overtime policy captures additional hours worked by employees and determines how they are compensated through payroll.

Settings → Attendance → Overtime Policy → Add New

How to Set Up an Overtime Policy

1

Create the Policy

Navigate to Settings → Attendance → Overtime Policy and click Add New. Enter a name for the policy.

2

Configure Request Settings

Define whether employees can request extra hours by enabling/disabling the toggle. Also configure whether managers without payroll roles can see the net overtime amount.

3

Select Hourly Rate Type

Choose between:

  • Single Rate: One rate for all overtime
  • Custom Rate: Different rates for weekdays, weekends, public holidays, and days off (days off only applies to shift employees)
4

Configure Rate Details

For both single and custom rates, configure:

  • Rate Type: Unified (single rate) or Split (combination of different salary types)
  • Rate Multiplier: Default is 1.5 (minimum 0.1, no maximum, rounded to 2 decimals)
  • Salary Component: "Basic Salary" or "Basic Salary + Allowances"
  • Days Calculation: Actual Calendar Days, Actual Working Days, or Custom Days (default 30)
  • Number of Hours: Default is 8 hours
5

Assign Employees

Click on employee names or the '+' icon to assign them to the policy. Note: Each employee can only be assigned to one overtime policy at a time.

6

Save the Policy

Review your settings and click Save.

Daily Rate Formula: Salary Component (Basic OR Basic + Allowances) / Days (Custom OR Calendar OR Working) × Working hours per day

Important: If an employee shows "Unavailable" when assigning, they are already assigned to another overtime policy. Remove them from the existing policy first.

Time and Pay Adjustments

Overview

Time and Pay Adjustments is where Line Managers and HR Managers process entries for extra hours and attendance violations (late arrivals, early departures, absences).

Attendance → Time and Pay Adjustments

Related Policies

  • Overtime Policy - For compensating extra hours via payroll
  • Day in Lieu Policy - For compensating extra hours via time off
  • Deduction Policy - For handling attendance violations

Workflow Overview

All entries created from attendance tracking appear under the Pending Tab. Managers then take appropriate actions to move entries through the workflow.

Actions for Line Managers

Action Description
Review Review violation entries (Late arrival, Early departure, Absent) and extra hours entries. Status changes to 'Reviewed' in Pending Tab.
Unreview Revert a reviewed decision using the kebab menu (three dots)
Reject Move entry to Rejected tab. Can be moved back to Pending using 'Move to Pending' action.

Work Disruption: When reviewing violation entries under the advanced tracking method, Line Managers must select 'Yes' or 'No' for work disruption.

Actions for HR Managers (Super Admin)

Action Description
Approve for Payroll Approve violation entries and extra hours (overtime) for payroll processing
Approve for Time Off Approve extra hour entries (days in lieu) as time off
Review & Approve For entries requiring work disruption selection that haven't been reviewed by Line Manager

Entry Movement Rules (Advanced Tracking)

Forward movement (Pending → Approved/Rejected → Processed):

  • Entries must be moved from smaller occurrence to higher occurrence in order
  • Example: If you have occurrences 1, 2, 3 in Pending Tab, approve them in order 1 → 2 → 3

Backward movement (Approved/Rejected → Pending):

  • Entries must be moved from higher occurrence to smaller occurrence in order
  • Example: If you have occurrences 1, 2, 3 in Approved Tab, move to Pending in order 3 → 2 → 1

Exception: REJECT can be performed on entries in the Pending Tab in any order.

Split Shifts and Extra Hours

For employees with split shifts (e.g., Morning 9 AM - 2 PM and Evening 7 PM - 11 PM):

  • Extra hour entries are created for each individual shift
  • Both entries must be processed to pay overtime for that day
  • Scheduling extra hours from Time and Pay Adjustments is restricted if there is more than one shift in a day

Deductions for Split Shifts

When calculating deductions for split shift employees:

  • The final deduction is halved and distributed evenly across shifts
  • This prevents double deductions for split shift employees

Known Limitations

Half-Day Leave: Employees cannot record attendance and HR cannot view or edit attendance for half-day leave periods. This is a current system limitation.

Attendance Variance Reporting: Cannot export or display pre-edit attendance values in reports, limiting tracking of attendance modifications.

Overtime Validation: Hours worked are calculated automatically without validating checkout legitimacy or indicating missing/manipulated records.

Edge Cases

  • Multiple location visits: Enable Multiple Visits feature for employees who work at multiple client sites daily
  • Biometric vs App conflicts: When both methods are used, ensure device serial numbers are correctly mapped to offices
  • Timezone considerations: Attendance times are recorded in the configured office timezone

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my check-in being rejected?

Check-ins are rejected if the shift is not published, you're outside the geo-fence radius, or you're checking in outside the allowed time threshold. Contact your manager or HR for assistance.

How do I correct a missed check-in or check-out?

Submit an Attendance Regularization request through Requests → My Tickets → Create Ticket → Select "Attendance Regularization". Your manager will review and approve the correction.

Can I check in from home or a remote location?

This depends on your company's geo-fencing policy. If remote work is allowed, your HR administrator may configure specific locations or disable geo-fencing for your profile.

Why don't I see my overtime hours?

Overtime tracking requires you to be assigned to an overtime policy. Contact your HR administrator to verify your policy assignment.

How do notifications work?

The system sends push notifications 10 minutes before your scheduled check-in and check-out times. Ensure notifications are enabled for the Bayzat app on your device.

How do I track visits to multiple locations during the day?

Use the Multiple Visits feature in the Bayzat mobile app. After checking in for the day, go to the Attendance widget and select "Start Visit". Choose your location from the map, and when you're done, select "End Visit". Repeat for each location you visit. You can view your complete visit timeline in Attendance → My Attendance.

What if my visit location doesn't appear in the list?

If your company allows it, you can add your current location by selecting "Add your current location" when starting a visit. Make sure you're at the actual meeting spot first, as only locations within your configured radius will appear. Enter the location name and details, then save it for future use.
