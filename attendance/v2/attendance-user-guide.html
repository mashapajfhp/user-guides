What is Attendance?

Overview

Attendance tracks employee work hours through mobile check-ins, biometric devices, or manual entry. Access it from Time > Attendance > Employee attendance for admin reporting or Time > Attendance > My attendance for employee self-service.

View attendance records, edit check-in/out times, generate reports, track overtime and deductions, and export data for payroll processing.

Key Benefits

  • Eliminate manual timesheet entry and spreadsheet tracking
  • Automate attendance deductions and overtime calculations for payroll
  • Track work hours across multiple locations and shifts
  • Enable employees to regularize attendance through approval workflows
  • Generate compliance reports for labor law requirements
  • Integrate biometric devices or use mobile app check-ins

Product Foundation Overview

How Attendance Fits

Attendance is transactional data that captures employee work hours, check-ins, check-outs, breaks, and schedule adherence. It sits at the intersection of Time Tracking and Payroll, feeding data into compensation calculations and leave management.

Mental model: Work Timing (template) ‚Üí Assigned to Employee ‚Üí Employee checks in/out ‚Üí Attendance record created ‚Üí Data flows to Payroll/Leave/Deductions

Attendance records affect every employee with assigned work timings and impact payroll, overtime calculations, leave balances, and compliance reporting across the organization.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before configuring Attendance:

  • What work timings do you need? ‚Äî Fixed schedules, flexible hours, shift work, or split shifts. This determines how check-ins are validated.
  • How will employees check in? ‚Äî Mobile app, biometric devices, or manual entry. This affects hardware needs and location verification.
  • What attendance violations matter? ‚Äî Late arrivals, early departures, absences. Define thresholds and whether they trigger deductions.
  • How do you handle overtime? ‚Äî Pay it, convert to time off, or ignore it. This requires configuring Overtime or Days in Lieu policies.

Initial Setup

Complete these configuration steps before employees begin tracking attendance. All settings are accessible via Settings ‚Üí Attendance.

1. Configure Work Timings

Define the schedules employees will follow. Work timings control check-in/out windows, break allowances, and overtime calculations.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Attendance
  2. Expand the Work Timings accordion
  3. Click Add new to create a schedule
  4. Enter schedule name and working hours (start/end times)
  5. Set late arrival threshold (minutes after start time before marked late)
  6. Set early departure threshold (minutes before end time considered early)
  7. Configure break duration (total minutes allowed per day)
  8. Choose whether to allow check-ins outside the office location
  9. Select extra hours calculation method (if overtime policies apply)
  10. Save the work timing

Planning tip: Create separate work timings for different employee groups (office staff, shift workers, remote employees) rather than trying to use one schedule for all.

2. Define Office Locations

Set up the physical locations where employees will check in. Each office requires geographical coordinates for location-based verification.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Attendance
  2. Expand the General accordion
  3. Add office locations with names and addresses
  4. Set the geo-fence radius (distance from office center where check-ins are allowed)
  5. Save each office configuration

Geo-fencing: The radius determines how far from the office center employees can be when checking in. Typical values: 50-200 meters depending on building size.

3. Set Up Work Centers (For Shift-Based Employees)

Work centers group employees who follow dynamic shift schedules. Skip this step if all employees follow fixed work timings.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Attendance
  2. Expand Work Centers for Shift Scheduling
  3. Click Add new to create a work center
  4. Name the work center (e.g., "Retail Floor Staff", "Security Team")
  5. Assign schedulers (users who can create shifts for this group)
  6. Link office locations where this work center operates
  7. Add employees to the work center
  8. Save the configuration

Constraint: Each employee can belong to only one work center. Plan your work center structure before assigning employees.

4. Configure Attendance Policies (Optional)

Set up overtime compensation, days in lieu, and deduction policies if you need to translate attendance into payroll impacts. These are optional ‚Äî you can track attendance without them.

Overtime Policies:

  1. Expand Overtime Policies accordion
  2. Click Add new
  3. Name the policy and select hourly rate type (Single or Custom)
  4. Configure rate multipliers and salary components
  5. Assign employees to the policy
  6. Save

For detailed overtime policy configuration, see the Overtime guide.

Days In Lieu Policies:

  1. Expand Days In Lieu Policies accordion
  2. Click Add new
  3. Set hours-to-day conversion ratio (default: 8 hours = 1 day)
  4. Assign employees
  5. Save

Deductions Policies for Payroll:

  1. Expand Deductions Policies for Payroll accordion
  2. Click Add new
  3. Configure separate formulas for absent days, early departures, and late arrivals
  4. Assign employees
  5. Save

5. Enable Image Capture (Optional)

Require employees to take a photo when checking in/out to verify identity. This prevents proxy attendance.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Attendance
  2. Expand the General accordion
  3. Toggle on "Enable image capture"
  4. Save

Note: Image capture works on mobile app check-ins and kiosk mode. It does not apply to biometric device check-ins.

6. Assign Employees to Work Settings

Link each employee to their office location, work week, and work timing. This step is required for attendance tracking to function.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to the employee's profile (People ‚Üí Employees ‚Üí select employee)
  2. Go to the Work tab
  3. Select the employee's assigned office from the dropdown
  4. Select the work week (defines working days vs. weekends)
  5. Select the work timing (the schedule created in step 1)
  6. If using shift scheduling, assign the employee to a work center
  7. Save changes

Required fields: Office, work week, and work timing must all be set before an employee can check in. Missing any of these will block attendance tracking for that employee.

7. Configure Biometric Devices (Optional)

If using physical biometric devices instead of mobile app check-ins, configure device integration.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Attendance
  2. Expand Biometric Devices accordion
  3. Add device details (device ID, location, API credentials)
  4. Test device connection
  5. Map device users to Bayzat employee records
  6. Save configuration

For biometric device setup details, see the Biometric Attendance guide.

8. Set Up Approval Flow for Attendance Regularization (Optional)

If you want employees to request attendance corrections via tickets, configure the approval routing.

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Tickets
  2. Select Attendance Regularization ticket type
  3. Define ticket name and description
  4. Choose whether to apply to all employees or custom selection
  5. Save ticket configuration
  6. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Approval Flow
  7. Select Attendance Regularization
  8. Assign approval roles (default: Super Admin)
  9. Configure advanced conditions if needed (e.g., route to Line Manager first, then HR)
  10. Save approval flow

For approval flow configuration details, see the Approval Flow guide.

Workflow Integration

Real-World Workflow Examples

Attendance supports workflow automation through shift events. When shifts are created, updated, or deleted, workflows can trigger automated actions to notify employees, generate reports, or update related systems.

Example: Shift Creation Notification

  • Trigger: Employee shift is created
  • Condition: IF shift is scheduled within the next 7 days
  • Action: Send email notification to employee with shift details
  • Result: Employees receive advance notice of their schedules without manual communication from managers

Example: Shift Update Alert

  • Trigger: Employee shift is updated
  • Condition: IF shift timing or location changes
  • Action: Send push notification to employee mobile app
  • Result: Employees are immediately informed of schedule changes, reducing confusion and no-shows

Example: Absence Tracking

  • Trigger: Employees are marked as absent for past day
  • Condition: IF absence count exceeds threshold
  • Action: Create notification for line manager
  • Result: Managers can proactively address attendance patterns without daily report reviews

Example: Attendance Report Generation

  • Trigger: Mark as absent button is clicked on attendance daily report
  • Condition: IF marking multiple employees
  • Action: Generate attendance report and send to HR
  • Result: HR receives consolidated absence data for payroll processing automatically

Approval Flow

How Attendance Requests Route

Attendance regularization requests follow a configurable approval chain. When employees submit tickets to correct their check-in or check-out times, the request routes through designated approvers based on the configured flow. By default, Super Admins serve as the first approval step, but organizations can customize routing based on line manager hierarchy, department structure, or specific conditions like request amount or employee level.

Types Requiring Approval

Attendance regularization is the primary ticket type requiring approval. This covers requests to modify check-in times, check-out times, or both for days when employees forgot to clock in/out or had technical issues with the attendance system. The approval flow can be configured as a simple single-step approval (e.g., Line Manager only) or a multi-step chain (e.g., Line Manager ‚Üí HR Manager ‚Üí Super Admin for exceptions).

Routing Examples

How different attendance requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Standard Regularization

Employee submits a ticket to correct a missed check-out time ‚Üí Routes to their Line Manager for single-step approval ‚Üí If approved, attendance record updates automatically

Example 2: Multi-Day Correction

Employee requests regularization for 3 consecutive days ‚Üí Routes to Line Manager first ‚Üí IF approved by Line Manager AND total hours exceed threshold, escalates to HR Manager for secondary approval

Example 3: Retroactive Request

Employee submits regularization for a date more than 7 days ago ‚Üí Bypasses Line Manager ‚Üí Routes directly to Super Admin for approval due to policy exception rule

Example 4: Conditional Routing

Employee regularizes attendance during a pay period that is already locked ‚Üí IF payroll status is Processed, routes to Finance Manager in addition to Line Manager ‚Üí Both must approve before attendance record can be modified

Approval Flow Configuration

Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Approval Flow ‚Üí Attendance Regularization to configure routing rules. You can define:

  • Default approval chain (applies to all requests unless conditions override)
  • Advanced conditions (IF statements based on employee attributes, request timing, or data values)
  • Approval step roles (Line Manager, HR Manager, Super Admin, or custom roles)
  • Escalation rules (automatic routing to next approver if no response within timeframe)

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

KSA Labor Law Compliance

Penalty Escalation (By Design): Penalties must vary based on occurrence count (e.g., 15 min late: 1st=warning, 2nd=5% daily wage). System enables customer decisions coherent with KSA Ministry of Human Resource and Social Development labor law rather than strict implementation.

Deduction calculations must be fair, consistent, and documented for audits. Automatic updates maintain accuracy when occurrences are rejected.

Attendance Editing Rules

Rule Constraint Impact
Deduction Editing Remarks are mandatory when editing amount. Once added to payroll, editing is locked ‚Äî only removal possible. Medium
Timestamp Validation Check-in time cannot be higher than check-out time. Dates must be in DD/MM/YYYY format. Time must be in 24-hour format. High
Concurrent Edits System lacks robust concurrent edit handling ‚Äî race conditions may occur when multiple users edit the same record simultaneously. Medium
Inactive Employees System allows mentioning inactive or off-boarded employees in comments. Medium

Shift Scheduling Constraints

Split Shift Limit: Maximum of 2 shifts per employee per day. Cannot schedule shifts on days off, during half-day leave, or add extra hours if multiple shifts exist.

Constraint Details
Copy/Paste Range Shifts can be copied up to 90 days in advance. Cannot paste into previous dates. Copy functionality disabled when 'all' work center is selected.
Deletion Window Cannot delete shifts for past dates or current day if employee has checked in. Undo option visible for 30 seconds after bulk deletion.
Publish Restrictions Publish shifts button only available under specific work center. Cannot publish for 'all' work centers.

Edge Cases

  • Multiple violations same day: Late arrival + early departure on same day ‚Äî system tracks each violation separately and applies deductions per policy level.
  • Retroactive policy changes: Policy edits only affect future attendance actions, not existing records. Historical violations remain under original policy rules.
  • Grace period handling: Minor lateness within grace period does not trigger violation. Check Work Timings configuration for grace period settings.
  • Public holidays overlap: If public holiday falls on weekend, system treats it as full extra hours for overtime calculation.
  • Cross-border employees: UAE vs KSA rules ‚Äî ensure correct labor law settings are applied in company configuration.
  • Employee transferred mid-period: If employee moves between departments with different policies, new policy applies from transfer date forward.
  • Zero extra hours request: Employees cannot request overtime below the minimum hours threshold set in Settings > Attendance > Overtime Policies.
  • Partial attendance days: Half-day leave with check-in allowed ‚Äî system prevents check-in during half-day leave periods by design.
  • Biometric check-in failure: If facial recognition retry limit is exceeded, system falls back to configured behavior (image capture or block check-in).
  • Split shift deductions: Deductions are halved for split shifts ‚Äî each shift treated independently for violation calculations.
  • Concurrent edits conflict: If two users edit same record simultaneously, last save wins. No conflict resolution ‚Äî coordinate edits to avoid data loss.
  • Inactive employee mentions: System allows tagging inactive employees in comments ‚Äî verify employee status before mentioning.
  • Offboarded employee records: Employees terminated before offboarding feature was introduced still appear in reports ‚Äî filter by employment status to exclude.

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I edit attendance records after they've been approved for payroll?

No. Once attendance records are approved for payroll, they cannot be modified through the Edit Attendance dialog. Any corrections must be made manually in the payroll system or via a new attendance regularization ticket for the next cycle.

Why can't I create a leave request from the Daily Attendance Report for some employees?

The Create leave request option in the three-dot menu is only available for employees who have attendance data for that day and are not currently on leave. If the employee has no check-in/out records, the option will be disabled.

What's the difference between Clear and Delete in the row action menu?

Clear resets all attendance data for that day (check-in, check-out, breaks, overtime) while keeping the employee row. Delete removes the entire attendance record. Both options are only active when the record has attendance data.

Why don't I see the Copy Schedule and Publish shifts buttons in Shift Scheduler?

These buttons require a specific work center to be selected. When viewing "All" work centers, the copy and publish functionality is disabled. Select a single work center from the filter to enable these features.

Can employees check in during half-day leave?

No. The system prevents check-in during approved half-day leave periods. Employees must request attendance regularization after the leave ends if they worked during that time.

How do I schedule split shifts for the same employee on one day?

Use the Shift Scheduler to create two separate shifts for the same employee on the same date. Ensure the shifts don't overlap and that no day off exists for that day. Maximum 2 shifts per employee per day.

Why do checkout details appear in the wrong order in the Edit Attendance dialog?

This is a known UI issue. Checkout entries may not display chronologically. Verify timestamps manually when reviewing or editing attendance records with multiple check-outs.

Can I filter the Daily Attendance Report to exclude specific employees?

No. The current filter options (Department, Location, Employment type) apply inclusively. To exclude specific employees, export the Custom Report to Excel and filter manually.

What happens when I approve an attendance regularization ticket?

The system automatically updates the employee's check-in and check-out times to match the ticket request. This action cannot be reversed ‚Äî any further changes require manual editing of the attendance record.

Why can't I see attendance reports for all employees as a Line Manager?

Line Managers can only view attendance reports for employees who directly report to them. To view all employees, you need Super Admin or Time Off Manager permissions.

How do I enable facial recognition for attendance check-in?

Navigate to Settings > Attendance > Biometric Data section. Enable image capture and configure retry limits and fallback behavior. Employees must have a primary passport photo uploaded in their documents.

Can I schedule shifts more than 90 days in advance?

No. The Shift Scheduler copy functionality is limited to 90 days in advance. To plan further ahead, save shifts as drafts and publish them closer to the date.

What's the difference between Overtime policies and Days in Lieu policies?

Overtime policies calculate monetary compensation for extra hours worked. Days in Lieu policies convert extra hours into time-off credits (default: 8 hours = 1 day). Both are configured in Settings > Attendance.

Why do I see 7 pending tickets in the attendance banner?

This indicates 7 attendance regularization tickets awaiting approval in the Pending tab. Navigate to Requests > Employee Tickets to review and approve or reject each ticket.

Can I bulk delete shifts across multiple employees and dates?

Yes. Use the Delete icon in Shift Scheduler, select work center, date range, employees, and shift status. Shifts can be deleted up to 6 months in advance. You have 30 seconds to undo.
