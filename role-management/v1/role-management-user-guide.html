Feature Overview

What Role Management Does

Role Management is Bayzat's centralized access control system that governs what employees can see and do within the platform. It operates on two primary entities: Role Assignments (mapping employees to roles) and Custom Roles (role definitions with permissions). The feature enables administrators to assign pre-built system roles like Super Admin, Line Manager, and People Manager, or create custom roles with precisely defined permissions using a policy-based model.

Who Uses Role Management

This feature is primarily used by:

    • Super Admins: Full control over all role assignments and custom role creation
    • People Managers: May have delegated permissions to assign roles within their scope
    • HR Administrators: Manage role assignments as part of employee onboarding and access governance

    Key Capabilities

    • Role Assignment: Assign one or multiple roles to any employee through an intuitive dialog interface
    • Custom Role Creation: Build organization-specific roles by selecting from 60+ available policies
    • Restrictable Roles: Configure roles with restriction groups to limit access by department, office, or custom groupings
    • Workflow Integration: Use roles as criteria filters and routing targets in automated workflows
    • Multilingual Support: Define custom role names and descriptions in both English and Arabic

Business Value

Role Management delivers security, efficiency, and compliance by ensuring employees have appropriate access aligned with their responsibilities. By supporting multiple concurrent roles per employee, organizations can reflect complex reporting structures and cross-functional responsibilities. The policy-based permission model provides granular control without requiring technical expertise, while workflow integration enables intelligent automation based on organizational structure. Restrictable roles add an additional security layer, ensuring users only access data relevant to their scope of work.

User Journey

Role Management in Bayzat involves two distinct but interconnected workflows: managing role assignments (mapping employees to roles) and defining custom roles with specific permissions. Understanding these workflows helps administrators efficiently control access and maintain security across the platform.

Primary Workflow: Managing Role Assignments

The role assignment workflow follows a clear progression from identifying access needs to granting and maintaining appropriate permissions for employees.

Stage 1: Identify Access Requirements

Administrators begin by determining which employees need platform access and what level of permissions they require. This involves:

    • Reviewing organizational structure and reporting lines
    • Understanding job responsibilities and required system access
    • Identifying employees who need administrative, managerial, or specialized permissions
    • Determining if employees need access to specific modules (Payroll, Attendance, Insurance, etc.)

    Decision Point: Should the employee receive a standard system role, a custom role, or multiple roles?

    Stage 2: Assign Roles to Employees

    Once access requirements are clear, administrators navigate to Settings → Role Management and use the "Assign roles" button to map employees to appropriate roles.

    The assignment process involves:

    1. Selecting the employee from a searchable dropdown
    2. Choosing one or more roles from the available list (40+ system and custom roles)
    3. Configuring restriction groups for restrictable roles (if applicable)
    4. Reviewing the selected roles and their associated permissions
    5. Saving the role assignment

    Expected Outcome: The employee gains immediate access to features and data permitted by their assigned role(s). The role assignment appears in the Role assignment table with the employee's name and assigned roles visible.

    Stage 3: Monitor and Adjust Role Assignments

    As organizational needs evolve, administrators regularly review and update role assignments:

    • Viewing the complete list of employees with role assignments
    • Identifying employees who may need additional or reduced permissions
    • Editing existing role assignments using the Edit button
    • Adding or removing roles as job responsibilities change
    • Removing all roles when employees leave or change positions

    Decision Point: Does the employee need role modifications due to promotion, department transfer, or changing responsibilities?

    Stage 4: Leverage Roles in Workflows and Operations

    Assigned roles become powerful tools for automation and routing throughout the platform:

    • Workflow Criteria: Filter workflow execution based on employee roles using "Employee → roles" with operations like "contains all," "contains any," or "is empty"
    • Email Recipients: Route workflow emails to all employees with specific roles (e.g., all People Managers)
    • Task Assignment: Automatically assign tasks to role holders
    • Notification Routing: Send notifications to employees based on their roles
    • Line Manager Routing: Use "Employee → direct reports to" for hierarchical routing

    Expected Outcome: Workflows automatically adapt to organizational structure, routing approvals, notifications, and tasks to the right people based on their roles.

    Secondary Workflow: Defining Custom Roles

    When standard system roles don't meet organizational needs, administrators create custom roles with tailored permission sets.

    Stage 1: Identify Permission Requirements

    Administrators analyze what specific permissions a new role requires:

    • Reviewing the 60+ available policies in the system
    • Understanding which features and data the role should access
    • Determining read-only vs. read-write permissions
    • Identifying approval or management capabilities needed

    Stage 2: Create Custom Role

    Using the Custom roles tab, administrators define new roles:

    1. Click "Add new" on the Custom roles tab
    2. Enter role name in English and Arabic
    3. Provide role description in both languages
    4. Select appropriate policies from the 60+ available options
    5. Save the custom role definition

    Expected Outcome: The new custom role appears in the Custom roles table with a "Custom" badge and becomes available for assignment to employees.

    Stage 3: Assign and Monitor Custom Roles

    Once created, custom roles function identically to system roles:

    • Appear in the role selection list when assigning roles to employees
    • Can be combined with other system or custom roles
    • Are available for workflow criteria and routing
    • Can be edited to adjust permissions as needs change
    • Can be deleted if no longer needed (after removing from all employees)

    Typical Use Case Scenarios

    Scenario 1: Onboarding a New HR Manager

    2. Clicks "Assign roles" and selects the new HR Manager from the employee list
    3. Assigns "People Manager" role for full employee data access

    5. Saves changes
    6. Employees' access permissions update immediately to reflect new responsibilities

Initial Setup

Setting up Role Management for the first time requires careful planning and configuration to ensure appropriate access control across your organization. Follow these steps to establish a secure and efficient role management system.

Step 1: Access Role Management

    • Log in to Bayzat with Super Admin credentials
    • Navigate to Settings from the main menu
    • Click on Role Management
    • You will see two tabs: Role assignment and Custom roles
Settings → Role Management

Step 2: Review Existing Role Assignments

Before making changes, assess the current state of role assignments:

  1. On the Role assignment tab, review the table showing all employees with assigned roles
  2. Note which employees already have roles (typically the account creator has Super Admin)
  3. Identify employees who need role assignments
  4. Check for any roles that may need adjustment

Step 3: Review Available System Roles

Familiarize yourself with the built-in roles before assigning them:

  1. When assigning roles (covered in Step 5), you'll see a searchable list of 40+ available roles
  2. Hover over the information icon next to each role to see specific permissions
  3. Note which roles are marked as Restrictable (these allow restriction groups)
  4. Identify roles relevant to your organization's structure and modules

Common System Roles

  • Super Admin: Full platform access and configuration capabilities
  • People Manager: Comprehensive employee data access and management
  • Line Manager: Access to direct reports' information and approvals
  • Payroll Manager: Payroll processing and configuration
  • Time Off Manager: Time-off policy management and approvals
  • Attendance Manager: Attendance tracking and reporting
  • Invoice Manager: Accounts payable invoice management
  • Invoice Approver: Invoice approval capabilities
  • Task Manager: Full task management across the organization
  • Knowledge Hub Manager: Knowledge base content management

Step 4: Create Custom Roles (If Needed)

If standard system roles don't meet your organization's needs, create custom roles before assigning them:

  1. Click on the Custom roles tab
  2. Click Add new button
  3. Enter the role name in English and Arabic
  4. Provide a clear description in both languages explaining the role's purpose
  5. Select appropriate policies from the 60+ available options:
    • Search for policies by name
    • Select multiple policies to combine permissions
    • Common policies include: Time off line manager policy, Invoice Approver policy, Invoice Manager policy, Payment Authorizer policy
  6. Click Save
Best Practice: Create custom roles with descriptive names that clearly indicate their purpose (e.g., "Finance Approver," "Department Manager," "Regional HR Lead"). This makes role assignment easier and more intuitive for future administrators.

Step 5: Assign Initial Roles to Key Personnel

Start by assigning roles to essential personnel who need immediate access:

Assign Roles to HR Team

  1. On the Role assignment tab, click Assign roles
  2. In the dialog, click the employee dropdown and search for the HR team member
  3. Select the employee
  4. The dialog advances to role selection (Step 1: Select roles & restrictions)
  5. Check the boxes for appropriate roles:
    • For HR Managers: Consider "People Manager" + "Payroll Manager" + "Time Off Manager"
    • For HR Coordinators: Consider "Line Manager" + "Attendance Manager"
  6. If assigning a restrictable role, configure restriction groups to limit access to specific employee segments
  7. Click Next to proceed to Step 2: Review & save
  8. Review the selected roles and their permissions
  9. Click Save

Assign Roles to Department Managers

  1. Repeat the assignment process for each department manager
  2. Typical role combination: "Line Manager" (for direct report access)
  3. Add department-specific roles as needed (e.g., "Invoice Approver" for Finance Manager)

Assign Roles to Finance Team

  1. For Accounts Payable staff, assign:
    • "Invoice Manager" - for creating and managing invoices
    • "Invoice Approver" - for approving invoices (if applicable)
    • "Invoice Payment Initiator" - for creating payments
    • "Invoice Payment Authorizer" - for authorizing payments (if applicable)
  2. Consider creating a custom "Finance Approver" role combining multiple finance-related policies

Step 6: Configure Recommended Default Settings

Role Assignment Strategy

  • Principle of Least Privilege: Assign only the roles necessary for each employee's job function
  • Multiple Roles: Combine roles when employees have multiple responsibilities (e.g., "Line Manager" + "Time Off Manager")
  • Restrictable Roles: Use restriction groups to limit access to specific departments, offices, or employee segments
  • Regular Review: Schedule quarterly reviews of role assignments to ensure they remain appropriate

Custom Role Guidelines

  • Create custom roles for recurring permission patterns in your organization
  • Use clear, descriptive names that indicate the role's purpose
  • Document the intended use of each custom role in the description field
  • Limit the number of custom roles to avoid confusion (aim for 5-10 maximum)

Step 7: Verify Role Assignments

After initial setup, verify that roles are working correctly:

  1. Return to the Role assignment tab
  2. Review the table to confirm all key personnel have appropriate roles
  3. Click Edit on a few assignments to verify role details
  4. Ask newly assigned users to log in and confirm they can access expected features
  5. Test workflow routing if using roles in workflows

Step 8: Document Your Role Strategy

Create internal documentation for your role management approach:

  • List all custom roles and their purposes
  • Document which job titles should receive which roles
  • Create a process for requesting role changes
  • Establish a review schedule for role assignments
  • Identify who can assign roles (typically HR managers with Super Admin or People Manager roles)

Step 9: Train Additional Administrators

If multiple people will manage roles:

  1. Assign "People Manager" or "Super Admin" role to additional administrators
  2. Walk them through the role assignment process
  3. Review the custom roles you've created and their purposes
  4. Share your role assignment documentation
  5. Demonstrate how roles integrate with workflows (if applicable)
