Feature Overview

What is Employee Tickets?

Employee Tickets is a comprehensive request management system within the Bayzat HR platform that enables employees to submit various types of requests and allows administrators to review, approve, and process them through structured workflows. The feature is currently in Beta and supports multiple ticket categories including Payroll, Business Travel, Attendance, and custom categories.

Who Uses This Feature?


    • Employees: Submit ticket requests for leave encashment, travel tickets, attendance regularization, salary discrepancies, work from home requests, and other custom ticket types

    • Line Managers: Review and approve tickets for their direct reports as part of the approval workflow

    • HR Administrators: Manage ticket configurations, approval workflows, and process approved tickets

    • Payroll Administrators: Process tickets that require payroll adjustments and mark them as processed

    • Business Travel Approvers: Review and approve travel ticket requests based on company policies

    • Transaction Processing Admins: Handle financial aspects of approved tickets requiring monetary adjustments

    Key Capabilities


    • Multi-Category Support: Handle diverse ticket types including Leave Encashment, Travel Tickets (Grade A & B), Attendance Regularization, Salary Discrepancy Requests, Work From Home Requests, and custom ticket types

    • Flexible Approval Workflows: Configure multi-level approval chains with role-based routing and automatic escalation

    • Payroll Integration: Seamlessly add approved tickets to payroll cycles with automatic calculation of financial impacts

    • Comprehensive Tracking: Monitor ticket status across Pending, Approved, and Rejected states with detailed activity timelines

    • Bulk Operations: Approve or reject multiple tickets simultaneously for efficient processing

    • Advanced Filtering: Search and filter tickets by ID, employee name, category, status, priority, and date ranges

    • Reporting & Analytics: Generate detailed reports and export ticket data for compliance and audit purposes

    • Document Management: Attach supporting documents to tickets with mandatory attachment requirements for specific ticket types

    Business Value

    Employee Tickets transforms ad-hoc request management into a structured, auditable process that:


    • Reduces Processing Time: Automated workflows eliminate manual routing and reduce approval cycle times by up to 60%

    • Improves Compliance: Complete audit trails and enforced approval hierarchies ensure policy adherence and regulatory compliance

    • Enhances Visibility: Real-time dashboards and status tracking provide transparency for both employees and administrators

    • Streamlines Payroll: Direct integration with payroll processing eliminates duplicate data entry and reduces errors

    • Supports Customization: Flexible ticket type configuration allows organizations to adapt the system to their specific needs

    • Enables Data-Driven Decisions: Comprehensive reporting provides insights into request patterns, approval bottlenecks, and resource allocation

Accessing Employee Tickets

How to Access Employee Tickets

Employee Tickets is a Beta feature marked with a New badge in the navigation menu. There are multiple ways to access and work with employee tickets in Bayzat.

Primary Navigation Path


    • From the main dashboard, click on Requests in the left sidebar

    • In the expanded menu, select Employee Tickets

    • You will land on the Employee Tickets page with the Pending tab active by default

Alternative Access Points

From Employee Profile


You can access tickets related to a specific employee:



  • Navigate to People > Employees

  • Select an employee from the list

  • Go to the employee's profile

  • Access the Tickets section to view or create tickets for that employee

Quick Actions


The Create employee ticket button is available in the top-right corner of the Employee Tickets page, allowing you to quickly initiate a new ticket without navigating away.

Configuration Access

To configure ticket types, approval workflows, and related settings:


  1. Click Settings in the left sidebar

  2. Navigate to Company section

  3. Select Employee Structures to configure employee groupings for ticket routing


Note: The Employee Tickets feature is currently in Beta. You'll see a "Beta feature" indicator throughout the interface. Some advanced configuration options may require assistance from Bayzat support.

Core Tasks and Procedures

Core Tasks and Procedures

This section covers the primary workflows for managing employee tickets, organized by functional area.

1. Ticket Management and Review

1.1 Viewing and Filtering Tickets

The Employee Tickets page organizes tickets into three main tabs:


    • Pending - Tickets awaiting review and approval (default view)

    • Approved - Tickets that have been approved

    • Rejected - Tickets that have been rejected

    To filter tickets:


    1. Click the Filters button in the top toolbar

    2. The filters panel opens on the right side

    3. Apply filters by:

      • Ticket Type - Select specific ticket categories (Leave Encashment, Travel Ticket, Attendance Regularization, etc.)

      • Employee - Search and filter by employee name or ID

      • Date Range - Filter by ticket creation or submission date

      • Status - Additional status filters within each tab



    4. Click Apply to update the ticket list

    To search tickets:



    • Use the search bar at the top to search by Ticket ID, Employee ID, or Employee Name

    • Results update in real-time as you type

    To customize columns:



    • Click the Columns button to show/hide specific data columns

    • Available columns include: Ticket ID, Employee Name, Ticket Type, Submitted Date, Priority, Status, Approvers

    1.2 Reviewing Ticket Details

    To view complete ticket information:


    1. Click on any ticket row in the table

    2. The ticket detail modal opens, displaying:

      • Ticket Information - ID, Category, Status, Priority

      • Employee Details - Name, ID, Department, Line Manager

      • Ticket Content - Specific details based on ticket type (e.g., Leave Encashment calculation breakdown)

      • Activities Timeline - Complete history of ticket actions, status changes, and comments




    Ticket Type Examples:

    • Leave Encashment - Shows formula, daily pay calculation, allowance breakdown, and number of days

    • Travel Ticket Requests - Displays grade, destination, and policy details

    • Attendance Regularization - Shows date, time, and reason for adjustment

    • Salary Discrepancy - Details the discrepancy and requested correction


    1.3 Approving or Rejecting Tickets

    Single Ticket Approval:


    1. From the Pending tab, locate the ticket to approve/reject

    2. Option A - Quick Action:

      • Click Approve or Reject button directly in the ticket row



    3. Option B - From Detail View:

      • Click the ticket row to open the detail modal

      • Click Approve or Reject at the top of the modal

      • Add comments explaining your decision (optional but recommended)



    4. Confirm your action

    5. The ticket moves to the appropriate tab (Approved or Rejected)

    6. The employee and relevant stakeholders receive notifications

    Bulk Approval/Rejection:


    1. In the Pending tab, select multiple tickets using the checkboxes

    2. A bulk action bar appears at the top showing the number of selected tickets

    3. Click Approve Selected or Reject Selected

    4. Add bulk comments if needed

    5. Confirm the bulk action


    Approval Workflow: Tickets may require approval from multiple approvers based on the configured workflow. Role badges indicate approver types:

    • P - Payroll table admin

    • T - Transaction processing admin

    • S - Super Admin

    • B - Business Travel Approver


    1.4 Adding Comments to Tickets


    1. Open the ticket detail modal

    2. Navigate to the Activities section

    3. In the comment box at the bottom, type your comment

    4. Click Add Comment

    5. Comments are visible to all approvers and the ticket creator

    2. Creating and Submitting Tickets

    2.1 Creating a New Employee Ticket


    1. From the Employee Tickets page, click Create employee ticket in the top-right corner

    2. The ticket creation form opens

    3. Select the Employee for whom you're creating the ticket

    4. Choose the Ticket Type from the dropdown:

      • Leave Encashment

      • Travel Ticket Request (Grade A/B)

      • Attendance Regularization

      • Salary Discrepancy Request

      • Work From Home Request

      • Custom ticket types (if configured)



    5. Fill in the required fields based on the ticket type

    6. Set Priority (if you have admin permissions)

    7. Add any supporting documents or attachments

    8. Add a description or notes

    9. Click Submit to create the ticket


    Important: Some ticket types may have mandatory attachment requirements (e.g., "Testing Mandatory Attachment" ticket type). Ensure all required documents are uploaded before submission.

    2.2 Ticket Type-Specific Fields

    Leave Encashment Tickets:



    • Leave type to encash

    • Number of days to encash

    • Reason for encashment

    • System automatically calculates the amount based on configured formula

    Travel Ticket Requests:



    • Employee grade (determines ticket class and allowance)

    • Destination

    • Travel dates

    • Number of tickets (employee + dependents)

    Attendance Regularization:



    • Date of attendance to regularize

    • Actual time in/out

    • Reason for discrepancy

    • Supporting documentation

    3. Payroll Processing for Approved Tickets

    3.1 Viewing Payroll-Impacting Tickets


    1. Navigate to the Approved tab

    2. Look for tickets with Processed status badge

    3. These tickets have been included in payroll calculations

    3.2 Processing Tickets in Payroll

    For tickets that impact payroll (e.g., Leave Encashment, Salary Adjustments):


    1. Approved tickets automatically appear in the payroll processing queue

    2. Navigate to Finance > Payroll

    3. In the current payroll period, ticket-related adjustments appear as line items

    4. Review the adjustments:

      • Leave Encashment amounts

      • Salary corrections

      • Other ticket-driven changes



    5. Process payroll as usual - ticket amounts are automatically included

    6. After payroll is finalized, tickets are marked as Processed


    Payroll Integration: The system automatically creates payroll transactions for approved tickets that have financial impact. You can view these in the employee's payroll adjustments section.

    4. Reporting and Export

    4.1 Downloading Ticket Reports


    1. From the Employee Tickets page, click View reports

    2. The reports modal opens

    3. Select report parameters:

      • Date range

      • Ticket types

      • Status (Pending/Approved/Rejected)

      • Specific employees or departments



    4. Click Download to generate the report

    5. Reports are exported in Excel format (.xlsx)

    4.2 Exporting Ticket Data


    1. Click the Download button in the top toolbar

    2. Choose export format:

      • Current View - Exports tickets visible in the current tab with applied filters

      • All Tickets - Exports complete ticket data across all tabs

      • Selected Tickets - Exports only tickets you've selected via checkboxes



    3. The export file downloads automatically

    Export includes:



    • Ticket ID and type

    • Employee details

    • Submission and processing dates

    • Status and priority

    • Approver information

    • Financial amounts (where applicable)

    • Comments and activity history

    5. Configuration and Setup Tasks

    5.1 Configuring Employee Structures

    Employee Structures determine how tickets are routed and which approvers see which tickets.


    1. Navigate to Settings > Company > Employee Structures

    2. Click Add New to create a new structure

    3. Enter structure details:

      • Structure Name - Descriptive name (e.g., "Sales Department", "Dubai Office")

      • Description - Purpose and scope of this structure

      • Grouping Type:

        • Manual - Manually select employees to include

        • Automatic - Define rules to automatically group employees (e.g., by department, location, job title)





    4. Assign employees to the structure (for manual grouping)

    5. Configure approval roles and permissions for this structure

    6. Click Save

    5.2 Managing Structure Assignments

    From the Employee Structures page, you can:


    • View employees and roles - Click the view icon to see all employees and their assigned roles within a structure

    • View groups - See the grouping configuration and rules

    • Edit - Modify structure settings, employee assignments, or grouping rules

    • Delete - Remove a structure (only if no active tickets are associated with it)


    Configuration Limitations: Some advanced role and policy configurations require backend support. If you need to create custom approval roles or complex policy mappings, contact Bayzat support for assistance.

    6. Common Workflows

    6.1 Processing a Leave Encashment Request


    1. Employee submits a Leave Encashment ticket through their portal

    2. Ticket appears in your Pending tab

    3. Review the ticket details:

      • Verify the leave balance is sufficient

      • Check the calculation breakdown (formula, daily pay, allowances)

      • Review company policy compliance



    4. If approved:

      • Click Approve

      • Ticket moves to Approved tab

      • Amount is automatically added to next payroll cycle

      • After payroll processing, ticket is marked Processed



    5. If rejected:

      • Click Reject

      • Add a comment explaining the reason

      • Ticket moves to Rejected tab

      • Employee is notified of the rejection



    6.2 Handling Multi-Level Approvals

    Some tickets require approval from multiple roles:


    1. Ticket is submitted and routed to the first approver level

    2. First approver reviews and approves

    3. Ticket automatically moves to the next approval level

    4. Subsequent approvers receive notifications

    5. Process continues until all required approvals are obtained

    6. Final approval moves the ticket to Approved status

    Tracking approval progress:



    • Open the ticket detail modal

    • View the Approvers section to see:

      • Who has approved (with timestamps)

      • Who still needs to approve

      • Approver roles and sequence



    6.3 Reverting Approved Tickets

    If an approved ticket needs to be reconsidered:


    1. Navigate to the Approved tab

    2. Locate the ticket to revert

    3. Open the ticket detail modal

    4. Click Revert to Pending (if available)

    5. Add a comment explaining why the ticket is being reverted

    6. Ticket returns to Pending tab for re-review


Payroll Considerations: If a ticket has already been processed in payroll (marked as "Processed"), reverting it will require manual payroll adjustments. Contact your payroll administrator before reverting processed tickets.
