What is Employee Tickets?

Overview

Employee Tickets is a comprehensive request management system within the Bayzat HR platform that enables employees to submit various types of requests and allows administrators to review, approve, and process them through structured workflows. The feature is currently in Beta and supports multiple ticket categories including Payroll, Business Travel, Attendance, and custom categories.

Key Benefits

  • Centralized management of all employee requests including leave encashment, travel tickets, and attendance regularization
  • Automated approval workflows with role-based routing and multi-level authorization
  • Seamless payroll integration for approved tickets requiring financial adjustments
  • Real-time tracking and reporting with comprehensive audit trails
  • Bulk operations for efficient processing of multiple tickets

Who Uses This Feature?

  • Employees: Submit ticket requests for leave encashment, travel tickets, attendance regularization, and more
  • Line Managers: Review and approve tickets for their direct reports
  • HR Administrators: Manage ticket configurations, approval workflows, and process approved tickets
  • Payroll Administrators: Process tickets that require payroll adjustments
  • Business Travel Approvers: Review and approve travel ticket requests

Creating a New Ticket (Employee Flow)

Overview

Employees submit tickets through the My Tickets section in their profile. The process involves selecting a category, choosing a ticket type, and filling in the required fields.

Step-by-Step Guide

1

Access My Tickets

Click on your profile icon in the top navigation bar and select My tickets from the dropdown menu. Alternatively, navigate to Profile → My Tickets.

2

Initiate Ticket Creation

Click the "+ Create ticket" button (blue button, top-right corner). A dialog will appear with two panels:

  • Left panel: List of available categories (e.g., Time & Attendance, HR Requests)
  • Right panel: Ticket types within the selected category
3

Select Category and Ticket Type

Browse available categories in the left panel, click on a category to see its ticket types, then click on the desired ticket type card. Each card shows the ticket type name and description.

4

Complete the Ticket Form

Fill in all required fields (marked with *). The form displays standard fields (Subject, Description, Priority) plus custom fields specific to the ticket type.

Custom Field Types

Field Type Description Example
Text Single or multi-line text input "Reason for request"
Date Date picker control "Requested date"
Time Time selector "Start time", "End time"
Number Numeric input "Number of days"
List Dropdown selection "Leave type"
Checkbox Yes/No toggle "Urgent request"
Money Currency input "Reimbursement amount"
5

Submit the Ticket

Review your entries and click the "Submit" button. Upon successful submission, you'll receive a confirmation, a ticket ID (format: T-XXX), and the ticket will appear in your "My Tickets" list with "Pending" status.

Success Indicators: Ticket ID assigned (e.g., T-63), confirmation message displayed, ticket appears in your list with "Pending" status badge.

What Can Go Wrong

Issue Cause Solution
"Create ticket" button not visible No ticket types assigned to you Contact HR to verify access permissions
Category shows no ticket types Empty category or permission issue Select a different category or contact HR
Form validation error Required field missing Complete all fields marked with *
Submission failed Network connectivity Check internet connection and retry

Activities and Comments

Overview

The Activities and comments section provides a complete audit trail of all actions taken on a ticket. This collapsible section appears in both the side panel and full page ticket views.

Activity Timeline

The timeline displays all ticket events in chronological order (newest at top), providing full transparency on ticket progress:

Activity Type Description Example
created employee ticket Ticket was first submitted "Tejasvi Suvarna created employee ticket"
edited employee ticket Ticket details were modified "Zareen Pathan edited employee ticket"
accepted employee ticket Ticket was approved "Zareen Pathan accepted employee ticket"
rejected employee ticket Ticket was rejected "Zareen Pathan rejected employee ticket"
moved employee ticket to pending Status changed to pending "Zareen Pathan moved employee ticket to pending"

Activity Entry Components

Each activity entry shows:

  • User avatar: Profile picture of the person who performed the action
  • User name: Full name displayed in bold (e.g., "Zareen Pathan")
  • Action description: What action was taken
  • Timestamp: Date when the action occurred (e.g., "28 Oct 2025")

Adding Comments

At the bottom of the Activities and comments section:

1

Write a Comment

Locate the "Write a comment..." input field at the bottom of the section. Click on the field to activate it.

2

Submit Your Comment

Type your comment and press Enter or click Send to post. Your comment will appear in the activity timeline.

Use Cases for Comments: Ask clarifying questions about a request, provide additional information, explain approval/rejection decisions, request document uploads, or communicate directly with the ticket submitter.

UI Elements

Business Rules & Limitations

Core Business Rules

Rule Description Impact

Payroll integration Approved tickets with financial impact automatically added to payroll Amounts calculated based on configured formulas

System Constraints

  • Custom role and policy configuration requires backend support
  • No self-service UI for granular approval flow configuration
  • Pending ticket counts not displayed dynamically in dashboard widgets
  • Processed tickets cannot be easily reverted without manual payroll adjustments

Configuration Limitations: Advanced role and policy configurations require assistance from Bayzat support. Use predefined system roles where possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I submit a leave encashment request?

Navigate to Requests → Employee Tickets → Click "Create employee ticket" → Select Leave Encashment as the ticket type → Fill in the required details and submit.

Why is my ticket still pending?

Tickets may require multiple approvals. Open the ticket details to view the approval chain and see which approver is next in the sequence.

How do I know when my ticket is processed?

You'll receive a notification when your ticket status changes. Approved tickets show "Processed" badge once included in payroll.

Can I edit a submitted ticket?

Once submitted, tickets cannot be edited. If changes are needed, contact your manager or HR to reject the ticket so you can submit a new one.
