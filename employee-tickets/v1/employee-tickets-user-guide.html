1. What is Employee Tickets?

Employee Tickets is a request management system that allows employees to submit formal requests to HR, managers, and administrators. Instead of sending emails or filling paper forms, employees can raise structured tickets that follow defined approval workflows.

Why Use Employee Tickets?

Consider these common workplace scenarios:

  • You forgot to clock in - Raise an Attendance Regularization ticket to correct your attendance record
  • You want to encash unused leave - Raise a Leave Encashment ticket to convert leave days to cash
  • You need a travel ticket - Raise a Business Travel ticket for flight bookings
  • You notice a salary discrepancy - Raise a Payroll ticket to report and resolve the issue

Key Benefit: Every ticket creates an audit trail - you can see exactly when it was submitted, who reviewed it, what decision was made, and when. No more "lost" requests or unclear status.

Who Uses This Feature?

Role What They Do
Employees Raise tickets for various requests (leave encashment, attendance correction, travel, etc.)
Line Managers Review and approve/reject tickets from their team members
HR Administrators Configure ticket types, manage workflows, and process approved tickets
Payroll Team Process tickets that affect salary (leave encashment, adjustments)

This section explains how employees submit new tickets. Whether you need to correct attendance, request leave encashment, or any other HR-related request, the process follows these steps.

Step-by-Step Guide

1

Access My Tickets

Click on your profile icon in the top-right corner of the screen, then select "My tickets" from the dropdown menu. This is where all your submitted tickets are listed.

2

Start Creating a Ticket

Click the blue "+ Create ticket" button in the top-right corner. A dialog will appear with two panels showing available categories and ticket types.

3

Select Category and Ticket Type

The selection dialog shows:

  • Left panel: Categories (e.g., "Time & Attendance", "Payroll", "Business Travel")
  • Right panel: Available ticket types within the selected category

Click on a category first, then click on the specific ticket type you need.

4

Fill in the Ticket Form

After selecting a ticket type, a form appears with fields specific to that request type. Fill in all required fields (marked with *).

Understanding Field Types

Different ticket types have different fields. Here are the common field types you'll encounter:

Field Type What It Looks Like Example Use
Text Text box for typing Reason for request, comments
Date Calendar picker Date of incident, travel date
Time Time selector Check-in time, check-out time
List/Dropdown Selection menu Leave type, request category
Number Numeric input Number of days, amount
Money Currency input Reimbursement amount, ticket value
5

Review and Submit

Before submitting, review all the information you've entered. Once you're satisfied, click the "Submit" button.

Success! After submitting, you'll see:
- A confirmation message
- A ticket ID (e.g., T-63) assigned to your request
- The ticket appears in your "My Tickets" list with "Pending" status

What Happens Next?

After you submit a ticket:

  1. Your ticket enters the approval workflow
  2. The designated approver(s) receive a notification
  3. They review your request and either approve or reject it
  4. You receive a notification when the status changes
  5. If approved, the request is processed (e.g., added to payroll)

4. Tracking Your Tickets

Once you've raised a ticket, you'll want to know its status and communicate with approvers if needed. This section explains how to monitor your tickets.

Understanding Ticket Statuses

Every ticket goes through a lifecycle with these possible statuses:

Status What It Means What You Can Do
Pending Waiting for approval Wait for approver review; add comments if needed
Approved Request has been approved Wait for processing (e.g., payroll inclusion)
Rejected Request was declined Check rejection reason; submit new ticket if needed
Processed Request completed (e.g., added to payroll) No action needed - request is complete

Viewing Ticket Details

Click on any ticket in your list to open the detail panel. Here you'll see:

  • Ticket ID: Unique identifier (e.g., T-63)
  • Status badge: Current status with color coding
  • Submitted information: All the details you entered
  • Activities and Comments: Complete history of what happened

Activities and Comments

The "Activities and comments" section at the bottom of the ticket detail is crucial for tracking what's happening:

Activity Types You'll See

Activity What It Means
"[Name] created employee ticket" You submitted the ticket
"[Name] edited employee ticket" Someone modified the ticket details
"[Name] accepted employee ticket" An approver approved your request
"[Name] rejected employee ticket" An approver rejected your request
"[Name] moved employee ticket to pending" Status was changed back to pending

Adding Comments

You can communicate with approvers by adding comments:

  1. Open the ticket detail panel
  2. Scroll to the "Activities and comments" section
  3. Click the "Write a comment..." field
  4. Type your message and press Enter to send

When to Use Comments:
- Provide additional context for your request
- Respond to questions from approvers
- Ask about the status of your ticket
- Upload supporting documents (if requested)

Opening Full Page View

For a more detailed view, click the external link icon (arrow pointing outward) next to the ticket ID. This opens the ticket in a full page with more space to review details.

How to Approve or Reject a Ticket

1

Open the Ticket

Click on a ticket row to open the detail panel. Review all the submitted information carefully.

2

Make Your Decision

Click either "Approve" or "Reject". If rejecting, you should add a comment explaining why.

3

Bulk Actions (Optional)

To process multiple tickets at once:
1. Select tickets using the checkboxes on the left
2. Use the bulk action bar that appears at the bottom
3. Click "Approve" or "Reject" to process all selected tickets

Understanding Approver Roles

Different ticket types may require approval from specific roles. Look for these badges:

  • P - Payroll Approver
  • T - Transaction Approver
  • S - Super Admin
  • B - Business Travel Approver

Multi-Level Approval: Some tickets require approval from multiple people. If you approve a ticket that needs another approval, it will move to the next approver in the chain rather than being fully approved.

Generating Reports

Click the "View reports" button to download ticket data in Excel format. This is useful for:

  • Tracking approval patterns
  • Auditing ticket processing
  • Analyzing request volumes by type

7. Troubleshooting & FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I submit a leave encashment request?

Go to Profile → My Tickets → Click "+ Create ticket" → Select "Payroll" category → Choose "Leave Encashment" → Fill in the details and submit.

Why is my ticket still pending?

Tickets may require multiple approvals. Open your ticket details and check the activity section to see where it is in the approval chain. You can also add a comment to ask about the status.

Can I edit a submitted ticket?

No, once submitted, tickets cannot be edited. If you made a mistake, ask your manager or HR to reject the ticket, then submit a new one with correct information.

How do I know when my ticket is processed?

You'll receive a notification when the status changes. Approved tickets show a "Processed" badge once they've been included in payroll or completed.
