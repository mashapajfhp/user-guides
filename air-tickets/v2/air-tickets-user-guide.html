What is Air Tickets?

Overview

Air Tickets manages employee air ticket allowances through configurable policies supporting three redemption types: reimbursement for purchased tickets, booking requests for company-arranged travel, and encashment of benefit value. Access via Payroll > Air tickets or Requests > Air tickets.

Admins create requests on behalf of employees, review submissions across four status tabs (Pending/Approved/Processed/Rejected), and route to approval flows. Approved reimbursements and encashments add to payroll automatically.

Key Benefits

  • Eliminate manual tracking of employee air ticket entitlements and renewal cycles
  • Support three redemption methods (reimbursement, booking, encashment) in a single policy
  • Automate encashment generation at configured intervals via workflows
  • Route requests through approval flows with criteria-based routing
  • Integrate approved amounts directly into payroll processing
  • Track allowance balances and remaining requests per employee

Strategic Context

Product Foundation Overview

How Air Tickets Fits

Air Tickets is a transactional request system built on top of policy templates. Admins create reusable policies that define allowances, coverage, and redemption options, then assign employees to those policies.

Mental model: Air Ticket Policy (template) → Assigned to Employees → Employee submits request (Reimbursement/Booking/Encashment) → Routed through approval flow → Processed to Payroll or HR

One policy can serve many employees, and each employee can submit multiple requests per cycle based on policy limits.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating Air Ticket policies:

  • What redemption options will you offer? — Reimbursement for purchased tickets, company booking, encashment, or a combination
  • How will allowances vary? — By seniority, home country, grade level, or flat rate for all employees
  • What is the renewal cycle? — Annual from hire date, probation end date, or fixed calendar date (e.g., January 1)
  • Who is covered? — Employee only, or employee plus dependents (spouse, children, parents)
  • Will encashment be automatic or manual? — Auto-encashment directly to payroll, or employee-initiated requests requiring approval

Related Features

These Bayzat features work together with Air Tickets. Where available, links point to the published user guide.

  • Payroll Management — Processes approved air ticket encashments and reimbursements as employee adjustments in payroll cycles
  • Leave Encashment — Similar benefit encashment pattern with policy-based allowances and payroll integration

Initial Setup & Configuration

Configuration Walkthrough

Air Tickets requires policy configuration before employees can submit requests.

Settings → Company → Air Ticket Policies

The policies page displays all configured air ticket policies with key details:

  • Policy name and employee count
  • Redeem options enabled (Reimbursement, Booking, Encashment)
  • Active/Inactive toggle for each policy
  • Employee assignment status (Manual assignment with unassigned count)

Policy Setup

Create an air ticket policy to define allowances and redemption options for employees.

Step 1: Access Policy Creation

  1. Navigate to Settings → Company → Air Ticket Policies
  2. Click Add New policy button

Step 2: Configure Basic Setup

  • Policy Name: Descriptive name for the policy
  • Allowance Limit: Currency and amount per employee (varies by seniority or country)
  • Coverage: Employee only, or include spouse/children/parents

Note: Allowance amount applies collectively to all listed dependents, not individually.

Step 3: Define Policy Behavior

  • Request Timing: When employees can submit requests (e.g., annually from hire date)
  • Renewal Period: Interval in months for allowance renewal
  • Eligibility Date: Hiring date or probation end date
  • Maximum Requests: Number of requests allowed per renewal cycle

Step 4: Choose Redeem Options

Select which redemption methods employees can use:

  • Reimbursement: Employees submit receipts for already-purchased tickets
  • Air Ticket Booking: HR/Admin books tickets on behalf of employees
  • Encashment: Employees receive cash value instead of tickets
  • Auto Encashment: System automatically creates encashment requests at renewal intervals

⚠️ Auto Encashment Configuration: When enabling auto encashment, choose whether requests are directly added to payroll or require approval first. Eligibility requires hire date or probation end date to be set in employee profiles.

Step 5: Review Configuration

The policy detail summary displays all configured settings across three sections:

  • Basic Setup: Name, allowance, coverage
  • Policy Behaviour: Timing, renewal, request limits, redeem options
  • Employees Included: Assigned employee count

Assignment & Activation

Assign Employees to Policy

  1. From the policy list, click the three-dot menu on a policy row
  2. Select View or Edit
  3. Navigate to employee assignment section
  4. Add employees manually or use auto-grouping criteria

The assignment dialog provides:

  • Search by employee name or ID
  • Filters by department, office, or reports-to hierarchy
  • Bulk selection options

Constraint: Employees can only be assigned to one air ticket policy at a time.

Activate the Policy

  1. Toggle the Active/Inactive switch on the policy row
  2. Active policies allow employees to submit requests
  3. Inactive policies prevent new requests but preserve existing data

⚠️ Policy Editing Restrictions: Active policies cannot be edited once employees have raised ticket requests. Changes to policy details will reject or delete existing pending requests.

What Happens After Activation

  • Assigned employees see the policy in their My Air Tickets view
  • Policy status card displays remaining requests and balance
  • Request Air Ticket button becomes enabled (if requests remain)
  • For auto encashment policies, system monitors eligibility and creates requests automatically

Approval Flow

How Air Tickets Requests Route

All air ticket requests (Reimbursement, Booking, Encashment) require approval before processing. Default routing sends requests to Payroll table admin. Advanced flows route high-value requests to Line Manager based on policy allowance amount and currency criteria.

Default Approval Routing

Standard air ticket requests route to Payroll table admin in a single approval step. This applies when no advanced flow criteria match the request.

Advanced Approval Routing

Two advanced flows route high-value requests to Line Manager before Payroll admin:

  • Amount exceeding AED 3000: Policy Allowance Amount > 3000 AND Currency = AED → Line Manager (1 level)
  • Amount exceeding AED 4000: Similar amount-based criteria → Line Manager routing

Routing Examples

How different air ticket requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Standard Reimbursement (AED 2500)

Employee submits reimbursement request for AED 2500 → Routes to Payroll table admin → Admin approves → Request moves to Approved tab

Example 2: High-Value Booking (AED 3500)

Employee submits booking request for AED 3500 → Advanced flow matches (amount > 3000 AND currency = AED) → Routes to Line Manager first → Manager approves → Routes to Payroll table admin → Admin approves → Request moves to Approved tab

Example 3: Encashment Request (AED 4200)

Employee submits encashment request for AED 4200 → Advanced flow matches (amount > 4000) → Routes to Line Manager → Manager approves → Routes to Payroll admin → Admin processes encashment → Amount added to next payroll cycle

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

Policy Configuration Rules

Air ticket policies have specific configuration requirements and constraints:

  • Eligibility timing: First request can be configured to trigger after a set number of months from hire date or probation end date
  • Renewal cycles: Policies renew based on configured intervals (annual, milestone-based, or custom periods)
  • Coverage options: Employee coverage is mandatory (always 1), spouse/child/parent coverage is optional
  • Request limits: Number of requests per cycle is configurable at policy level
  • Redemption methods: Policies support three types: Reimbursement (already purchased), Booking (company arranges), or Encashment (cash benefit)

Auto-Encashment Constraints

For policies configured with automatic encashment:

  • Auto-encashment requests cannot be moved back to pending status (by design)
  • Requests show "Created on" instead of "Submitted on" to distinguish automated requests
  • No requested amount row appears (amount is pre-determined by policy)
  • No approvers shown (auto-approved or direct to payroll based on policy setting)
  • Employee hire date or probation end date must be set for eligibility calculation to work
  • Policy must be active for auto-encashment to trigger

Critical Limitations

⚠️ Policy Configuration Complexity (By Design)

Setting up air ticket policies requires careful configuration of eligibility rules, renewal cycles, and redemption methods. Each policy must be individually configured with interdependent fields: Allowance Limit, Eligibility (Employee + dependents), first request timing (months from hire), renewal cycle, requests per cycle, and redeem methods. Misconfiguration can lead to incorrect allowance calculations.

⚠️ No Bulk Policy Assignment (By Design)

Employees must be assigned to air ticket policies manually. There is no bulk import or automatic assignment based on employee attributes. The policy employees dialog has a search bar and filters but requires individual assignment.

⚠️ No Air Ticket Booking Integration (By Design)

Air ticket booking request type does not integrate with any travel booking platform. It only creates a request record that requires manual follow-up. On the Approved tab, booking-type requests show 'Upload ticket' instead of 'Add to payroll', indicating manual ticket upload is needed after external booking.

⚠️ Processed Requests Cannot Be Deleted or Reversed

Once an air ticket request is processed (added to payroll or marked as complete), it cannot be deleted, reversed, or moved back to pending status. This prevents correction of submission errors or test data cleanup. Jira reference: TSSD-1900, TSSD-1403, TSSD-506

⚠️ Calendar Year vs Rolling Year Tracking

The platform tracks air ticket policy eligibility from the first request date (rolling year), not on a calendar year cycle. This prevents employees from requesting air tickets in a new calendar year if they made a request in the prior year within the renewal window. Jira reference: TSSD-711

Request Status Workflow

Air ticket requests follow a strict status progression:

  • Pending: Submitted but not yet approved. Employee can edit or delete at this stage.
  • Approved: Approved by all required approvers. Admin can add to payroll or upload ticket (for booking type).
  • Processed: Added to payroll or ticket uploaded. Cannot be reversed or deleted.
  • Rejected: Rejected by an approver. Cannot be moved back to pending (must create new request).

⚠️ One-Way Status Transitions: Once a request moves past Pending status, it cannot be edited or moved backward in the workflow. This is by design to maintain payroll integrity but limits flexibility for error correction.

Policy Change Impact

⚠️ Existing Requests Affected by Policy Changes: If policy details are changed (except name), existing requests may be rejected or deleted automatically. Plan policy changes carefully and communicate with employees who have pending requests.

Edge Cases

  • Employee home country changes mid-year: Policy allowance is based on configuration at policy creation. Changing employee home country does not automatically recalculate allowance. Admin must manually adjust or create new policy.
  • Employee grade changes mid-cycle: Similar to home country, grade changes do not trigger automatic allowance recalculation. Rate applies based on employee grade at time of policy assignment.
  • Employee joins mid-interval: First request eligibility is calculated from hire date or probation end date plus configured months. No automatic prorating for partial years.
  • Family size changes (new child): If policy includes dependent coverage, admin must manually update employee's policy assignment or coverage settings. System does not auto-detect family changes.
  • Employee leaves before using entitlement: No automatic integration with EOS. Unused air ticket allowance must be manually handled (forfeiture or cash payout) based on company policy.
  • Ticket price exceeds allowance cap: For reimbursement type, employee can request up to allowance limit. If actual ticket cost is higher, employee pays difference. For booking type, admin must coordinate with employee on coverage.
  • Multiple policies assigned to one employee: System allows this but may cause confusion. Best practice is one active policy per employee. Allowances are tracked separately per policy.
  • Policy deleted with pending requests: Pending requests are automatically rejected when policy is deleted. Communicate policy deletion to affected employees in advance.
  • Policy deactivated mid-request: Requests submitted while policy was active continue through approval flow. New requests cannot be submitted while policy is inactive.
  • Partial approval scenarios: For booking requests with multiple passengers, system does not support partial approval. Request is all-or-nothing.
  • Currency conversion for international tickets: Reimbursement requests support multi-currency input. System records currency and amount as entered. Payroll handles conversion based on payroll currency settings.
  • Employee transferred to different policy: Existing requests remain tied to original policy. New requests use new policy. Allowance balances are tracked separately per policy.
  • Multi-leg flights: For booking type, enter as single booking request with multiple passenger rows if needed. For reimbursement, submit one request with total amount or multiple requests if tracking separately.

Data Integrity Checks

Before processing payroll with air ticket requests, verify:

  • No approved requests for offboarded employees (check for 'Inactive' badge)
  • All approved amounts are within policy allowance limits
  • Booking-type requests have ticket documents uploaded
  • Employee currency matches payroll currency or conversion is handled
  • No duplicate requests for the same trip/period

Approval Flow Troubleshooting

  • Request stuck in pending: Check approval flow configuration. Verify approvers are active employees with access to Bayzat. Check if approver is on leave or offboarded.
  • Wrong approver assigned: Approval flow is determined at request submission time. Changing approval flow configuration does not affect pending requests. Reject and resubmit if routing is incorrect.
  • Employee cannot see approval status: Approver details are visible in admin detail view but may not be shown in employee self-service view. This is a known UX gap (WOF-005).

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the Request air ticket button disabled?

The button is disabled when you've used all requests in your current cycle. Check your policy card for 'Remaining requests: 0' status. The button will become available when your policy renews.

Can I partially encash my air ticket allowance?

No. Each encashment request must be for a specific amount. To split your allowance, submit multiple separate requests within your cycle limit.

How do I assign employees to air ticket policies?

Navigate to Settings → Company → Air ticket policies, select a policy, and use the employee assignment dialog. There is no bulk import or auto-assignment based on employee attributes.

Does the booking request type integrate with travel platforms?

No. Booking requests create a record only. After approval, you must manually book the ticket externally and upload it to the system.

When does 'Add to payroll' process the payment?

Clicking 'Add to payroll' adds the amount to the next payroll cycle. The payroll admin must finalize processing. It does not immediately process payment.

Can I edit an active policy?

No. Once employees have raised requests against a policy, you cannot edit it. Create a new policy for future cycles instead.

What happens to approved requests for offboarded employees?

They remain in the Approved tab with an 'Inactive' badge. Admins must manually cancel or process these requests before offboarding is complete.

Can I export filtered results?

The Download button exports all requests. It does not respect active filters or search criteria.

Can I see who is reviewing my request?

Employee view: The request shows 'Pending' status but approver details are not visible. Admin view: The detail panel shows the full approver chain.

When does my air ticket policy renew?

The employee profile Air tickets tab shows 'Expiring on [date]'. The My air tickets self-service view shows 'Used already' status without the expiry date.

Can I automate actions when air ticket requests are created or approved?

No. The only workflow trigger available is 'Air ticket cycle renewal is due'. There are no triggers for request status changes, creation, or processing events.

How many employees can I assign to one policy?

There is no system limit, but assignment is manual. The policy employees dialog requires individual selection. With 88+ unassigned employees, this can be time-consuming.
