What is Air Tickets?

Overview

Air Tickets manages employee air ticket allowances through configurable policies that support three redemption types: reimbursement for purchased tickets, booking requests for company-arranged travel, or encashment of the benefit value. Access it via Payroll → Air tickets or Requests → Air tickets.

Admins create requests on behalf of employees, review submissions across four status tabs (Pending/Approved/Processed/Rejected), and add approved amounts to payroll.

Key Benefits

  • Three redemption options (reimbursement, booking, encashment) accommodate different employee needs
  • Policy-based allowance tracking prevents over-allocation and ensures compliance
  • Automated encashment workflows eliminate manual renewal reminders
  • Centralized request management with approval routing and payroll integration
  • Employee self-service visibility into policy status and remaining balance
  • Multi-currency support for international travel reimbursements

Strategic Context

🎯 What Air Tickets Solves

Eliminates manual tracking of air ticket entitlements and prevents employees from missing contractual benefits.

  • Replaces spreadsheet-based allowance tracking
  • Standardizes redemption processes across reimbursement, booking, and encashment
  • Enables automatic benefit delivery at predefined intervals

💰 Why It Matters

Air ticket allowances are mandatory employment contract benefits in UAE and KSA markets.

  • Prerequisite for payroll processing (encashment amounts flow to payroll)
  • Drives employee satisfaction by ensuring timely benefit delivery
  • Controls HR workload—over 60% of requests are encashments that can be automated

🏗️ How It Connects

Configuration template assigned to employees that generates transactional requests.

  • Policies assigned to employees define allowance amounts and renewal cycles
  • Requests route through approval workflows before payroll processing
  • One policy can affect multiple employees across departments and locations

Product Foundation Overview

How Air Tickets Fits

Air Tickets is a transactional feature that processes employee benefit requests based on pre-configured policies. Policies define allowance amounts, coverage, and redemption options, then employees submit requests that flow through approval and payroll.

Mental model: Air Ticket Policy (template) → Assigned to Employees → Employee submits request → Approved → Added to Payroll or Ticket booked

One policy can affect many employees, and each employee can submit multiple requests per renewal cycle until their allowance is exhausted.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating Air Ticket policies:

  • What redemption options will you offer? — Reimbursement for purchased tickets, company booking, encashment, or auto-encashment
  • How will allowances vary? — By seniority, country of residence, grade, or home country
  • What triggers renewal? — Hiring date, probation end date, or fixed annual date (e.g., Jan 1)
  • Who is covered? — Employee only, or employee plus dependents (spouse, children, parents)

Related Features

These Bayzat features work together with Air Tickets. Where available, links point to the published user guide.

  • Payroll Management — Processes approved air ticket encashments and reimbursements as payroll adjustments
  • Approval Workflows — Routes air ticket requests through configured approvers based on criteria like request type or amount
  • Employee Profile — Stores dependent information for coverage validation and displays policy status to employees
  • Workflows (Automation) — Automatically generates encashment requests at configured intervals for auto-encashment policies
  • Leave Encashment — Similar benefit encashment pattern for leave balances

Initial Setup & Configuration

Configuration Walkthrough

Before employees can submit air ticket requests, configure policies at the company level.

Settings → Company → Air Ticket Policies

Each policy controls allowance limits, redemption options, and eligibility rules. The configuration page displays:

  • Policy name and employee count
  • Redeem options enabled (Reimbursement, Booking, Encashment)
  • Active/Inactive toggle for each policy
  • Employee assignment status (Manual assignment with unassigned count)

Use the three-dot menu to View, Edit, or Delete policies.

Policy Setup

Create a new air ticket policy to define allowances and redemption options.

Step 1: Basic Setup

  • Policy name: Descriptive name (e.g., "Senior Management Air Tickets", "UAE Employees Annual Allowance")
  • Allowance limit: Currency and amount per employee per cycle
  • Coverage: Select who is eligible (employee only, employee + spouse, employee + children, employee + parents)

Note: Allowance amount applies collectively to all listed dependents, not individually per person.

Step 2: Policy Behaviour

  • Request timing: When employees can submit requests (e.g., after probation, from hire date)
  • Renewal period: Interval in months for allowance reset (e.g., 12 months for annual benefit)
  • Maximum requests: Limit on number of requests per renewal cycle
  • Redeem options: Select which request types to enable:
    • Reimbursement: Employee purchases ticket, submits receipt for reimbursement
    • Air ticket booking: HR/Admin books ticket on employee's behalf
    • Encashment: Employee receives cash equivalent of allowance

⚠️ Policy Editing Restriction: Active policies cannot be edited once employees have raised ticket requests. Existing requests will be rejected/deleted if policy details change (except name).

Step 3: Eligibility Date Configuration

For policies with automatic encashment enabled:

  • Eligibility date: Choose between hiring date or probation end date
  • Renewal period: Set interval in months for automatic encashment generation

Automatic Encashment: System generates encashment requests automatically when employees become eligible. Choose between direct payroll addition or creating approved requests for admin review.

⚠️ Missing Date Warning: Employees without hire date or probation end date set in their profile will not be eligible for automatic encashment. Ensure employee profiles are complete before assigning them to auto-encashment policies.

Assignment & Activation

Assign Employees to Policy

After configuring policy details, select which employees are eligible.

  • Click "View all employees" or the employee count to open assignment dialog
  • Use search to find employees by name or ID
  • Apply filters by department, office, or reports-to hierarchy
  • Select employees individually or in bulk

⚠️ One Policy Per Employee: Employees cannot be assigned to multiple air ticket policies. If you assign an employee already on another policy, they will be moved to the new policy.

Activate the Policy

Toggle the policy to Active status to enable employee requests.

  • Active: Employees can submit requests based on configured redeem options
  • Inactive: Request submission is disabled; employees see policy status as Inactive

Employees assigned to active policies will see their policy details in the mobile app under Work → My Air Tickets, including:

  • Policy status (Available/Availed/Inactive)
  • Remaining requests in current cycle
  • Remaining balance from allowance
  • Coverage (who is eligible)

Configure who approves air ticket requests before processing.

Set up approval routing:

  • Default flow: Applies to all requests unless criteria-based flows match
  • Advanced flows: Route requests based on conditions (e.g., amount threshold, request type, employee department)

Approval Flow

How Air Tickets Requests Route

All air ticket requests require approval before processing. The default approval flow routes requests to the Payroll table admin for approval. Advanced approval flows can route requests to different approvers based on criteria such as policy allowance amount and currency.

Default Approval Flow

When no advanced approval flow criteria match, requests follow the default path:

  • Approver: Payroll table admin
  • Steps: Single-step approval

Advanced Approval Flows

Criteria-based routing directs high-value requests to specific approvers:

  • Adv AF - Amount exceeding 3000: When Policy Allowance Amount > 3000 AND Currency = AED, routes to Line Manager - 1 level
  • Adv AF - Amount more than 4000: Additional amount-based criteria for higher thresholds

Routing Examples

How different Air Tickets requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Standard Reimbursement Request (2,500 AED)

Employee submits reimbursement request for 2,500 AED → Routes to Payroll table admin (default flow) → Admin approves → Request moves to Approved tab

Example 2: High-Value Booking Request (3,500 AED)

Employee submits booking request for 3,500 AED → Amount exceeds 3,000 AED threshold → Routes to Line Manager - 1 level (advanced flow) → Manager approves → Request moves to Approved tab

Example 3: Encashment Request (Policy Allowance)

Employee submits encashment request → Routes based on policy allowance amount and currency → If allowance > 3,000 AED, goes to Line Manager; otherwise to Payroll admin → After approval, request can be added to payroll

Approval Status Tracking

Requests move through status tabs based on approval decisions:

  • Pending: Awaiting approver action
  • Approved: Approved by all required approvers, ready for processing
  • Rejected: Declined by approver
  • Processed: Added to payroll or booking completed

Business Rules & Limitations

Policy Configuration Requirements

Air ticket policies require careful setup of interdependent fields. Misconfiguration leads to incorrect allowance calculations.

⚠️ Policy Configuration Complexity

Each policy must individually configure: Allowance Limit, Eligibility (Employee + dependents), first request timing (months from hire), renewal cycle, requests per cycle, and redeem methods. All fields are interdependent.

By Design: This complexity reflects the variety of air ticket benefit structures across organizations.

Employee Assignment Limitations

⚠️ No Bulk Policy Assignment

Employees must be assigned to policies manually one at a time. No bulk import or automatic assignment based on employee attributes exists.

By Design: Manual assignment ensures explicit policy-to-employee mapping.

Request Modification Constraints

Processed requests cannot be deleted or reversed: Once a request is processed (added to payroll or booked), it cannot be moved back to pending or deleted. This prevents correction of submission errors after processing.

Rejected requests cannot be moved to pending: After rejection, requests cannot be reverted to pending status even if policy is modified. Employee must submit a new request.

Policy changes affect existing requests: Existing requests may be rejected or deleted if policy details change (except name changes).

Policy Renewal & Carry-Over

No indefinite carry-over: Unused air ticket balances do not automatically transfer between policy years. Carry-over rules must be manually configured per policy.

Renewal tracking from first request: Policy eligibility tracks from the first request date, not on a calendar year cycle. This affects when employees can submit new requests after using their allowance.

Approval Flow Constraints

Custom roles require development intervention: Adding leave managers or custom approvers to air ticket approval flows cannot be self-serviced. Custom roles are in beta and cannot be edited after creation.

No notification for proxy requesters: When managers raise requests on behalf of employees, no automated notification tags line managers outside the standard approval flow.

Access & Permissions

Policy configuration restricted to super admins: Only super administrators can access air ticket policy configuration by default. Other admin roles require custom permission setup.

No audit trail for policy creation: Platform does not provide self-service method to view who created a policy or when. Users must contact support for historical policy metadata.

Export & Reporting

Download button does not respect filters: Export functionality downloads all requests but does not respect active filters or search criteria.

Integration Gaps

No automatic EOS integration: Air ticket entitlements are not automatically integrated with End of Service calculations. Employees leaving before using their allowance must manually handle benefit conversion or forfeiture.

No dynamic reimbursement calculation: System cannot automatically calculate reimbursements based on actual ticket costs while respecting annual policy limits. All reimbursement amounts are manual.

Edge Cases

  • Employee home country changes mid-year: Recalculate eligibility manually. Policy rates are based on home country at policy assignment.
  • Employee grade changes mid-cycle: Rate from policy assignment time applies. Grade changes do not auto-update allowance mid-cycle.
  • Employee joins mid-interval: Prorated entitlement not automatic. Configure first request timing (months from hire) in policy.
  • Family size changes (new child): Update employee dependents in profile. Coverage eligibility updates on next request.
  • Employee leaves before using entitlement: No automatic EOS integration. HR must manually handle forfeiture or payout.
  • Ticket price exceeds capped amount: Employee pays difference OR company manually covers. No automatic handling.
  • Employee has multiple eligible policies: System does not prevent this. Employee can submit under any assigned policy (not cumulative).
  • Partial year for auto-created requests: Auto-encashment generates at eligibility date (hire + months configured). Not prorated for partial year.
  • Destination requires visa: Booking workflow has no visa handling. Manage externally.
  • Service period not met for encashment: Auto-encashment does not generate until eligibility criteria satisfied (hire date + configured months).
  • Policy changes mid-cycle: System handles updated intervals by rejecting existing requests. Employees resubmit under new rules.
  • Employee termination with outstanding encashments: Approved but unprocessed encashments remain in queue. Admin must manually reject or process.
  • Multiple policies assigned to one employee: Employee sees all policies on My air tickets page. Can submit under any (subject to each policy's remaining balance).
  • Manual override needed for automated request: HR cannot modify auto-generated encashment requests. Must reject and create manual request if adjustment needed.

Workflow Automation Gaps

Limited workflow triggers: Only "Air ticket cycle renewal is due" trigger exists. No triggers for request lifecycle events (created, approved, rejected, processed). To automate notifications or actions based on request status changes, use manual workarounds or contact support for custom workflow requests.

Data Export Limitations

Download does not respect filters: Export button downloads all requests regardless of active filters or search criteria. To export filtered results, manually filter in Excel after download.

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the Request air ticket button disabled?

The button is disabled when you have used all requests in your current cycle. Check your policy summary card for remaining requests and cycle renewal date.

Can I partially encash my air ticket allowance?

No. Each encashment request must be for a specific amount. To split your allowance, submit multiple separate requests within your cycle limit.

Does the booking request type book tickets automatically?

No. Booking requests create a record that requires manual follow-up. After approval, you must upload the ticket manually—there is no integration with travel booking platforms.

Can I see who is currently reviewing my air ticket request?

Employee view: Approver details are not visible in the My air tickets self-service view. Contact your HR admin for approval status.

When does Add to payroll process the payment?

Clicking Add to payroll adds the amount to the next payroll cycle. The payroll admin must finalize processing—it does not auto-process immediately.

Can I assign employees to air ticket policies in bulk?
