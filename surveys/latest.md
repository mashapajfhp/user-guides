</div>

<div class="section-body">

<div class="subsection">

### Overview

Surveys enable HR teams to collect employee feedback on engagement, satisfaction, workplace environment, and organizational health. Access surveys through Insights ‚Üí Surveys (admins) or Insights ‚Üí My Surveys (employees).

Create surveys manually or using AI, distribute to targeted employee groups, and analyze results with demographic filters and trend visualizations.

- Track engagement trends over time with historical score comparisons
- Customize engagement questions to match company-specific needs
- Protect employee anonymity while maintaining actionable analytics
- Organize high-volume surveys with categorization and filtering
- Analyze responses by department, manager, gender, and other demographics

## Product Foundation Overview

</div>

<div class="section-body">

<div class="subsection">

### How Surveys Fits

Surveys are transactional data collection tools that capture employee feedback at specific points in time. They exist independently from core HR records but can be filtered and analyzed using employee demographic data.

<div class="info-box">

**Mental model:** Curious about something ‚Üí Create a Survey ‚Üí Distribute to Employees ‚Üí Collect Responses ‚Üí Aggregate Insights ‚Üí Take Action

</div>

One survey can reach all employees or targeted segments. Survey results are point-in-time snapshots that don't alter employee records but inform organizational decisions.

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating surveys:

- **What feedback do you need?** ‚Äî Define specific objectives (engagement, satisfaction, NPS, pulse checks, 360 reviews, exit interviews)
- **Who should respond?** ‚Äî Determine target audience ‚Äî all employees, a specific department, role, location, or any targeted group
- **How will you ensure honest responses?** ‚Äî Decide on anonymity settings (anonymous responses hidden for groups under 3 employees)
- **How will you act on results?** ‚Äî Plan follow-up actions to avoid survey fatigue and maintain trust

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Related Features

- **Employee Records** ‚Äî Provides demographic data (department, gender, location) for survey segmentation and analysis
- **Performance Management** ‚Äî Engagement survey data may inform performance decisions and 360 review processes
- **Notification System** ‚Äî Distributes surveys and sends reminders to non-responders via email and push notifications
- **Reporting & Analytics** ‚Äî Enables trend visualization, heatmaps, and downloadable Excel reports of survey results
- **Mobile App** ‚Äî Allows employees to complete surveys on mobile devices with draft-saving capability

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Prerequisites & Requirements

| Requirement | Description | Status |
|----|----|----|
| Employee Registration | Employees must be registered on Bayzat platform to receive and respond to surveys | Required |
| Survey Creator Role | User must have Company Survey Creator or Super Admin role to create and manage surveys | Required |
| Minimum Response Threshold | At least 3 employee responses required to view anonymous survey results (protects individual identity) | Required |
| Demographic Data | Employee department, role, manager, and location data improves filtering and analysis capabilities | Recommended |
| AI Survey Generation | Access to Bayzat AI for automated question generation based on prompts | Optional |

</div>

## Initial Setup

</div>

<div class="section-body">

<div class="subsection">

### First-Time Configuration

Before creating your first survey, complete these foundational setup tasks to ensure optimal survey management and data collection.

#### 1. Define Survey Strategy

- Identify survey types needed (engagement, NPS, pulse, satisfaction, exit, 360 reviews)
- Determine survey frequency (weekly pulse, quarterly engagement, annual satisfaction)
- Establish anonymity policies for each survey type
- Set baseline participation targets (minimum response rates)

#### 2. Understand the Engagement Question Bank

Engagement surveys come with a pre-built question bank organized into 10 categories. You can customize questions during the survey creation wizard.

<div class="nav-path">

Insights ‚Üí Engagement insights ‚Üí New engagement survey ‚Üí Step 1: Questions

</div>

The 10 pre-built engagement categories are:

- **eNPS** ‚Äî 1 question (Net Promoter Score: "How likely are you to recommend our company as a place to work?" on a 0-10 scale)
- **Work environment** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Recognition & Reward** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Growth** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Manager Support** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Employee Wellbeing** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Performance Management** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Meaningful Work** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Organization Fit** ‚Äî 2 questions
- **Teamwork** ‚Äî 2 questions

Within each category you can remove existing questions or click **"Add another question"** to create custom ones.

#### Available Templates

Bayzat provides 7 pre-built survey templates. Navigate to **Insights ‚Üí Surveys ‚Üí New survey ‚Üí Choose a template** to preview any template before using it.

<div class="nav-path">

Insights ‚Üí Surveys ‚Üí New survey ‚Üí Choose a template

</div>

| Template | Questions | Time | Purpose |
|----|----|----|----|
| **Employee Engagement** | 19 | 10 mins | Measure performance against 9 key engagement drivers. Results feed into Engagement insights. |
| **Weekly Pulse Survey** | 2 | 2 mins | Track employee morale on a weekly basis with emoji mood scale and open comments. |
| **Employee Net Promoter Score (eNPS)** | 2 | 2 mins | Measure how employees feel about the company using the standard NPS 0-10 scale. |
| **Bayz Wellness Feedback** | 7 | 5 mins | Collect feedback on wellness events (biometric screenings, yoga, nutrition talks, etc.). |
| **Employee Satisfaction Survey** | 12 | 7 mins | Measure job satisfaction across work, growth, compensation, and manager relationships. |
| **Work Environment Survey** | 11 | 7 mins | Assess physical workspace, tools, teamwork, training, and job security. |
| **Health Insurance Feedback** | 8 | 8 mins | Understand employee priorities and experience with health insurance coverage. |

Each template preview has two tabs:

- **Questions tab:** Browse all questions and their types before starting
- **Sample Results tab:** Preview exactly what the results dashboard will look like with sample data (see [Understanding Survey Results](#understanding-results) below)

#### Question Scales

- **eNPS:** 0‚Äì10 scale (Not at all likely ‚Üí Extremely likely)
- **Engagement categories:** 1‚Äì10 scale (Strongly Disagree ‚Üí Strongly Agree)
- **Satisfaction/Mood:** 5-point emoji scale (Extremely dissatisfied ‚Üí Extremely satisfied, or Terrible ‚Üí Amazing)
- **Rating:** 1‚Äì5 numeric scale (Not at all ‚Üí Significantly, or Terrible ‚Üí Excellent)

<div class="info-box">

**Note:** There is no standalone settings page for engagement questions. The question bank is configured directly inside the engagement survey creation wizard (Step 1: Questions).

#### 3. Set Up 360 Review Feedback Categories

If using 360-degree reviews, configure feedback categories before creating surveys.

<div class="nav-path">

Settings ‚Üí Performance

</div>

- Define categories relevant to performance evaluation (e.g., Leadership, Communication, Technical Skills, Collaboration)
- Align categories with organizational competency frameworks
- Enable AI survey generation to use these categories for targeted question creation

#### 4. Configure Participant Groups

- Verify employee registration status (unregistered employees will be highlighted during survey creation)
- Review department and job role data accuracy for demographic filtering
- Establish manager hierarchies for upward feedback in 360 reviews
- Identify peer groups for peer feedback collection

#### 5. Establish Survey Naming Conventions

- Create consistent naming format: \[Type\] - \[Date/Period\] - \[Target Group\]
- Example: "Engagement Q1 2024 - All Employees" or "Exit Survey - January 2024"
- Use categorization for high-volume survey management (folders or tags)

#### 6. Plan Notification & Reminder Settings

Notifications are configured per survey during the creation wizard (Step 3: Settings ‚Üí Notifications and reminders).

<div class="nav-path">

Survey creation wizard ‚Üí Step 3: Settings ‚Üí Notifications and reminders

</div>

- **Publication notification:** Notify all participants when survey is published (Email + App notification, enabled by default)
- **Reminders for non-responders:**
  - 7 days before survey ends (Email + App)
  - 5 days before survey ends (Email + App)
  - 3 days before survey ends (Email + App, enabled by default)
  - 1 day before survey ends (Email + App, enabled by default)

### Post-Setup Verification

After completing initial configuration, verify setup by creating a test survey:

1.  Create a draft survey using AI or manual question creation
2.  Add a small test participant group (e.g., HR team only)
3.  Preview survey on both web and mobile platforms
4.  Test anonymity settings (if applicable)
5.  Verify notification delivery (email and push)
6.  Submit test responses and review results interface
7.  Delete test survey before launching production surveys

<div class="warning-box">

**‚ö†Ô∏è Survey Deletion Limitation:** The platform currently lacks a self-service survey deletion mechanism. Contact engineering support to delete surveys. This limitation affects both test and production surveys.

<div class="subsection">

### Prerequisites for Specific Survey Types

#### Engagement Surveys

- Pre-built question bank with 10 categories (19 questions total) ‚Äî customize during survey creation
- Recommended 30-day survey period (system recommendation shown during setup)
- Historical engagement data for trend analysis (if available)

#### 360 Review Surveys

- Up to 4 participant groups defined (self, manager, peers, direct reports)
- Maximum 30 questions per survey
- Feedback categories configured in Performance Management settings
- Manager and peer relationships established in employee records

#### NPS Surveys

- Clear communication plan for score interpretation (Promoters: 9-10, Neutral: 7-8, Detractors: 1-6)
- Action plan for addressing detractor feedback
- Baseline NPS target (0-30 = good, 30+ = excellent)

#### Exit Surveys

- Integration with offboarding workflow (if automated)
- Anonymity policy for departing employees
- Process for reopening surveys if needed (requires support intervention)

### Known Setup Limitations

| Limitation | Impact | Workaround |
|----|----|----|
| Cannot edit surveys after responses begin | Questions cannot be modified once participants start responding | Thoroughly review surveys in draft state; create new survey if changes needed |
| No self-service survey deletion | Requires engineering team intervention to delete surveys | Contact support for survey deletion requests |
| Cannot add participants to ongoing surveys | Participant list is locked once survey is launched | Create duplicate survey for additional participants |
| Cannot export non-submitted survey reports | Export functionality only supports completed submissions | Use UI filters to view non-submitters; manual data compilation required |

### Task: Understanding Survey Results

Survey results are displayed differently depending on the question type. Understanding these visualizations helps you interpret feedback accurately and take informed action.

#### Result Visualization Types

When you view results (either in Sample Results previews or actual survey results), questions produce one of five visualization types:

| Visualization | Question Type | What It Shows | Used In |
|----|----|----|----|
| **NPS Donut Chart** | eNPS / NPS (0-10 recommend scale) | Donut chart split into Promoters (9-10, green), Passives (7-8, yellow), and Detractors (0-6, pink). NPS Score displayed in center. | Employee Engagement, eNPS |
| **Numeric Scale Bar Chart** | Opinion scale (0-10, 1-10, or 1-5) | Vertical bars for each scale value showing percentage and respondent count. Average response score displayed above. | All templates with scale questions |
| **Emoji Scale Bar Chart** | Satisfaction / Mood (5-point emoji) | Five vertical bars with emoji faces ranging from negative to positive. Percentage, respondent count, and average shown. | Weekly Pulse, Employee Satisfaction, Work Environment |
| **Multiple Choice Horizontal Bars** | Single-select / Multi-select | Horizontal bars per option showing response count and percentage. Labeled "(multiple choice selection)". | Employee Satisfaction, Work Environment, Bayz Wellness |
| **Free Text Response List** | Open-ended text | Scrollable list of individual responses with "All responses" and "Starred" tabs. Star notable responses for follow-up. | All templates with text questions |

#### How to Read NPS Results

<figure class="screenshot-container">
<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mashapajfhp/user-guides/main/surveys/v1/validation/screenshots/51-enps-sample-results.png" class="screenshot" loading="lazy" alt="eNPS Sample Results showing NPS donut chart and bar chart" />
<figcaption>eNPS template Sample Results: NPS donut chart (top), per-value bar chart (middle), and free text responses (bottom)</figcaption>
</figure>

The NPS donut chart shows three segments:

- **Promoters (9-10):** Highly satisfied employees likely to recommend the company
- **Passives (7-8):** Satisfied but not enthusiastic ‚Äî at risk of disengaging
- **Detractors (0-6):** Dissatisfied employees who may negatively impact morale

**NPS Score = % Promoters - % Detractors.** Example: 40% Promoters - 20% Detractors = NPS Score of 20. Scores range from -100 to +100. A score above 0 is good; above 30 is excellent.

Below the donut chart, a bar chart shows the per-value distribution (0 through 10) with the number of respondents and percentage for each value.

#### How to Read Scale Results

<figure class="screenshot-container">
<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mashapajfhp/user-guides/main/surveys/v1/validation/screenshots/52-satisfaction-sample-results.png" class="screenshot" loading="lazy" alt="Employee Satisfaction Sample Results showing emoji scale and multiple choice visualizations" />
<figcaption>Employee Satisfaction template Sample Results: emoji scale charts (top), multiple choice bars (middle), free text list (bottom)</figcaption>
</figure>

Each scale question displays:

- **Response count:** "X out of Y respondents answered this question" ‚Äî shows participation rate per question
- **Average response:** Displayed with a green indicator (e.g., "Avg response: 4.0") ‚Äî higher is better
- **Distribution bars:** Vertical bars showing how responses are spread across the scale values
- **Scale labels:** Anchor text at the bottom (e.g., "Extremely dissatisfied" to "Extremely satisfied")

#### How to Read Multiple Choice Results

Multiple choice questions display horizontal bars showing:

- Each answer option with its response count and percentage (e.g., "4 responses \| 40%")
- Bar width proportional to the percentage of respondents who selected that option
- Only options that received responses are displayed with visible bars

#### How to Read Free Text Results

<figure class="screenshot-container">
<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mashapajfhp/user-guides/main/surveys/v1/validation/screenshots/50-weekly-pulse-sample-results.png" class="screenshot" loading="lazy" alt="Weekly Pulse Sample Results showing emoji scale and free text responses" />
<figcaption>Weekly Pulse template Sample Results: emoji mood scale (top) and free text responses with starring capability (bottom)</figcaption>
</figure>

Free text responses appear as a scrollable list with two tabs:

- **All responses:** Complete list of written feedback from respondents
- **Starred:** Responses you have bookmarked for follow-up or discussion

Each response has two action buttons:

- **Expand (arrow icon):** View the full response text in a larger view
- **Star (star icon):** Bookmark notable responses for follow-up ‚Äî starred responses appear in the "Starred" tab

**Tip:** Use the Sample Results tab when previewing templates to understand what your results dashboard will look like before launching a survey. This helps you choose the right question types to get the visualizations you need for analysis.

## Business Rules & Limitations

</div>

<div class="section-body">

<div class="subsection">

### Survey Creation & Management

| Rule | Description | Impact |
|----|----|----|
| **Anonymous Response Threshold** | Anonymous responses are hidden for groups with fewer than 3 employees | Protects individual identity but may limit detailed analysis for small teams |
| **Survey Editing Restrictions** | Cannot edit surveys once they are in 'ongoing' state | Questions can only be modified during draft or scheduled states |
| **Engagement Driver Requirements** | At least 1 scale question required for each engagement driver | Survey cannot be saved without meeting this minimum |
| **Question Limits** | Maximum of 30 questions per 360 review survey | Prevents survey fatigue while limiting comprehensive assessments |
| **Participant Group Limits** | Up to 4 participant groups per 360 review | Includes self, managers, peers, and optional direct reports |

- **Unregistered Employees:** Employees not registered on Bayzat will be highlighted during participant selection but cannot receive surveys
- **Response Timing:** Surveys can only be responded to after the configured start date/time
- **Survey Duration:** Minimum survey response time is 10 days; recommended duration is 30 days
- **Historical Data:** Employee data used in analysis reflects values at time of survey submission, not current values

<div id="troubleshooting-edge-cases" class="section section section-troubleshooting-edge-cases">

<div class="section-header">

## Troubleshooting & Edge Cases

</div>

<div class="section-body">

<div class="subsection">

### Common Issues

| Issue | Cause | Resolution |
|----|----|----|
| Survey results not visible | Fewer than 1 employee has responded | Wait for at least one response before viewing results |
| Anonymous responses hidden | Filter criteria results in fewer than 3 employees | Broaden filter criteria or review at higher organizational level |
| Cannot edit survey questions | Survey is in 'ongoing' state | Questions can only be edited during draft or scheduled states; contact support for urgent changes |
| Engagement driver error | No scale question assigned to engagement driver | Add at least one scale question to each engagement driver before saving |
| Unregistered employees highlighted | Selected participants are not registered on Bayzat | Register employees on platform before sending survey or remove from participant list |
| Export missing NPS scores | NPS scores only display in UI, not in exports | Manually record NPS scores from platform UI for external reporting |
| Cannot delete completed survey | No self-service deletion mechanism | Contact engineering team for survey deletion requests |
| AI-generated questions not relevant | Prompt lacks specificity or context | Regenerate with more detailed prompt including survey topic, purpose, and industry context |

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Edge Cases

- **Survey Fatigue:** Employees may disengage if surveys are too frequent or if feedback appears ignored; balance frequency with action on results
- **Cross-Platform Responses:** Employees can save draft responses on web and resume on mobile, or vice versa
- **Historical Data Gaps:** When calculating time trends, gaps in historical data may affect accuracy of trend analysis
- **Anonymous Feedback Paradox:** Improved anonymity may reduce admin ability to act on specific feedback while encouraging honest responses
- **Small Team Analysis:** Demographic filters on anonymous surveys may inadvertently reveal identities in teams with fewer than 5 members
- **Automated Survey Timing:** Health insurance surveys triggered automatically may conflict with other scheduled surveys, increasing response burden
- **Custom Question Comparability:** Custom engagement questions cannot be compared to historical data if they differ from standard questions
- **Scheduled Survey Cancellation:** Scheduled surveys can be canceled from web or mobile before launch, but not after start time
- **Shared Results Visibility:** Shared survey results only show summary page, not detailed individual responses
- **Response Deadline Enforcement:** Surveys have defined deadlines, but system behavior when deadline passes varies by survey type

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Best Practices

- Test survey questions with small group before wide distribution
- Set realistic survey duration (minimum 10 days, recommended 30 days)
- Communicate survey purpose and anonymity status clearly to employees
- Review and act on feedback within 2-4 weeks of survey close
- Avoid scheduling multiple surveys simultaneously to prevent fatigue
- Use AI-generated questions as starting point, then customize for context
- Export and archive survey data before requesting deletion
- Monitor response rates and send targeted reminders to non-responders

## FAQs & Support

</div>

<div class="section-body">

<div class="subsection">

### Frequently Asked Questions

<div class="faq-accordion">

Can I edit a survey after employees have started responding?

No. Once a survey is ongoing and responses have been collected, you cannot edit questions or add new ones. You can only edit surveys in draft or scheduled states.

How do I delete a survey I no longer need?

The platform currently lacks self-service survey deletion. Contact the engineering team for assistance with removing surveys and associated data.

Can I export a report showing employees who haven't submitted the survey?

While you can filter non-submitters in the UI, the export functionality only supports completed submissions. You cannot generate downloadable reports for non-submitted surveys.

How do I add new participants to an existing survey?

You cannot dynamically add participants to ongoing surveys. Plan your participant list carefully before launching.

Can I reopen a completed survey?

No. The surveys module does not support reopening completed surveys. Create a new survey if you need to collect additional responses.

Why can't I see NPS scores in my downloaded report?

NPS scores are visible on the platform UI but are not included in exported reports. View scores directly in the platform.

How does anonymity work in surveys?

Anonymous responses are hidden for groups with fewer than 3 employees to protect individual identity. Results are blurred for small departments in heatmap views.

Can I use AI to create survey questions?

Yes. Use the 'Start from AI' option and provide a specific prompt describing your survey topic, purpose, and desired question types.

How do I track survey completion rates?

View response rates in the Results tab. The system shows overall completion percentages and allows filtering by department or role.

Can employees save incomplete survey responses?

Yes. Employees can save draft responses and resume surveys later on web or mobile platforms.

<div id="glossary" class="section section section-glossary">

<div class="section-header">

## Glossary

</div>

<div class="section-body">

| Term | Definition |
|----|----|
| **360 Review** | A performance evaluation method that collects feedback from multiple participant groups including self, managers, peers, and direct reports. |
| **Anonymous Survey** | A survey where individual responses cannot be traced back to specific employees, protecting respondent identity. |
| **Detractor** | In NPS surveys, an employee who scores 1-6, indicating dissatisfaction and potential turnover risk. |
| **Engagement Driver** | A category or theme that influences employee engagement levels, such as recognition, growth opportunities, or work environment. |
| **Engagement Survey** | A survey designed to measure employee motivation, commitment, and emotional connection to their work and organization. |
| **Heatmap** | A visual representation of survey results showing engagement performance across departments or groups using color-coded indicators. |
| **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** | A metric calculated by subtracting the percentage of detractors from promoters, measuring overall employee satisfaction and loyalty. |
| **Promoter** | In NPS surveys, an employee who scores 9-10, indicating high satisfaction and likelihood to recommend the organization. |
| **Pulse Survey** | A short, frequent survey designed to track employee morale and sentiment trends over time. |
| **Response Rate** | The percentage of invited employees who completed the survey, used to measure survey participation effectiveness. |
| **Survey Fatigue** | A decline in survey participation or quality caused by excessive survey frequency or lack of action on feedback. |
| **Survey Template** | A pre-built survey with predefined questions and structure, designed for common use cases like engagement or satisfaction assessments. |
