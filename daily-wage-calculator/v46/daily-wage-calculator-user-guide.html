Daily Wage Calculator

Calculate daily wages for salary proration, leave encashment, and unpaid leave deductions

What is Daily Wage Calculator?

Feature Overview

The Daily Wage Calculator is a flexible and customizable method for calculating daily wages, including salary proration, leave encashment, and unpaid leave deductions. This feature enables HR teams and payroll administrators to accurately determine employee compensation adjustments based on actual days worked and leave taken.

Who Uses This Feature?

The primary users are Superadmin, Payroll table managers, and transaction processors. Additionally, HR personnel and payroll administrators responsible for managing employee leave and salary calculations rely on this feature for daily operations.

Key Benefits

Flexible Calculation Methods

Choose from calendar days, working days, or custom days to align with your company's labor law requirements. Most UAE and KSA companies use 30 custom days to align with local regulations.

Accurate Salary Adjustments

Automatically generate pay deductions and calculate daily pay based on salary and working days, ensuring precise compensation calculations for all leave scenarios.

Comprehensive Leave Management

Handle multiple leave scenarios including unpaid leave deductions, leave encashment for end-of-service, and salary proration for new joiners and departing employees with a single unified system.

Product Foundation Overview

Business Problem Solved

The Daily Wage Calculator solves the challenge of calculating and deducting daily pay for employees taking unpaid leave by determining the appropriate salary reduction based on the number of working or calendar days. Without this feature, manual calculations would be time-consuming and prone to errors.

Core Concepts

Daily pay is calculated by dividing total salary by number of actual working days, and can use either calendar days (30 days) or working days (e.g., 22 days). Public holidays are deducted from working days to ensure accurate calculations.

Related Features

Salary Proration

Adjusts and calculates an employee's salary proportionally based on the actual days worked, affecting their first salary and end-of-service settlement.

Leave Encashment

Allows super admins to configure and customize leave encashment policies for end-of-service calculations.

Unpaid Leave Deductions

Calculates and manages unpaid leave deductions on a daily basis for employee payroll.

Access the Daily Wage Calculator from Settings → Payroll → Daily Wage Calculation. This is the central configuration hub for all daily wage calculation methods in your organization.

  2. Select Payroll from the settings options
  3. Choose Daily Wage Calculation from the payroll submenu

Setup Process

Initial Configuration

Before setting up the Daily Wage Calculator, ensure you have determined your organization's calculation methodology. Most UAE and KSA companies use 30 custom days to align with labor law. However, your specific requirements may differ based on your employment contracts and labor agreements.

Step-by-Step Setup

1

Navigate to Daily Wage Calculation Settings

Go to Settings > Payroll > Daily Wage Calculation. The configuration panel will display your current settings and available options.

2

Select Calculation Basis

Select calculation base from three options: calendar days, working days, or custom days. Click the dropdown menu to view all available options.

3

Choose Salary Components

Choose salary components to factor into calculations. Select either Basic Only or Basic + Allowances depending on your payroll policy. This selection applies to all daily wage calculations including proration, leave encashment, and unpaid leave deductions.

4

Configure Exclusions

Enable options to exclude weekends and public holidays from the calculation if applicable to your organization. Public holidays are deducted from working days when calculating daily pay.

5

Review Configuration Summary

The system displays a summary of your selected configuration. Verify that the calculation basis, salary components, and exclusion settings match your requirements before proceeding.

6

Save Configuration

Click the Save button to apply your Daily Wage Calculation settings. Changes will affect all active (unpaid) amounts in the payroll table.

7

Handle Existing Transactions (if applicable)

When payroll month is open, super admin must reject existing prorated salary transactions before saving new proration settings. If prompted, review and reject any active transactions that need to be recalculated with the new settings.

Verification Steps

  • Confirm that the Daily Wage Calculation page displays your selected calculation basis
  • Verify that salary component selection matches your configuration
  • Check that exclusion settings (weekends, public holidays) are correctly applied

Feature Usage

Common Workflows

Processing Unpaid Leave

Approve unpaid leave requests, automatically generate pay deductions, calculate daily pay based on salary and working days. The system uses your configured daily wage calculation to determine the deduction amount for each unpaid leave day.

Calculating Leave Encashment

Configure and customize leave encashment policies for end-of-service calculations. Select salary components and calculation methods specific to each leave type for accurate end-of-service settlements.

Applying Salary Proration

Adjust and calculate an employee's salary proportionally based on the actual days worked, affecting their first salary and end-of-service settlement. The daily wage calculation automatically applies to prorated amounts.

Usage Examples

Example 1: Unpaid Leave Deduction An employee with a basic salary of AED 3,000 takes 2 days of unpaid leave in a month with 22 working days. Using the configured daily wage calculation (Basic Only / Custom Days / 30), the daily rate is AED 100 (3,000 ÷ 30). The unpaid leave deduction is AED 200 (2 days × AED 100), automatically reflected in the payroll.
Example 2: End-of-Service Leave Encashment An employee with 15 days of accrued vacation leave is leaving the company. The system calculates leave encashment using the configured daily wage formula (Basic + Allowances / Working Days / 22). If the daily rate is AED 150, the total encashment is AED 2,250 (15 days × AED 150), included in the final settlement.

Business Rules & Limitations

Core Business Rules

Daily pay is calculated by dividing total salary by the number of actual working days, and can use either calendar days (30 days) or working days (e.g., 22 days). The following table outlines the core calculation rules:

Rule Description Impact
Calculation Base Selection Calculation can be based on calendar days, working days, or custom days Determines the denominator in daily wage calculation; different bases produce different daily rates
Public Holiday Deduction Public holidays are deducted from working days Reduces the number of working days, increasing the daily wage rate
Salary Component Inclusion Can choose salary components to factor into calculations: basic salary only, basic salary plus allowances, or allowances only Determines which salary elements are included in the daily wage calculation
Month Length Variation Calculation varies depending on month length (e.g., 31 days in January, 28 days in February) Calendar day calculations automatically adjust; custom day calculations remain fixed
Unpaid Leave Deduction Unpaid leave deductions must be calculated daily and set off against the employee's monthly pay Reduces employee compensation based on approved unpaid leave days
Leave Encashment Eligibility Can configure specific leave types for end-of-service calculations Determines which leave balances are paid out during employee separation

System Constraints

  • Changes to calculation settings will affect all active (unpaid) amounts in the payroll table
  • Can choose between Basic Only or Basic + Allowances for salary components in leave encashment
  • Most UAE and KSA companies use 30 custom days to align with labor law
  • Calculation method changes require manual transaction review if payroll month is open
  • Public holiday calendar must be maintained separately and updated annually

Compliance Requirements

  • Daily wage calculations must comply with UAE Labor Law and KSA labor regulations
  • All salary components used in calculations must be documented and justified
  • Public holiday calendars must reflect official national holidays and company-specific holidays
  • Leave encashment calculations must follow end-of-service settlement requirements
  • Audit trails must be maintained for all calculation configuration changes

Edge Cases

Important: Calculation varies depending on month length (e.g., 31 days in January, 28 days in February), and different calculation methods can impact final wage calculations

Be aware of these edge cases when using the Daily Wage Calculator:

  • February Variations: When using calendar days, February calculations will differ from other months due to the 28/29-day month length. Custom day calculations (e.g., 30 days) remain consistent.
  • Mid-Month Policy Changes: If calculation settings change mid-month, existing transactions may need manual adjustment. Plan configuration changes at month-end when possible.
  • Public Holiday Overlaps: If a public holiday falls on a weekend, verify your public holiday calendar reflects the actual working day impact.
  • Partial Month Employment: For employees joining or leaving mid-month, salary proration calculations must account for actual days worked versus calendar days.
  • Multiple Leave Types: When an employee has both paid and unpaid leave in the same month, ensure each leave type uses the correct deduction calculation method.
  • Allowance Changes: If salary allowances change during a month, the daily wage calculation may need adjustment depending on when the change takes effect.

Resolution Steps

Follow these steps to resolve Daily Wage Calculator issues:

1

Identify the Issue

Determine which calculation is affected: salary proration, unpaid leave deduction, or leave encashment. Check the specific employee record and payroll transaction in question.

2

Verify Configuration Settings

Navigate to Settings > Payroll > Daily Wage Calculation and confirm the calculation base (calendar days, working days, or custom days) and salary component selection match your company policy.

3

Check Public Holiday Calendar

Verify that all applicable public holidays for the relevant month are recorded in the system. Missing holidays will inflate the daily wage rate.

4

Review Leave Policy Configuration

For unpaid leave or leave encashment issues, navigate to Settings > Leaves > Leave Policies and confirm the leave type has the correct deduction calculation method configured.

5

Manually Verify Calculation

Use the formula: Daily Rate = Total Salary ÷ (Working Days - Public Holidays) to manually verify the system's calculation is correct.

6

Reject and Resave Transactions

If configuration changes were made, reject the affected payroll transactions and resave them to recalculate with updated settings.

7

Document Changes

Record the issue, root cause, and resolution for audit purposes and future reference.

Glossary of Terms

Term Definition
Basic Salary The fixed monthly compensation component excluding allowances and benefits. Used as the basis for daily wage calculations when "Basic Only" is selected.
Calendar Days A calculation base using the actual number of days in a calendar month (28-31 days). Daily wage varies by month length.
Custom Days A fixed calculation base (typically 30 days) selected by the company to standardize daily wage calculations regardless of actual month length.
Daily Rate The calculated daily wage amount derived by dividing total salary by the number of working or calendar days. Used for proration, leave deductions, and encashment.
Daily Wage Calculator A Bayzat payroll feature that calculates daily wage rates for salary proration, leave encashment, and unpaid leave deductions based on configurable calculation bases and salary components.
End-of-Service (EOS) The final settlement calculation when an employee separates from the company, including gratuity, accrued leave encashment, and final salary adjustments.
Gratuity A lump-sum payment to employees at the end of service based on tenure and final salary, calculated according to UAE or KSA labor law requirements.
Gross Salary Total compensation including basic salary and all allowances. Used in daily wage calculations when "Basic + Allowances" is selected.
Leave Encashment The monetary value of unused paid leave paid to an employee, typically calculated at the end of service using the daily wage rate.
Leave Policy A company-defined set of rules governing leave types, accrual, eligibility, and deduction calculations including unpaid leave handling.
Public Holiday A nationally recognized or company-designated day off that is excluded from working day calculations, increasing the daily wage rate.
Salary Proration The proportional adjustment of an employee's salary based on actual days worked, affecting first salary and end-of-service calculations.
Unpaid Leave Leave taken by an employee without pay, resulting in a daily wage deduction from monthly salary based on the number of unpaid leave days.
Working Days A calculation base using the actual number of working days in a month (typically 22 days), excluding weekends and public holidays.
