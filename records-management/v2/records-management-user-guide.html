What is Records Management?

Overview

Records Management centralizes employee document storage, tracking, and compliance monitoring within Bayzat. Access it from Company > Employee Documents or individual employee profiles to upload, organize, and monitor document expiration dates.

Upload documents individually or in bulk, configure custom document types, set mandatory requirements, and receive automated expiry reminders.

Key Benefits

  • Automated expiry reminders prevent compliance violations for passports, visas, and Emirates IDs
  • OCR technology extracts data from uploaded documents, reducing manual entry errors
  • Centralized storage replaces scattered files across email and shared drives
  • Custom document types adapt to company-specific requirements beyond default templates
  • Bulk upload processes up to 20 documents at once for faster onboarding
  • Missing document tracking identifies incomplete employee records at a glance

Strategic Context

🎯 What Records Management Solves

Eliminates manual tracking of employee document expiration dates that cause compliance violations and last-minute renewals.

  • Replaces Excel spreadsheets and email reminders with automated monitoring
  • Standardizes document collection across onboarding, employment lifecycle, and offboarding
  • Enables proactive renewal workflows before documents expire

💰 Why It Matters

Document compliance is a prerequisite for payroll processing, visa renewals, and audit readiness.

  • Trade license documents must be current for WPS payroll file generation
  • Emirates ID and visa expiry dates drive End of Service calculations
  • Missing mandatory documents block employee onboarding completion

🏗️ How It Connects

Records Management is transactional data stored per employee, referenced by compliance and payroll processes.

  • Documents are uploaded to employee profiles and tracked in centralized dashboards
  • Expiry dates trigger email reminders to designated recipients
  • Document status affects employee profile completion percentage and payroll eligibility

Product Foundation Overview

How Records Management Fits

Records Management is a transactional data system that stores employee and company documents throughout their lifecycle. It sits at the center of HR operations, capturing documents from onboarding through offboarding.

Mental model: Document Type (template) → Uploaded to Employee/Company Record → Tracked for expiry → Automated reminders sent → Compliance maintained

Impact spans all employees and company-wide compliance requirements, affecting payroll processing, legal documentation, and regulatory adherence.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before configuring Records Management:

  • Which documents are mandatory vs optional? — Determines whether employees can proceed without uploading specific documents
  • Which documents have expiry dates? — Affects reminder configuration and compliance tracking workflows
  • How far in advance should expiry reminders be sent? — Ranges from 1-12 months before expiry; default is 7 months for critical documents
  • Who receives document expiry notifications? — Configure email recipients to ensure timely renewals

Related Features

These Bayzat features work together with Records Management. Where available, links point to the published user guide.

  • Employee Tickets — Employees can submit document-related requests that HR processes through the ticket system
  • End of Service — Final settlement calculations require complete employee documentation for compliance
  • Payroll Management — Trade license documents must be current to process payroll; employee payment details stored as documents
  • Letters — Generated letters are stored as company documents for record-keeping
  • Email Notifications — Automated expiry reminders sent to stakeholders based on document configuration
  • Onboarding — Document collection begins before employee joins, continues through active employment
  • Offboarding — Document management extends through employee exit, ensuring compliance records are complete

Initial Setup

First-Time Configuration

Before uploading or managing employee records, configure document types and company settings to ensure proper tracking and compliance.

Configure Document Types

Define which document types your organization requires for employee records:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Company
  2. Select Employee Document Types
  3. Click Add New to create custom document types
  4. Enter document name in English and Arabic (2-70 characters)
  5. Mark documents as mandatory if required for all employees
  6. Enable expiry date tracking for time-sensitive documents
  7. Set email reminder frequency (1-12 months before expiry)
  8. Save the document type configuration

Default document types: Passport, Residency Visa, and Emirates ID come pre-configured with 7-month expiry reminders. You cannot delete default types, but you can modify their settings.

Set Up Office Locations

Create office locations to enable location-based tracking and employee assignment:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Company
  2. Expand the Offices section
  3. Click Add New
  4. Enter office name and physical location
  5. Adjust attendance check-in radius if using location-based attendance (default: 100 meters)
  6. Save the office location

Geo-location coordinates are automatically filled when you enter the office address.

Configure Company Logo

Upload your company logo to appear on employee payslips and platform interface:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Company settings
  2. Locate the Company Logo section
  3. Click Change logo
  4. Upload your logo file
  5. Save changes

Add Bank Account Details

Store company bank information for payroll processing:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Payroll
  2. Expand the Bank Accounts section
  3. Click Add New
  4. Enter bank account details (account number, bank name, IBAN)
  5. Select the associated Trade License if you have multiple licenses
  6. Save the account details

Multiple accounts: You can add multiple bank accounts if your organization operates under different trade licenses or uses separate accounts for different purposes.

Prepare Employee Data Template

Before bulk uploading employee records:

  1. Navigate to Company → All Employees
  2. Click the bulk upload option
  3. Download the Excel template
  4. Review template columns and required fields
  5. Do not add or delete rows/columns in the template
  6. Use only provided dropdown options for categorical fields
  7. Format dates as year-month-day (YYYY-MM-DD)
  8. Paste values only — do not use formulas

Data validation: Remove sensitive data like passport numbers and visa details before uploading to prevent validation errors. Store these documents separately in the employee profile after upload.

Configure Custom Data Fields

If your organization tracks additional employee information beyond standard fields:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Custom Data
  2. Select Employee or Company custom data type
  3. Click Add New field
  4. Define field name, type, and validation rules
  5. Map custom fields to payroll templates if needed for salary calculations
  6. Save the custom field configuration

Before updating custom data: Always download a backup of existing records. Previous values will be overwritten and cannot be retrieved.

Set Up User Roles and Permissions

Define who can access and manage employee records:

  1. Review the Role Management guide for detailed role configuration
  2. Assign appropriate roles to HR administrators and managers
  3. Configure whether employees can edit their own personal information, documents, and dependent data

Employee editing permissions: You can control whether employees can add, edit, or delete their own documents, personal information, and dependent records. Configure these settings to balance data accuracy with employee self-service.

Verification Checklist

Before importing employee data, verify:

  • All mandatory document types are configured with appropriate expiry reminders
  • Office locations are created for all physical sites
  • Company logo is uploaded and displays correctly
  • Bank account details are accurate and linked to correct trade licenses
  • Custom data fields are defined for any organization-specific tracking needs
  • User roles and permissions align with your organization's data governance policies
  • Excel template is downloaded and reviewed for required fields

Key Tasks

Task: Configure Employee Document Types

Set up custom document categories to track employee compliance documents with expiry dates and mandatory status.

Subtask: Create Custom Document Type

  1. Navigate to Settings > Company
  2. Locate Employee Document Types section
  3. Click "Add New"
  4. Enter document type name (2-70 characters, supports Arabic)
  5. Mark as mandatory if required for all employees
  6. Enable expiry date tracking toggle
  7. Set email reminder frequency (1-12 months before expiry)
  8. Save configuration

Note: Default document types (Passport, Residency Visa, Emirates ID) cannot be deleted but can be edited. The "Others" document type has locked expiry toggle (always TRUE) but expiry date entry remains optional.

Subtask: Edit Existing Document Type

  1. Access Settings > Company > Employee Document Types
  2. Locate document type to modify
  3. Click Edit icon
  4. Update name, mandatory status, or reminder settings
  5. Save changes

Expected Outcome: Document types appear in upload dropdowns across employee profiles and bulk import templates.

Task: Upload Employee Documents

Store employee compliance documents centrally with OCR data extraction and automatic expiry tracking.

Subtask: Bulk Upload Documents

  1. Navigate to Company > Employee Documents
  2. Click "Upload document" or drag files to upload area
  3. Select up to 20 documents at once
  4. Choose processing method:
    • "Save as single document" — OCR extracts data automatically
    • "Save each page separately" — manual data entry required
  5. Review OCR-extracted data (name, document type, expiry date)
  6. Assign each document to correct employee
  7. Create new employee record if needed
  8. Click "Save and Finish"

File Size Limit: Maximum 5MB per document. Multi-page documents saved separately will not be processed by OCR.

Subtask: Upload from Employee Profile

  1. Navigate to Company > All Employees
  2. Select employee
  3. Go to Documents tab
  4. Click "Add Document"
  5. Select document type from dropdown
  6. Upload file
  7. Enter expiry date if applicable
  8. Add description (optional)
  9. Save

Expected Outcome: Documents appear in employee profile, company-wide document dashboard, and expiry tracking reports. Email reminders trigger based on configured frequency.

Task: Manage Employee Records

Create, update, and maintain comprehensive employee information across personal, work, and compliance data.

Subtask: Create Employee Record (Single)

  1. Navigate to Company > All Employees
  2. Click "Add Employee"
  3. Enter required fields:
    • First and last name (legal names)
    • Work email (or personal email if work email unavailable)
    • Hire date
    • Department and job title
    • Employment status (active/inactive)
  4. Add optional fields:
    • Phone numbers
    • Nationality and residence
    • Reporting manager
    • Trade license number, MOL ID, biometric ID
  5. Save employee record

Subtask: Bulk Upload Employee Records

  1. Navigate to Company > All Employees
  2. Click "Bulk Upload" or "Download Template"
  3. Download Excel template
  4. Fill employee data:
    • Use legal names as they appear in official documents
    • Use year-month-day date format
    • Select values from dropdown columns only
    • Paste values only (no formulas)
    • Do not add/delete rows or columns
  5. Remove sensitive data (passport numbers, visa details) to prevent upload errors
  6. Upload completed Excel file
  7. Review validation messages
  8. Download error report if upload fails
  9. Correct errors and re-upload

Backup Required: Download a backup of existing employee data before bulk upload. Previous records will be overwritten and cannot be retrieved.

Subtask: Update Employee Profile

  1. Navigate to Company > All Employees
  2. Search for employee using global search (CMD/CTRL+K) or filter list
  3. Click employee name to open profile
  4. Select tab to edit:
    • About Me: Personal info, contact details, identification documents
    • Work: Department, job title, work timings, office location, reporting manager
    • Documents: Upload/manage employee documents
    • Dependents: Add dependent records with documents
    • Payroll: Salary configuration, bank details, pay components
    • Assets: Assign company assets
  5. Click "Edit" in relevant section
  6. Update fields
  7. Save changes

Work Email Restriction: If employee has already registered on Bayzat, the work email field is locked. Contact Customer Support via chat to change registered email addresses.

Expected Outcome: Employee data syncs across all modules (payroll, leave, attendance, performance). Profile completion percentage updates. Missing mandatory fields trigger validation warnings.

Edge Cases

Document Management Edge Cases

  • Multi-page documents: Can be saved as single document (OCR processed) or split into separate pages (no OCR, manual data entry required)
  • Document type conflicts: If document type name exists in Arabic but you're creating in English, system may show duplicate error
  • Missing mandatory documents: System tracks mandatory documents per employee; missing count appears in dashboard widget
  • Expiry reminder timing: Default 7-month reminder may not suit all document types; adjust per document type as needed
  • OCR extraction errors: If OCR misreads data, manually verify and correct extracted information before saving

Employee Records Edge Cases

  • Incomplete profiles: Use "Incomplete" filter in Profile completion to identify employees missing data across categories
  • Deleted employee recovery: Profile can be retrieved but associated data may be partially lost; always backup before deletion
  • Duplicate email addresses: System prevents duplicate work emails; use personal email in work email field if official email unavailable
  • Employee status transitions: Mark employees as inactive with departure date and reason; they remain in system but excluded from active counts
  • Recently viewed limit: Only last 5 profiles shown; older views are not retained
  • Search result overflow: Only 5 matching searches shown before "Show more" option appears

End of Service Edge Cases

  • Non-AED currency: Employees paid in non-AED currencies receive zero gratuity calculation automatically
  • Future departure dates: Variable pay and work expenses not shown for future months; only current/past data visible
  • Pending work expenses: Must resolve all pending or approved work expenses before processing EOS
  • Custom gratuity calculations: When overriding MOL formula, admin must provide remark explaining custom calculation
  • Rehire scenarios: Rehire option only available if EOS has not been submitted
  • Salary configuration lock: Once departure date is set, salary configuration becomes non-editable

Custom Data Edge Cases

  • Overwritten data: Previous custom data records are overwritten during bulk updates and cannot be retrieved
  • Payroll-mapped fields: If custom company data is mapped to payroll templates, ensure updates are made before payroll processing
  • Blank vs conflicting values: During merge operations, if all custom data is identical it's retained; any conflict results in blank value

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I update employee document types to track expiry dates?

Navigate to Settings > Company > Employee Document Types, select the document type, enable expiry tracking, and configure email reminder frequency (1-12 months before expiry).

Can I customize document notification preferences?

Currently, the system sends monthly automated emails for document expiry reminders. Custom notification frequencies are not available beyond the configured reminder months.

How do I bulk upload employee records?

Go to Company > All Employees, download the Excel template, fill in employee data following the format rules, and upload the completed file. Do not add or delete rows/columns, and use only provided dropdown options.

What happens if I upload documents with the wrong employee email?

Delete the incorrect invite from the Invitations page, update the employee's work email in their Personal tab, then resend the invite. If the employee already registered, contact support via the chat feature.

Can I download all employee documents at once?

Bulk document download is not currently supported. Documents must be downloaded individually from each employee's profile.

How do I recover a deleted employee profile?

Profile recovery is partial—not all associated data can be restored. Contact support immediately to assess what data can be retrieved before re-creating the profile.

Why can't I add a specific city to work location?

Work locations are limited to officially recognized government locations. Project-based or emerging destinations without official city status cannot be added.

Can I modify End of Service calculations after submission?

Once an EOS transaction is submitted, it cannot be reopened through the UI. Contact support to delete and recalculate the transaction manually.

How do I track which employee has a specific bank account?

The platform lacks an automated IBAN lookup tool. Contact the development team for manual investigation to identify which employee profile is linked to a specific account number.

Can I use custom data fields in AI Reports?

Employee custom fields are not currently supported in AI Reports functionality. This limitation is technical and not in the near-term roadmap.
