What is Records Management?

Overview

Records Management is Bayzat's central system for storing and managing employee information across the entire employment lifecycle. Access it from Company → All employees to view, edit, and track employee profiles with 12 specialized tabs covering personal details, work information, documents, payroll, attendance, and performance.

Create new employee records, bulk upload via Excel, invite employees to self-register, track document expiry, manage company assets, and export employee data. The system supports list, card, and org chart views with configurable columns.

Key Benefits

  • Single source of truth for all employee data eliminates duplicate spreadsheets and manual tracking
  • Automated document expiry reminders prevent compliance lapses for visas, passports, and licenses
  • Profile completion tracking identifies missing data and enables bulk invitations to employees
  • 12-tab employee profiles integrate with payroll, leave, attendance, and performance systems
  • Bulk upload and export capabilities support large workforce onboarding and reporting
  • Company asset tracking links equipment to employees with status monitoring

Strategic Context

🎯 What Records Management Solves

Replaces fragmented employee data across spreadsheets, email attachments, and filing cabinets with a unified digital system.

  • Eliminates manual tracking of document expiry dates and renewal reminders
  • Standardizes employee data structure across departments and locations
  • Enables self-service employee profile updates and document uploads

💰 Why It Matters

Employee records are the foundation for payroll processing, leave management, attendance tracking, and compliance reporting.

  • Payroll requires accurate bank details, salary structures, and employment status
  • Leave policies reference hire dates, contract types, and work schedules
  • Attendance systems use office locations, work timings, and reporting hierarchies

🏗️ How It Connects

Records Management is transactional data that flows into every HR process and report.

  • Employee profiles are created once and referenced by all modules
  • Document types configured in Settings → Company apply to all employees
  • Changes to employee status (active/inactive) cascade to payroll, leave, and access permissions

Product Foundation Overview

How Records Management Fits

Records Management is transactional data — it stores individual employee information that changes over time as people join, update profiles, and leave the organization. Each employee record is a standalone entity containing personal details, work information, documents, and lifecycle events.

Mental model: Employee (record) → Contains profile data → Updated by admins and employees → Drives payroll, compliance, reporting

Every employee record impacts payroll calculations, leave balances, document compliance tracking, and organizational reporting.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before adding employees to Bayzat:

  • What employee data fields are mandatory for your organization? — Determines profile completion requirements and compliance readiness
  • Which document types need expiry tracking? — Configure custom document types with reminder frequencies before bulk upload
  • How will you manage bulk data entry? — Excel template vs. manual entry vs. API integration affects initial setup time
  • What employee permissions will you allow? — Decide whether employees can edit their own personal info, documents, and dependents

Related Features

These Bayzat features work together with Records Management. Where available, links point to the published user guide.

  • Payroll Management — Processes salary calculations using employee contract data, bank details, and work information from employee records
  • End of Service — Calculates final settlements using hire date, contract type, salary, and leave balances from employee records
  • Role Management — Controls which admins can view and edit employee records based on assigned permissions
  • Workflows — Automates actions when employee records are created, updated, or deleted (e.g., send welcome email, trigger onboarding tasks)
  • Leave Management — Assigns leave policies to employees and tracks leave balances stored in employee profiles
  • Attendance Tracking — Records check-in/check-out data linked to employee IDs and office locations
  • Health Insurance — Links insurance policies to employees and dependents stored in employee records
  • Performance Management — Tracks goals and reviews tied to employee profiles
  • Company Assets — Assigns laptops, phones, and equipment to specific employees

Initial Setup

Records Management requires minimal initial configuration. The feature is accessible immediately after company onboarding, with core employee record capabilities available by default.

Required Setup Steps

Complete these steps before adding employees:

1. Configure Company Information

Settings → Company

Set up foundational company details that populate employee records.

  • Add office locations (Settings → Company → Offices) — enables location-based employee assignment and attendance tracking
  • Define departments and job titles — required for employee work profiles
  • Configure employee document types (Settings → Company → Employee Document Types) — determines which documents are mandatory vs. optional, sets expiry tracking and reminder frequencies
  • Set up company logo — appears on payslips and letters

2. Configure Custom Data (Optional)

Settings → Custom Data

Add custom fields to capture organization-specific employee information.

  • Create custom employee fields for data not covered by standard profile sections
  • Custom fields appear in employee profiles and can be used in reports
  • Note: AI Reports do not currently support custom field data

3. Set Employee Permissions

Settings → Company → Employee Permissions

Control what employees can edit in their own profiles.

  • Configure whether employees can add/edit/delete their own documents
  • Set whether employees can edit personal information and work details
  • Define whether employees can manage dependent data
  • Note: Larger organizations often restrict editing to prevent data integrity issues

Recommended Setup Steps

These enhance the feature but are not blocking:

Configure Document Expiry Reminders

Settings → Company → Employee Document Types

Set up automated email reminders for expiring documents.

  • For each document type, enable expiry date tracking
  • Set reminder frequency (1-12 months before expiry)
  • Default reminder: 7 months before expiry for Passport, Residency Visa, Emirates ID
  • System sends automated reminders to stakeholders when documents approach expiration

Set Up Company Bank Details

Settings → Payroll → Bank Accounts

Add company bank accounts for payroll processing.

  • Required if using Bayzat for payroll
  • Can add multiple bank accounts if needed
  • Associate each account with the relevant Trade License

Configure Company Assets

Set up asset tracking if distributing equipment to employees.

  • Navigate to the employee profile to assign assets
  • Track asset status: working, damaged, or in-repair
  • Record purchase dates, assignment dates, and office locations

Verification Steps

Confirm setup is complete:

  1. Navigate to Company → All employees — employee list should load with configured columns
  2. Click Add Employee — form should show configured departments, offices, and job titles in dropdowns
  3. Check Settings → Company → Employee Document Types — mandatory documents should be marked, expiry reminders configured
  4. Test employee invitation flow — invite should include company logo and correct email template

Next step: You can now add employees manually, via Excel bulk upload, or by sending invitations. See the Core Tasks section for detailed instructions.

Workflow Integration

Real-World Workflow Examples

Records Management triggers 6 employee-related workflow events. These workflows automate actions when employee data changes:

Employee Created Workflow (Active)

  • Trigger: New employee record is created in the system
  • Condition: IF employee has a work email address
  • Action: Send welcome email with platform invitation link
  • Result: New hires receive access credentials automatically without HR manually sending invites

Employee Updated Workflow (Active)

  • Trigger: Employee profile data is modified
  • Condition: IF department or manager field changes
  • Action: Notify new manager and update reporting hierarchy
  • Result: Organizational changes propagate to approval flows and leave policies without manual updates

Hire Date Triggered Workflow (Active)

  • Trigger: Employee hire date arrives
  • Condition: IF employee status is Active
  • Action: Assign default leave policies and create payroll record
  • Result: Leave balances and payroll configuration initialize automatically on day one

Probation End Date Workflow (Active)

  • Trigger: Employee probation end date is reached
  • Condition: IF employee contract type is Limited or Unlimited
  • Action: Update leave entitlements to full-time allocation and notify HR
  • Result: Leave balances adjust automatically when probation completes without manual intervention

Salary Updated Workflow (Active)

  • Trigger: Employee salary amount changes in payroll tab
  • Condition: IF salary increase exceeds 10%
  • Action: Create approval request for Finance Manager review
  • Result: Significant salary changes require oversight before processing in next payroll cycle

Scenarios Solved by Workflows

Scenario Without Workflow With Workflow
New employee joins with missing documents HR manually tracks missing documents in spreadsheet and sends reminder emails System sends automated reminders to employee when mandatory documents are not uploaded within 7 days
Employee document expires (passport, visa, Emirates ID) HR checks document expiry dates manually and contacts employees individually System sends email reminders 7 months before expiry to employee and manager automatically
Employee transfers to new department HR manually updates leave policies, approval flows, and notifies new manager System reassigns policies based on new department rules and notifies stakeholders automatically
Bulk employee data import from Excel HR imports data, then manually sends invitations to each employee one by one System detects new employees with work emails and sends invitation batch automatically after import completes

Opportunity 2: When employee work email changes, automatically update email in all connected systems (payroll, benefits, insurance) and send confirmation to old and new addresses.

Opportunity 3: When employee dependent is added, automatically check if health insurance policy allows dependents and create insurance enrollment request if eligible.

Approval Flow

How Records Management Requests Route

Employee profile changes can trigger approval flows when configured in Settings. Document edit/delete requests and payment details changes require approval routing to prevent unauthorized data modifications. Default flows route to Super Admin or Line Manager based on request type and company configuration.

Types Requiring Approval

Records Management supports approval flows for these employee data change requests:

  • Document Changes: When employees request to edit or delete uploaded documents (passport, visa, Emirates ID, contracts). Default approver: Line Manager or Super Admin based on document type.
  • Payment Details: When employees update bank account information or IBAN. Default approver: Finance Manager or Super Admin to prevent fraudulent changes.
  • Personal Information: When employee edit permissions are restricted and employees request changes to address, emergency contact, or dependent data. Default approver: HR Admin or Line Manager.
  • Work Information: When employees request changes to department, job title, or reporting manager. Default approver: Line Manager first, then HR Admin for final approval.

46+ approval flow types are available across the platform. Records Management-specific flows are configured in Settings > Company > Employee Permissions.

Routing Examples

How different Records Management change requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Employee submits request to delete expired passport document → Routes to Line Manager for single-step approval → Manager approves or rejects with comment.

Example 2: Employee updates bank account IBAN in payroll tab → Routes to Finance Manager first → IF approved, escalates to Super Admin for final verification before payroll processing.

Example 3: Employee requests to edit personal address when permissions are restricted → Routes to HR Admin for approval → IF employee is in probation period, requires additional Super Admin approval.

Example 4: Employee submits dependent document for health insurance enrollment → Routes to Line Manager for verification → IF dependent age exceeds policy limit, escalates to HR Admin for exception review.

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

Data Import & Validation

WOF-001: Bulk Upload Template Limitations (HIGH IMPACT)

The Excel bulk upload template does not support job type/employment type fields. Comprehensive validation is not performed during upload, which can lead to incomplete employee records.

Workaround: Manually update job type and employment type fields after bulk upload via individual employee profiles.

Template does not support job type/employment type Fields must be populated manually after upload Bulk upload only
Limited validation during Excel upload Invalid data may be accepted without immediate error All bulk imports
Sensitive data fields (passport, visa) cause upload errors Remove these fields from template before upload Bulk upload only
Date format must be year-month-day Incorrect formats cause validation errors All date fields

End of Service Calculation Rules

WOF-002: EOS Framework Gaps (HIGH IMPACT — BY DESIGN)

Once an EOS settlement is finalized, it cannot be self-service reopened. Strict date validation prevents backdated settlements before the first day of the previous closed payroll period. Gratuity fields become non-editable when the calculated amount is zero.

Design Rationale: Prevents payroll integrity issues and ensures compliance with labor law calculation requirements.

Workaround: Contact Bayzat support to reopen finalized settlements. For backdated EOS, reopen the affected payroll period first.

Rule Constraint Applies To
Finalized settlements cannot be reopened Requires backend support intervention to delete and recalculate All EOS transactions
Departure date validation Cannot be before first day of previous closed payroll period All EOS calculations
Gratuity fields locked when zero Cannot edit amount or add comments when gratuity calculates to zero Non-AED employees, limited contracts
Variable pay not shown for future dates Cannot predict future variable compensation Future departure dates only
Work expenses not shown for future dates Cannot predict future reimbursements Future departure dates only
Gratuity is zero for non-AED employees MOL formula only applies to AED-paid employees Non-AED currency employees
Salary configuration locked after departure date set Cannot modify base salary components once EOS initiated All employees with departure date

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bulk upload employee records?

Yes. Download the Excel template from Company > All Employees, fill in employee data (avoid adding/deleting rows or columns), and upload. Job type/employment type fields are not supported in bulk upload—configure these individually after import.

How do I fix validation errors during bulk upload?

Download the error report generated after a failed upload. Correct the flagged rows in your Excel file (check date formats, required fields, and dropdown values), then re-upload. Remove sensitive data like passport numbers or visa details if causing errors.

Why can't I change an employee's work email after they've registered?

Work email is locked after registration to maintain account security. Contact Bayzat Support via the chat feature to request an email change for registered employees.

How do I handle employees with incomplete profiles?

Use the Filters panel to select "Incomplete" under Profile Completion. This shows all employees missing required information. Click each employee to view their profile completion percentage and fill in missing fields across the 12 profile tabs.

Can I download all employee documents at once?

No. The platform does not support bulk document downloads. Documents must be downloaded individually from each employee's Documents tab or from Company > Employee Documents.

What happens to employee records after offboarding?

Employees removed from payroll after EOS submission remain in the system with "Inactive" status. Their records are preserved for compliance and reporting but no longer appear in active employee lists by default.

How do I track document expiry dates?

Navigate to Company > Employee Documents to view expiring/expired documents. The system tracks documents expiring in the present month and next 3 months. Configure email reminders (1-12 months before expiry) in Settings > Company > Employee Document Types.

Can I customize which columns appear in the employee list?

Yes. Click the Column Selector icon in the top-right of the employee list. Select/deselect from 12 available columns including Employee ID, Department, Role, Profile Completion, Hire Date, and more.

Why can't I delete an employee record?

Employee deletion is not available from the list view. Offboarding is handled through the End of Service workflow on individual employee profiles (Payroll tab). This preserves audit trails and compliance records.

How do I resend an invitation sent to the wrong email?

Go to Company > Invitations, delete the incorrect invite using the dustbin icon, update the employee's work email in their Personal tab, then resend the invite from Company > All Employees. If the employee already registered, contact Support to change the email.

What's the difference between Card, List, and Org Chart views?

List view shows tabular employee data with sortable columns. Card view displays employees as visual cards with photos and key details. Org Chart view maps reporting relationships—requires at least one employee with a "Reports to" field filled to display.

Can I add custom fields to employee records?

Yes. Navigate to Settings > Custom Data to create custom fields for Employee, Company, or Asset categories. Custom fields appear in employee profiles and can be used for filtering and reporting (note: AI Reports does not currently support custom fields).
