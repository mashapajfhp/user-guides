### Feature Description

<span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">End of Service is a feature that calculates and settles the final payment for employees when they leave the company, including gratuity, leave encashments, and other financial components according to UAE labor law.</span> This feature ensures accurate, compliant calculations for all employee departures, whether due to resignation, termination, or contract completion.

### Who Uses This Feature?

| User Role | What They Do | Value Proposition |
|----|----|----|
| **HR Administrators** | Initiate and manage employee departures, coordinate offboarding processes, and ensure proper documentation | Process employee exits with confidence knowing all legal entitlements are calculated correctly‚Äîeliminating compliance risks and potential labor disputes |
| **Payroll Managers** | Calculate final settlements including gratuity, leave encashments, and deductions | Generate accurate, UAE labor law-compliant settlements in minutes‚Äîrather than spending hours on manual calculations and cross-referencing regulations |
| **Finance Teams** | Review settlement amounts and process final payments to departing employees | Receive fully itemized, audit-ready settlement breakdowns‚Äîwithout back-and-forth verification with HR or manual reconciliation |
| **Departing Employees** | Receive transparent breakdown of their final settlement and entitlements | Understand exactly how your final payment was calculated‚Äîbuilding trust through transparency and reducing disputes at separation |

### Key Benefits

- Automated calculation of gratuity based on UAE labor law requirements
- Accurate leave encashment calculations integrated with time-off data
- Comprehensive settlement breakdown including additions and deductions
- Compliance with MOHRE regulations for all departure scenarios
- Printable settlement documents for employee records
- Support for both limited and unlimited contract types

### How End of Service Fits

End of Service is a **transaction calculator** that computes final settlement amounts when employees depart. It pulls data from payroll, leave records, and contract information to generate compliant settlement documents.

<div class="info-box">

**Mental model:** Departure Trigger ‚Üí Gratuity + Leave Encashment + Deductions ‚Üí Settlement Document ‚Üí Payroll Processing

</div>

Calculations follow UAE labor law requirements with gratuity caps and formulas based on years of service and contract type.

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before processing end of service:

- **Contract type?** ‚Äî Unlimited or Limited affects gratuity formula
- **Departure reason?** ‚Äî Termination, Resignation, or End of Contract
- **Leave balance?** ‚Äî Verify accrued leave for encashment calculation
- **Deductions needed?** ‚Äî Any outstanding loans or advances to deduct?

</div>

<div class="subsection">

### Related Features

- **Payroll Module** ‚Äî Employee salary data and transaction processing
- **Employee Profile** ‚Äî Contract details and employment history
- **Time-off Management** ‚Äî Leave balances for encashment calculation

- **Gratuity Liability Report** ‚Äî Organization-wide gratuity projections

## Feature Discovery

### How to Access

<span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">The End of Service feature is accessed by navigating to Finance \> Payroll table, selecting an employee, and then clicking the End of Service tab within the employee profile</span>.

## Setup Process

### Initial Configuration

Before processing End of Service calculations, ensure the following are configured in your Bayzat system:

- Leave policy settings for leave encashment calculations
- Employee contract types (Unlimited or Limited) are correctly assigned
- Payroll currency is set to AED for gratuity-eligible employees
- Basic salary is accurately recorded in the Active Payroll record

### Leave Encashment Settings Configuration

<span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-3939">Leave encashment calculations can be configured to use different daily wage calculation methods. ### Understanding Gratuity Calculations

<span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-3123">The system provides calculation transparency through help text and expandable breakdown dialogs that explain the gratuity methodology</span>. Key calculation formulas:

<div class="feature-card">

#### Service Duration Calculation

<span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-4496">Total Service Duration = Gross Service Duration - Unpaid Leave Days</span>

The system displays both the gross service duration (from hire date to departure date) and separately lists any unpaid leave deductions to show the net service period used for gratuity calculation.

</div>

<div class="feature-card">

#### Daily Wage Calculation

<span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-2605">Daily Wage = (Basic Salary + Allowances) / 30</span>

This formula is used for calculating both gratuity and leave encashment. The calculation breakdown shows this formula to ensure transparency in how amounts are derived.

</div>

<div class="feature-card">

#### Gratuity Formula (Service \< 5 Years)

Gratuity = (Basic Salary √∑ 30) √ó 21 √ó Years of Service

For employees with less than 5 years of continuous service, 21 days of basic salary per year of service.

</div>

<div class="feature-card">

#### Gratuity Formula (Service ‚â• 5 Years)

Gratuity = \[(Basic Salary √∑ 30) √ó 21 √ó 5\] + \[(Basic Salary √∑ 30) √ó 30 √ó (Years - 5)\]

For employees with 5 or more years of service: 21 days per year for the first 5 years + 30 days per year for each additional year. Maximum cap: 2 years of basic salary.

</div>

<div class="feature-card">

#### Pro-rated Monthly Pay

<span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-2605">Pro-rated Pay = (Basic Salary + Allowances) / Days in Month √ó Days Worked</span>

Monthly pay components are pro-rated based on the number of days worked in the departure month, with consistent decimal precision to avoid rounding errors.

</div>

### Best Practices

- Always verify the hiring date is correct before calculating End of Service. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-4206">Note that the hiring date field is locked once payroll transactions exist</span>
- Double-check the departure date to ensure accurate service duration calculation
- Review gratuity calculations carefully, especially for employees with over 5 years of service. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-3123">Use the gratuity breakdown dialog to verify the calculation methodology</span>
- Ensure all leave balances are finalized before processing End of Service. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-2958">Verify that the leave calculation method is configured correctly for your organization</span>
- Document any additions or deductions with clear reference numbers and notes
- Review the final settlement amount with the employee before submission when possible. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1099">Verify that amounts match across the EOS tab, transaction page, and payroll table</span>
- Keep printed settlement documents for compliance and audit purposes. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1887">Downloaded gratuity files include clear month identification for proper record-keeping</span>
- For employees outside the UAE, confirm that the system correctly shows zero gratuity per UAE law. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1026">Currency settings persist during offboarding to ensure consistency</span>
- Wait until the payroll period is closed before attempting to submit the final EOS transaction

## Business Rules & Limitations

### Core Business Rules

<span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">End of Service calculations follow specific rules based on employee contract type, departure reason, and UAE labor law requirements</span>.

| Business Rule | Details | Impact |
|----|----|----|
| Gratuity Eligibility | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Minimum 1 year of continuous service required for gratuity eligibility</span> | Employees with less than 1 year service receive no gratuity |
| Non-AED Currency Rule | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Non-AED employees receive 0 gratuity per UAE law</span> | Only AED-based employees are eligible for gratuity calculation |
| Gratuity Calculation Formula | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Different formulas apply for service periods under 5 years versus over 5 years of service</span> | Calculation method changes based on tenure duration |
| Gratuity Maximum Cap | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Gratuity capped at maximum of two years' basic salary</span> | Calculated gratuity cannot exceed two years of basic salary |
| Contract Type Handling | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Different handling for limited and unlimited contract types</span> | Contract type selection affects calculation methodology |
| Variable Pay Visibility | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Variable pay and work expenses not shown for future departure dates</span> | Future-dated calculations exclude variable compensation components |
| Unpaid Leave Exclusion | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Unpaid leave days are excluded from service calculation</span>. <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-3842">Leave policies can be configured to exclude unpaid leave from service duration, and the calculation breakdown shows the deduction separately</span> | Service duration is reduced by any unpaid leave periods |
| Departure Reason Options | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Valid reasons include Termination, Resignation, or End of contract</span> | Departure reason must be selected from predefined list |
| Payroll Period Closure | Final EOS transaction submission is only available after the payroll period containing the departure date is closed | Prevents premature submission and ensures all payroll data is finalized before settlement |
| Hiring Date Lock | <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-4206">Hiring date field becomes read-only and locked when employee has payroll transaction history</span> | Prevents modification of hiring date that would invalidate historical payroll calculations |
| EOS Tab Visibility | <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1377">EOS tab is visible for active employees and disabled/hidden for offboarded employees</span> | Prevents changes to completed settlements after employee offboarding |
| Calculation Transparency | <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-3123">Gratuity calculation methodology is accessible through help text and breakdown dialogs, with editable fields for manual adjustments</span> | Allows users to understand and verify calculation logic |

### System Constraints

- <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Variable pay and work expenses are not predictable for future months, limiting accuracy of forward-looking calculations</span>
- Gratuity calculations are based solely on basic salary, not gross salary or allowances
- The system requires manual entry of departure date, reason, and contract type‚Äîthese cannot be auto-populated from other modules
- Leave encashment calculations depend on accurate leave balance data in the system
- Historical data for past departures may not be available for recalculation or audit purposes

### Compliance Requirements

All End of Service calculations must comply with UAE labor law as administered by the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE). Key compliance points include:

- <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Calculations must follow MOHRE-mandated formulas based on years of service and basic salary</span>
- <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1026">Gratuity must be calculated and paid in AED currency per UAE labor law (not KSA or other GCC regulations)</span>
- Final settlement documents should be retained for audit and compliance verification
- All deductions and additions must be documented with clear justification
- <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1099">Settlement amounts must be consistent across all system interfaces (EOS tab, transaction page, payroll table)</span>

## Troubleshooting & Edge Cases

### Common Issues

| Issue | Cause | Resolution |
|----|----|----|
| Gratuity amount shows as zero | Employee is non-AED currency or has less than 1 year service | Verify employee currency setting and service duration. Non-AED employees are not eligible for gratuity under UAE law. |
| Leave encashment not calculating | Leave balance data may be missing or inaccurate in the system | Check employee leave balances in the Time-Off module before calculating End of Service. Ensure leave policies are correctly configured. |
| Variable pay not appearing in calculation | <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Variable pay is not shown for future departure dates</span> | If calculating for a future date, variable pay components will not be included. Use actual departure date for complete calculation. |
| Gratuity calculation differs from expected amount | Contract type or service duration may be incorrectly selected | Verify contract type (Unlimited vs Limited) and confirm total service duration. Review calculation formula for the applicable service period. |
| Cannot modify gratuity or leave amounts | Fields may be locked after initial calculation | Check if the settlement has been submitted. You may need to create a new calculation to make changes. |
| Deductions not saving | Deduction type or amount may be invalid | Verify deduction details are complete with reference number and notes. Ensure amount does not exceed total settlement. |
| Cannot submit EOS transaction | Payroll period containing departure date is not yet closed | Wait until the payroll cycle is closed. System will display message: "Submitting this transaction is available once the payroll period \[dates\] is closed" |
| Hiring date field is locked and cannot be modified | <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-4206">Hiring date becomes read-only when employee has existing payroll transaction history</span> | This is by design to maintain payroll data integrity. If the hiring date is incorrect, contact support to resolve the issue before processing EOS. |
| EOS tab not visible or disabled | <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-1377">Employee may already be offboarded, or you may lack necessary permissions</span> | Verify employee status in the system. EOS tab is automatically disabled after offboarding to prevent changes to completed settlements. |
| Calculation breakdown not showing details | <span class="citation" source="jira" ref="TSSD-2605">Daily wage calculation details may not be immediately visible</span> | Look for the breakdown dialog or expandable section showing the formula: (basic + allowances) / 30. Click the gratuity amount to view the detailed calculation breakdown. |

### Edge Cases

<div class="info-box warning">

**Important:** <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">Gratuity can be edited and leave encashments can be modified after initial calculation</span>. Always review these adjustments carefully before final submission.

</div>

The following scenarios require special attention:

- **Leap Year Calculations:** <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Leap years require calculation using 366 days instead of 365</span>. Ensure the system correctly accounts for leap years in service duration calculations.
- **Partial Year Service:** Employees with less than 12 months of service are not eligible for gratuity. The system should display zero gratuity for these cases.
- **Multiple Contract Changes:** If an employee has had both limited and unlimited contracts, clarify which contract type applies at the time of departure.
- **Unpaid Leave Periods:** <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="14243037048465">Unpaid leave days are excluded from service calculation</span>. Verify any unpaid leave is properly deducted from total service duration.
- **Recurring Deductions:** <span class="citation" source="zendesk" ref="18272729992977">The system allows ability to add one-off or recurring deductions</span>. Ensure recurring deductions are not duplicated if the employee has already departed.
- **Currency Conversion:** Non-AED employees will show zero gratuity. Confirm this is the intended behavior per UAE labor law before finalizing settlement.

## Support Resources

### Frequently Asked Questions

<div class="faq-accordion">

How is gratuity calculated under UAE labor law?

<div class="faq-answer">

For unlimited contracts: 21 days of basic salary per year for the first 5 years, then 30 days per year thereafter. For limited contracts, different rules apply based on reason for departure. The system automatically applies the correct formula based on contract type and departure reason.

</div>

What's included in the end-of-service settlement?

<div class="faq-answer">

The settlement includes: gratuity payment, leave encashment for unused vacation days, any pending salary, and deductions for outstanding loans or advances. Each component is itemized in the settlement breakdown.

</div>

Can I calculate gratuity for an employee who hasn't left yet?

<div class="faq-answer">

Yes. Use the Gratuity Liability Report to calculate projected gratuity for all active employees. This helps with financial planning and provisioning without processing an actual departure.

</div>

Why is the leave encashment amount different from expected?

<div class="faq-answer">

Leave encashment uses the Daily Wage Calculator settings. Check Settings ‚Üí Payroll ‚Üí Daily Wage Calculator to verify which salary component and days calculation method is configured for end-of-service leave encashment.

</div>

How do I add deductions to the final settlement?

<div class="faq-answer">

During the EOS calculation process, you can add manual deductions such as outstanding loans, advances, or other amounts owed. These are subtracted from the final settlement total before payment.

</div>

## Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|----|----|
| **Basic Salary** | The fixed monthly salary component used as the basis for gratuity calculation under UAE labor law. Excludes allowances and variable pay. |
| **Calendar Days** | The total number of days in a calendar year used for service duration calculations. Standard year = 365 days; leap year = 366 days. |
| **Contract Type** | Classification of employment contract as either Unlimited (open-ended) or Limited (fixed-term). Affects gratuity calculation methodology. |
| **Daily Rate** | The daily wage calculated by dividing the monthly basic salary by 30. Used as the basis for gratuity and leave encashment calculations. |
| **Departure Date** | The last working day or final date of employment for an employee. Used to calculate total service duration and gratuity eligibility. |
| **Departure Reason** | The classification of why an employee is leaving the company. Valid options include Termination, Resignation, or End of contract. |
| **End of Service (EOS)** | The process of calculating and settling all financial obligations to an employee upon their departure from the company, including gratuity, leave encashment, and deductions. |
| **Gratuity** | A mandatory end-of-service benefit payable to employees in the UAE based on years of service and basic salary, calculated according to MOHRE labor law. |
| **Gratuity Liability** | The total amount of gratuity owed to all current employees, calculated as of a specific date. Used for financial planning and compliance reporting. |
| **Leave Encashment** | The monetary value of unused annual leave days paid to an employee upon departure, calculated at the daily rate. |
| **MOHRE** | Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation. The UAE government authority that establishes labor law requirements, including gratuity calculation rules. |
| **Service Duration** | The total length of continuous employment for an employee, calculated from hire date to departure date. Excludes unpaid leave periods. |
| **Settlement Document** | The final printed or digital record of an End of Service calculation showing gratuity, leave encashment, additions, deductions, and total amount due to the employee. |
| **Unpaid Leave** | Days of absence without pay that are excluded from service duration calculations for gratuity eligibility purposes. |
| **Variable Pay** | Compensation components that fluctuate month-to-month (e.g., commissions, bonuses). Not included in gratuity calculations for future departure dates. |
