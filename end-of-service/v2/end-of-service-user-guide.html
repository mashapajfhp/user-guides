End of Service

Calculate and settle final payments for departing employees in compliance with UAE labor law

What is End of Service?

Feature Description

End of Service is designed to calculate and settle the final payment for employees when they leave the company, including gratuity, leave encashments, and other financial components according to UAE labor law. This feature ensures accurate, compliant calculations for all employee departures, whether due to termination, resignation, or contract completion.

Who Uses This Feature?

HR Administrators or Payroll Managers who manage employee departures and final settlements use the End of Service feature to process employee exits and ensure all financial obligations are met correctly.

Key Benefits

  • Automated calculation of gratuity based on UAE labor law requirements
  • Accurate leave encashment calculations integrated with time-off data
  • Comprehensive settlement breakdown including additions and deductions
  • Compliance with MOHRE regulations for all employee departure types
  • Printable settlement documents for record-keeping and employee communication
  • Support for both unlimited and limited contract types

Product Foundation Overview

Business Problem Solved

Manual end-of-service calculations are time-consuming, error-prone, and difficult to keep compliant with changing UAE labor laws. Organizations need a reliable system to calculate gratuity, leave encashments, and final settlements accurately for every departing employee.

Key Benefits & Impact

Reduces payroll processing time, eliminates calculation errors, ensures legal compliance, provides transparent settlement breakdowns to employees, and generates audit-ready documentation. HR teams can process departures confidently without manual verification.

How It Works

Navigate to Finance > Payroll table, select an employee, access their profile, click End of Service, enter departure date and reason, choose contract type, and the system calculates the settlement amount with gratuity, leave encashments, and other components.

Core Concepts

End of Service operates on several foundational principles:

  • Gratuity is calculated based on years of continuous service and basic salary, in compliance with the new UAE labor law
  • Non-AED employees receive zero gratuity
  • Different handling applies for limited and unlimited contract types
  • Leave encashments are calculated from employee time-off records
  • Final settlement includes optional additions and deductions

Related Features

End of Service interacts with the Finance module, pulls data from the Payroll table, uses employee profile information, and connects with time-off data for leave encashment calculations. Understanding these integrations helps ensure complete and accurate settlements.

  6. Review the gratuity calculation (different formula applies for limited contracts)
  7. Review leave encashments
  8. Process any additional payments or deductions
  9. Submit the transaction

Usage Examples

Example 1: Calculating Gratuity for a UAE-Based Employee

For an employee with 7 years of continuous service and a basic salary of AED 5,000, the gratuity is calculated as follows: Daily rate = AED 5,000 ÷ 30 = AED 166.67. For service over 5 years, the formula applies: (21 days × daily rate × 5 years) + (30 days × daily rate × 2 years) = AED 17,500 + AED 10,000 = AED 27,500. The maximum gratuity is capped at two years' basic salary (AED 10,000), so the final gratuity amount would be AED 10,000.

Example 2: Non-AED Employee Gratuity Handling

For employees residing outside the United Arab Emirates, the system displays an alert notification stating they are not owed gratuity, demonstrating location-aware content. The End of Service calculation will show zero gratuity for these employees.

Example 3: Leave Encashment Calculation

An employee has 15 unused leave days at the time of departure. The system calculates the leave encashment based on the employee's daily salary rate and the number of unused days. This amount is added to the final settlement.

Business Rules & Limitations

Core Business Rules

End of Service calculations are governed by specific rules and constraints that ensure compliance with UAE labor law:

Rule Description Impact
Gratuity Calculation per MOHRE Law Gratuity is calculated based on years of continuous service and basic salary, with different formulas for service periods under and over 5 years Ensures legal compliance with UAE labor regulations
Minimum Service Requirement Minimum 1 year of continuous service is required for gratuity eligibility Employees with less than 1 year receive no gratuity
Non-AED Currency Handling Non-AED employees receive 0 gratuity Only AED-based salaries qualify for gratuity calculation
Gratuity Cap Maximum gratuity is capped at two years' basic salary Limits maximum payout regardless of service length
Basic Salary Only Gratuity calculation is based solely on basic salary, not gross salary Allowances and variable pay are excluded from calculation
Unpaid Leave Exclusion Unpaid leave days are excluded from service calculation Reduces effective service period and gratuity amount
Contract Type Handling Different handling for limited and unlimited contract types Calculation methodology varies based on contract classification
Departure Reason Documentation Departure reason must be selected (Termination, Resignation, or End of contract) Required for accurate record-keeping and compliance

System Constraints

Variable pay and work expenses are not shown for future departure dates. This limitation affects:

  • Inability to predict variable compensation components for future months
  • Requirement to use historical data for calculations on future departure dates
  • Need for manual adjustment if variable pay is expected to change

Compliance Requirements

End of Service calculations must comply with:

  • UAE Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE) labor law requirements
  • Current gratuity calculation formulas and caps
  • Employee contract terms and conditions
  • Company payroll and financial policies
  • Data protection and privacy regulations

Edge Cases

Important: Gratuity can be edited and leave encashments can be modified. Always review calculated amounts before final submission to ensure accuracy.

Leap Year Calculations: Leap years require calculation using 366 days instead of 365. The system automatically accounts for this when calculating daily rates and service periods.

Employees with Less Than 1 Year Service: No gratuity is payable for employees with less than 1 year of continuous service. However, they may still be entitled to leave encashment and final monthly pay.

Multiple Departures in Same Month: Process each employee's End of Service separately to ensure accurate individual calculations. Use the Gratuity File feature to verify total liability after processing multiple departures.

Contract Type Changes During Employment: Different handling for limited and unlimited contract types. If an employee's contract type changed, use the contract type that was active at the time of departure.

Unpaid Leave Periods: Unpaid leave days are excluded from service calculation. Ensure unpaid leave records are accurate in the system before calculating gratuity.

Resolution Steps

  1. Identify the specific issue from the Common Issues table above
  2. Verify all employee data is current (contract type, basic salary, leave balance)
  3. Review the applicable business rule or constraint that may be affecting the calculation
  4. Make necessary corrections to employee records or calculation inputs
  5. Recalculate End of Service amount
  6. Review all components (gratuity, leave encashment, additions, deductions) for accuracy
  7. If issue persists, contact Bayzat support with employee details and calculation screenshot

Glossary of Terms

Term Definition
Additions Optional extra payments or benefits added to the End of Service settlement, such as bonuses, outstanding allowances, or other financial components not automatically calculated.
Basic Salary The fixed monthly salary component used as the basis for gratuity calculation, excluding allowances, bonuses, and variable pay.
Continuous Service Uninterrupted employment period with the same employer, used to determine gratuity eligibility and calculation amount. Unpaid leave periods are excluded.
Daily Rate The calculated daily wage derived by dividing the monthly basic salary by 30, used in gratuity and leave encashment calculations.
Deductions Amounts subtracted from the End of Service settlement, such as outstanding loans, advances, or other financial obligations owed by the employee.
End of Service (EOS) The process of calculating and settling all final payments owed to an employee when they leave the company, including gratuity, leave encashment, and other financial components.
Gratuity A mandatory end-of-service benefit payable to employees in the UAE based on years of service and basic salary, calculated according to MOHRE labor law with a maximum cap of two years' salary.
Gratuity Liability The total financial obligation a company has to pay gratuity to all current employees if they were to leave on a specific date, used for financial planning and reporting.
Leave Encashment The monetary value of unused annual leave days paid to the employee at the time of departure, calculated based on daily rate and remaining leave balance.
Limited Contract An employment contract with a fixed end date, subject to specific gratuity calculation rules under UAE labor law.
MOHRE Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation - The UAE government authority responsible for labor law and employment regulations.
Unlimited Contract An employment contract with no fixed end date, subject to different gratuity calculation rules compared to limited contracts under UAE labor law.
Unpaid Leave Leave periods taken without pay, which are excluded from the continuous service calculation and reduce the effective service period for gratuity purposes.
Variable Pay Compensation components that vary month-to-month, such as commissions or bonuses, which are excluded from gratuity calculation and may not be available for future departure dates.
