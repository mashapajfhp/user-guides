Feature Overview

What Knowledge Hub Does

Knowledge Hub is Bayzat's centralized knowledge management platform that transforms how organizations store, organize, and access company information. It replaces scattered documents and inefficient information retrieval with an AI-powered, hierarchical content system.

Who Uses Knowledge Hub

    • HR Administrators: Create and maintain company policies, procedures, and resources
    • Knowledge Hub Managers: Organize content structure and manage permissions across spaces
    • All Employees: Search for information, access resources, and stay updated on company knowledge
    • Department Heads: Share department-specific documentation with controlled access

    Key Capabilities

    • Hierarchical Organization: Structure content using Spaces (top-level categories), Folders (sub-categories), and Pages (detailed content)
    • AI-Powered Discovery: Bayzat AI chatbot provides two modes: 'Chat with documents' for searching the knowledge base and 'Writing Assistant' for content creation
    • Flexible Permissions: Control access at space level with three permission tiers: 'Only me and admins', 'All employees can view', and 'Custom' permissions
    • Multi-Format Support: Upload files, create rich-text pages with formatting, and embed cross-links between documents
    • Activity Monitoring: Track content changes with filterable activity feed showing edits, uploads, and creations
    • Smart Recovery: 30-day trash retention system allows restoration of accidentally deleted content

    Business Value

    Knowledge Hub eliminates the productivity drain of scattered information and manual document searches. Organizations report:

    • Reduced time spent searching for information from hours to seconds
    • Improved onboarding efficiency with centralized policy access
    • Enhanced compliance through version-controlled document management
    • Better knowledge retention when employees leave the organization
    • Increased transparency with permission-based information sharing
Knowledge Hub provides instant information retrieval through AI chatbot responses to natural language queries, creating a centralized knowledge repository that improves organizational communication and efficiency.

User Journey

The Knowledge Hub user journey follows a structured workflow for managing organizational knowledge through Spaces, Folders, and Pages. This end-to-end process enables administrators and knowledge managers to create, organize, share, and maintain company information effectively.

Stage 1: Initial Access and Planning

Users begin by accessing the Knowledge Hub from the Company menu in the Bayzat platform. At this stage, administrators assess what information needs to be centralized and plan the organizational structure.

Key Decisions:

    • What categories of information need dedicated Spaces?
    • Who should have access to different types of content?
    • How should content be organized for easy discovery?

    Stage 2: Space Creation and Organization

    Administrators create Spaces as top-level containers for related content. Each Space represents a major category of organizational knowledge (e.g., HR Policies, IT Resources, Company Handbooks).

    Expected Outcomes:

    • Spaces created with descriptive names and clear purposes
    • Initial permission settings configured (Private to you, Editor, or Custom permissions)
    • Logical structure established for content organization

    Stage 3: Content Population

    Once Spaces are established, users populate them with Folders, Pages, and Files. This stage involves uploading documents, creating informational pages, and organizing content hierarchically.

    Typical Use Case Scenario:

    An HR administrator creates a "Company Policies" Space, then adds folders for "Leave Policies," "Code of Conduct," and "Benefits Information." Within each folder, they create detailed pages explaining specific policies and upload supporting PDF documents.

    Stage 4: Permission Configuration and Sharing

    Administrators configure access permissions at the Space level, determining who can view and edit content. Permissions automatically inherit to folders and pages within the Space.

    Decision Points:

    • General Access Level: Choose between "Only me and admins," "All employees can view," or "Custom" permissions
    • Individual Permissions: Assign Editor, View only, or No access to specific users
    • Sharing Method: Share via Copy Link or publish on Newsfeed

    Stage 5: Content Discovery and Usage

    Employees access shared content through multiple pathways: direct links, Newsfeed posts, search functionality, or the Bayzat AI chatbot. The Overview page provides quick access to favorite spaces, recent files, and activity feeds.

    Expected Outcomes:

    • Employees can quickly locate needed information
    • AI chatbot provides instant answers to natural language queries
    • Activity feed keeps users informed of recent updates
    • Favorite spaces and pages enable quick access to frequently used content

    Stage 6: Ongoing Maintenance and Updates

    Knowledge Hub content requires regular review and updates to remain accurate and relevant. Administrators edit existing pages, upload new documents, and remove outdated content.

    Maintenance Workflow:

    1. Review content for accuracy and relevance
    2. Update pages with new information
    3. Remove or archive outdated documents
    4. Monitor activity feed for user engagement
    5. Respond to employee feedback and questions

    Stage 7: Content Lifecycle Management

    When content is no longer needed, administrators can delete Spaces, folders, or pages. Deleted items move to the Trash with a 30-day retention period, allowing recovery if needed.

    Final Outcomes:

    • Organized, up-to-date knowledge repository
    • Improved employee access to company information
    • Reduced time spent searching for documents
    • Enhanced organizational communication and transparency
Best Practice: Establish a regular review schedule (quarterly or bi-annually) to audit Knowledge Hub content, update outdated information, and remove obsolete documents. This ensures the knowledge base remains a trusted, current resource for all employees.

Initial Setup

Setting up the Knowledge Hub involves creating your first Space, configuring permissions, and establishing an organizational structure for your company's knowledge base. This section guides you through the first-time configuration process.

Step 1: Access the Knowledge Hub

    • Log in to your Bayzat account with Super Admin, Knowledge Hub Manager, or Editor permissions
    • Click the Company menu in the main navigation
    • Select Knowledge Hub from the dropdown menu

You will land on the Knowledge Hub Overview page, which displays:

  • Favorite spaces: Quick access to your most-used Spaces (initially empty)
  • Favorite folders: Quick access to frequently accessed folders (initially empty)
  • Recent files and pages: Recently viewed or edited content
  • Activity feed: Updates on document edits, uploads, and creations

Step 2: Create Your First Space

Spaces are the top-level containers for organizing content. Create your first Space to begin building your knowledge base.

  1. Click the Create button in the top-right corner of the Knowledge Hub interface
  2. Select Create new space from the dropdown menu

The Create Space dialog will appear with the following fields:

Required Settings

FieldDescriptionRecommended Value
NameThe display name for your SpaceUse clear, descriptive names (e.g., "HR Policies," "IT Resources," "Employee Handbook")
DescriptionBrief explanation of the Space's purpose1-2 sentences describing what content this Space contains
PermissionsWho can access this SpaceStart with "Private to you" for initial setup, then adjust after adding content
  1. Enter a descriptive Name for your Space (e.g., "Company Policies")
  2. Add a Description explaining the Space's purpose (e.g., "Official company policies, procedures, and guidelines for all employees")
  3. Select initial Permissions:
    • Private to you: Only you, Super Admins, and Knowledge Hub Managers can access (recommended for initial setup)
    • Editor: Specific users can edit content
    • Custom permissions: Granular control over individual user access
  4. Click Create to save your Space

Step 3: Configure Default Permission Settings

After creating your Space, configure permissions to control who can access and edit content. Permission settings apply to the entire Space and automatically inherit to all folders and pages within it.

Understanding Permission Levels

Permission LevelDescriptionUse Case
Only me and adminsOnly you, Super Admins, and Knowledge Hub Managers can viewConfidential content, draft Spaces under development
All employees can view this space and its contentsAny registered employee can view; only editors can modifyCompany-wide policies, general resources, public information
CustomGranular control with individual user permissionsDepartment-specific content, role-based access

Setting General Access Permissions

  1. Navigate to your newly created Space
  2. Click the Share button or select Share from the three-dot menu
  3. In the Share dialog, click Edit permissions
  4. Under General view access, select your preferred permission level from the dropdown

Setting Individual User Permissions (Custom)

If you selected Custom permissions, you can assign individual access levels:

  1. In the Edit permissions dialog, view the list of employees
  2. For each user, select their permission level from the dropdown:
    • Editor: Can create, edit, and delete content within the Space
    • View only: Can view content but cannot make changes
    • No access: Cannot see or access the Space
  3. Use the search field to find specific employees
  4. Use the Filters button to view users by permission level
  5. Click Save to apply permission changes
⚠️ Admin Permissions: Super Admins and Knowledge Hub Managers automatically have editor access to all company Spaces. Their permission level cannot be changed.

Step 4: Establish Organizational Structure

With your first Space created, establish a logical structure using folders and pages.

Creating Folders

  1. Navigate inside your Space
  2. Click the Create dropdown in the Space view
  3. Select Create new folder
  4. Enter a folder name and description
  5. Click Create

Creating Pages

  1. Within a Space or folder, click Create
  2. Select Create new page
  3. Enter page title and content using the rich text editor
  4. Add formatting, images, and links as needed
  5. Click Save to publish the page

Uploading Files

  1. Within a Space or folder, click Create
  2. Select Upload a file
  3. Choose files from your computer
  4. Click Upload to add files to the Space
