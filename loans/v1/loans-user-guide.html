What is Employee Loans?

Overview

The Employee Loans feature digitizes employee loan requests, approvals, and repayment tracking. Access loan requests through Payroll ‚Üí Employee Loans, and manage loan policies through Settings ‚Üí Payroll ‚Üí Employee Loan Policies.

Approve or reject loan requests, track disbursement status, and monitor repayment schedules. The system automatically creates payroll additions for disbursements and recurring deductions for repayments.

Key Benefits

  • Eliminate manual loan tracking spreadsheets and paper-based approval workflows
  • Automate loan disbursement additions and recurring repayment deductions in payroll
  • Enforce consistent loan policies with configurable limits and eligibility rules
  • Track loan status from request through full repayment in one system
  • Reduce errors in loan calculations and repayment schedules

Product Foundation Overview

How Employee Loans Fits

Employee Loans lets you create loan policies that define lending rules, eligibility criteria, and repayment terms. Once a policy is assigned to employees, they can submit loan requests through the platform. Approved loans automatically generate payroll additions for disbursement and recurring deductions for repayment.

Mental model: Loan Policy (template) ‚Üí Assigned to Employees ‚Üí Loan Request Submitted ‚Üí Approved ‚Üí Disbursement Addition + Recurring Deduction Created in Payroll

One loan policy can affect multiple employees. Changes to policy eligibility affect future loan requests, not existing loans.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating loan policies:

  • What loan amounts will you offer? ‚Äî Fixed amounts or percentage of salary
  • How will repayment work? ‚Äî Static period (e.g., 12 months) or flexible monthly deduction amount
  • Who is eligible? ‚Äî All employees, specific groups, or exclude probation employees
  • What currency? ‚Äî Must match your payroll currency settings

Related Features

  • Payroll ‚Äî Automatically creates loan disbursement additions and recurring repayment deductions
  • Employee Records ‚Äî Determines which employees can see and request loans based on policy assignment
  • Roles & Permissions ‚Äî Controls who can approve loan requests (Super Admin required)
  • Finance Reporting ‚Äî Tracks loan balances and repayment status across payroll cycles

Initial Setup

First-Time Configuration

Before employees can request Employee Loans, create at least one loan policy with defined limits and repayment terms.

Setup Steps

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Payroll ‚Üí Employee Loan Policies
  2. Click Create New Loan Policy
  3. Enter policy name and description
  4. Select currency for loan transactions
  5. Define loan amount method (fixed amount or salary percentage)
  6. Set minimum and maximum loan limits
  7. Configure repayment terms (static period or flexible monthly deduction)
  8. Add or exclude employees from policy eligibility
  9. Toggle Exclude employees on probation if needed
  10. Save and activate the policy

Required Configuration Settings

Setting Description Status

Currency Defines currency for all loan transactions under this policy Required
Loan Amount Method Choose fixed amount or percentage of employee salary Required
Minimum Loan Amount Must be greater than 0 Required

Post-Setup Verification

After creating your first loan policy, verify the following:

  • Policy appears in Settings ‚Üí Payroll ‚Üí Employee Loan Policies list
  • Policy status shows as Active
  • Eligible employees can see the policy when submitting loan requests
  • Loan limits and repayment terms match company requirements

Known Setup Limitations

‚ö†Ô∏è Non-Assigned Loan Policy Restriction

Employees cannot create loan requests without an assigned loan policy. Ensure all eligible employees are included in at least one active policy before enabling the feature.

Custom Role Limitation: The platform does not support creating multiple custom roles with distinct loan approval permissions for sequential workflow management. This prevents implementing complex, multi-level approval processes.

Key Tasks

Task: Set Up Loan Policies

Create and configure loan policies that define eligibility, amounts, and repayment terms for employees.

Subtask: Create New Loan Policy

Steps:

  • Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Payroll ‚Üí Employee Loan Policies
  • Click "Create New Loan Policy"
  • Enter policy name (mandatory in system's primary language), description, and select currency
  • Define loan amounts: choose fixed amount or salary percentage method
  • Set minimum loan amount (must be >0) and maximum limits
  • Configure repayment terms: static (fixed period) or flexible (monthly deduction amount)
  • Add or exclude specific employees; toggle probation employee exclusion if needed
  • Save and activate policy

Subtask: Modify Existing Policy

Steps:

  • Locate policy in Employee Loan Policies list
  • Click edit icon to modify policy details
  • Update loan amounts, repayment terms, or employee eligibility as needed
  • Save changes

Subtask: Deactivate Loan Policy

Steps:

  • Open policy settings
  • Toggle activation status to "Inactive"
  • Confirm deactivation (only Super Admins can perform this action)

Note: Employees without an assigned loan policy cannot create loan requests. Ensure employees are added to appropriate policies before they submit requests.

Task: Process Employee Loan Requests

Review, approve, or reject loan requests submitted by employees through the platform.

Subtask: View Pending Loan Requests

Steps:

  • Navigate to Payroll tab ‚Üí Employee Loans section
  • Review pending loan requests showing employee name, requested amount, and repayment terms
  • Click on individual request to view full details

Subtask: Approve Loan Request

Steps:

  • Select loan request from pending list
  • Click "Approve" button
  • System automatically creates loan disbursement addition in payroll
  • System creates recurring loan repayment deduction in payroll
  • Request moves to "Approved" status

‚ö†Ô∏è Loan disbursement and repayment additions/deductions cannot be deleted or changed once created. Verify loan details carefully before approval.

Subtask: Reject Loan Request

Steps:

  • Select loan request from pending list
  • Click "Reject" button
  • Request moves to "Rejected" column
  • Option to delete rejected loan if needed

Note: Loans can only be rejected while in pending status. Once approved and added to payroll, they cannot be rejected through the standard workflow.

Monitor loan disbursements, ongoing repayments, and completion status across all employees.

Steps:

Subtask: Request Loan Deletion (Approved Loans)

Steps:

  • Identify approved loan requiring deletion
  • Contact Bayzat support team with loan details (employee name, loan ID, amount, reason for deletion)
  • Support team performs backend SQL intervention to delete loan and associated payroll deductions
  • Verify deletion after support team confirmation

‚ö†Ô∏è The platform lacks a user interface mechanism for bulk deleting loan requests without automatically notifying employees. Bulk deletions require manual backend intervention by the support team.

Subtask: Handle Loans for Inactive Employees

Steps:

  • Identify inactive employees with outstanding loan balances in Disbursed loans section
  • Determine resolution: continue tracking, write off, or request manual deletion
  • Contact support for manual status transition if employee is not added to payroll

Note: The system lacks flexibility in loan status management for employees not added to payroll. Manual transitions or deletions for disbursed loans require support team assistance.

Task: Integrate Employee Loans with Payroll

Ensure loan disbursements and repayments are correctly reflected in payroll processing.

Subtask: Verify Loan Disbursement Addition

Steps:

Subtask: Verify Recurring Loan Repayment Deduction

Steps:

  • Access employee payroll record
  • Verify recurring deduction appears in payroll table
  • Confirm deduction amount matches repayment terms (static period or monthly deduction)
  • Monitor deduction across subsequent payroll cycles until loan is fully repaid

Note: Loan disbursements and repayments are automatically integrated with the payroll system. Manual adjustments to these entries are not supported and require backend intervention.

Subtask: Handle Payroll Integration Issues

Steps:

  • Identify discrepancies between loan records and payroll entries
  • Verify loan status in Employee Loans section
  • Check payroll cycle dates and employee payroll inclusion status
  • Contact support if loan is disbursed but not reflected in payroll, or if repayment deductions are missing

Business Rules & Limitations

Loan Policy Requirements

Policy Assignment Required: Employees cannot submit loan requests without an assigned loan policy. Ensure all eligible employees are included in at least one active policy before enabling loan requests.

Loan Disbursement Rules

  • Non-Editable Transactions: Loan disbursement and repayment entries cannot be modified or deleted once created
  • Rejection Window: Loans can only be rejected while in pending status; approved loans require backend intervention to modify

Edge Cases

  • Employee Termination with Active Loan: Loan balance remains in system even after employee becomes inactive; no automatic settlement or write-off mechanism
  • Payroll Reset Impact: Resetting payroll module requires backend intervention and affects all loan records; plan carefully before requesting reset
  • Multiple Loan Policies: Employee can be assigned to multiple policies but can only have one active loan request at a time
  • Rejected Loan Deletion: Only rejected loans can be deleted; approved or disbursed loans require support intervention
  • Loan Policy Deactivation: Deactivating a policy does not affect existing loan requests or active loans under that policy
  • Currency Mismatch: Loan policy currency must match employee salary currency; system does not support cross-currency loans
  • Partial Repayment Scenarios: If employee salary is insufficient to cover full monthly deduction, system behavior depends on payroll configuration
  • Bulk Operations: No bulk approval, rejection, or deletion available; each loan must be processed individually

Data Integrity Scenarios

‚ö†Ô∏è Leave Request Deletion: If a leave request linked to payroll deductions is deleted after processing, the deduction details may not be retained in payroll records. Verify all leave-related deductions before deleting requests.

  • Deleted Employees in Payroll Table: Platform lacks automatic synchronization; deleted employees may remain in payroll table requiring manual cleanup
  • Manual Deduction Conflicts: Manual payroll adjustments cannot override automatic loan repayment deductions; coordinate adjustments carefully
  • Salary Arrears with Loans: Salary arrears cannot be deleted even for inactive employees; may create reconciliation challenges with active loans

Approval Flow Edge Cases

  • Custom Approval Roles: Platform does not support multiple custom roles for sequential loan approval workflows
  • Approval Hierarchy Limitations: Loan approval requires Super Admin role; cannot delegate to department managers or custom roles
  • Notification Management: Bulk loan deletions trigger automatic employee notifications; no option to suppress notifications

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Can employees request Employee Loans without an assigned loan policy?

No. Employees must be assigned to an active loan policy before they can submit loan requests.

Can I delete a loan after it has been approved?

Approved loans cannot be deleted through the UI. Contact support for backend assistance if deletion is required before disbursement.

What happens to loan records when an employee becomes inactive?

Inactive employees with outstanding loan balances remain visible in the Disbursed loans section. The system does not currently filter or hide these records automatically.

Can I modify loan repayment deductions after approval?

No. Once a loan is approved, the system automatically creates disbursement additions and recurring repayment deductions that cannot be edited or deleted.

How do I track which Employee Loans have been paid as cash versus through payroll?

The system currently does not include a "Paid as Cash" indicator. Track cash-based loan transactions manually outside the platform.

Can I create custom roles for loan approval workflows?

Loan approval workflows are currently limited to Super Admin roles. Custom role-based approval routing is not supported.

What happens if I need to delete all loan requests at once?

The platform does not support bulk deletion of loan requests through the UI. Contact support for backend assistance with comprehensive loan request management.

Can Expense Managers view loan data?

The Expense Manager role may have access to loan data due to lack of granular permission controls. Configure role permissions carefully to limit cross-module visibility.

What happens to Employee Loans when I reset the payroll cycle?

Payroll cycle resets are limited to 6 months and require backend support intervention. Loan records in closed cycles cannot be deleted to maintain audit trail integrity.

Can I change loan status manually if an employee is not added to payroll?

No. The system does not support manual status transitions for disbursed loans. Loans remain in disbursed status even after the due date if the employee is not in payroll.
