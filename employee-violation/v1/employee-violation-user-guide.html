1What is the Employee Violation System?

The Employee Violation System is a comprehensive tool within Bayzat that enables HR managers and supervisors to track, document, and manage employee violations and disciplinary actions. This system provides a structured approach to handling workplace misconduct while maintaining compliance with company policies and labor regulations.

ⓘ

Beta Feature

The Employee Violation System is currently in BETA. This means the feature is still being refined and may receive updates. Your feedback helps improve the system.

Key Capabilities

Violation Tracking

Log and track employee violations with detailed records including violation type, date, description, and supporting documentation.

Progressive Discipline

Configure escalation rules that automatically apply progressive disciplinary actions based on violation frequency and severity.

Employee Directory

View violation history per employee, enabling informed decision-making and pattern identification.

Reports & Analytics

Generate comprehensive reports on violation trends, employee records, and compliance metrics.

2Product Foundation Overview

System Architecture

The Employee Violation System is built as an embedded application within the Bayzat platform, accessible through the Apps menu. It integrates seamlessly with the Employee Tickets system for workflow management.

📄 Violations Module

Central hub for viewing all violations. Filter by status (All, Pending, Approved, Rejected). Each violation creates a corresponding ticket for tracking.

👥 Employee Directory

Browse employees and view their individual violation history. Access detailed profiles with violation counts and disciplinary records.

📈 Reports

Analytics dashboard with violation statistics including totals, employee metrics, and trend analysis through visual charts.

⚙ Settings

Configure violation types with validity periods and set up escalation rules for progressive discipline management.

Integration with Employee Tickets

Every violation logged in the system automatically creates an Employee Ticket. This integration ensures:

  • Unified tracking of violations across systems
  • Approval workflow through the ticketing system
  • Complete audit trail of disciplinary actions
  • Status synchronization between violations and tickets
💡

Pro Tip

Clicking on a Ticket ID in the violations list opens the corresponding ticket in the Employee Tickets system for detailed review and approval actions.

5Managing Violations

Logging a New Violation

To log a violation against an employee:

  1. Click the Log Violation button (purple button in top-right)
  2. In the dialog that appears:
    • Employee: Select from the dropdown list
    • Violation Type: Choose from 26 pre-configured types
    • Violation Date: Set the date of occurrence
    • Notes: Add any relevant details or context
  3. Click Log Violation to submit

Violation Types Dropdown

When logging a violation, you can select from 26 pre-configured violation types. These cover various workplace infractions from minor to severe.

Each violation in the list displays:

  • Employee Name: The staff member involved
  • Violation Type: Category of the infraction
  • Date: When the violation occurred
  • Status: Current state (Pending/Approved/Rejected)

Important Note

Once a violation is logged, it cannot be deleted. It can only be rejected through the approval workflow. Ensure all information is accurate before submitting.

6Violation Types & Validity Periods

The system comes pre-configured with 26 violation types, each with a defined validity period. The validity period determines how long a violation remains "active" for escalation calculations.

Understanding Validity Periods

30 Days

Minor infractions that expire quickly. Suitable for occasional lateness or minor policy deviations.

180 Days

Moderate violations with medium-term tracking. Used for repeated minor issues or moderate misconduct.

365 Days

Serious violations tracked for a full year. Applied to significant misconduct requiring long-term monitoring.

No Validity

Permanent records that never expire. Reserved for the most serious infractions.

Complete Violation Types List

Violation Type Validity Period Description
Negligence/Carelessness 365 Days Failure to exercise proper care in duties
Lateness 30 Days Arriving after scheduled work time
Leaving Work Early 30 Days Departing before end of shift without approval
Unauthorized Absence 180 Days Missing work without proper notification
Insubordination 365 Days Refusal to follow legitimate instructions
Harassment No Validity Unwanted behavior toward colleagues
Safety Violation 365 Days Breach of workplace safety protocols
Dress Code Violation 30 Days Non-compliance with dress standards
Confidentiality Breach No Validity Unauthorized disclosure of sensitive information
Theft/Fraud No Validity Stealing or deceptive practices
Substance Abuse No Validity Use of prohibited substances at work
Poor Performance 180 Days Failure to meet job standards
Misuse of Company Property 180 Days Improper use of company resources
Falsification of Records No Validity Providing false information in documents
Conflict of Interest 365 Days Personal interests conflicting with duties
💡

Validity Period Impact

When an employee's violation exceeds its validity period, it is no longer counted toward escalation thresholds. For example, a lateness violation (30-day validity) logged on January 1st would not count toward escalation calculations after January 31st.

7Escalation Rules & Progressive Discipline

Escalation rules define the progressive disciplinary actions that are automatically applied based on the number of violations an employee accumulates within the validity period.

Progressive Discipline Flow

A typical escalation progression follows this pattern:

1st - Verbal Warning → 2nd - Written Warning → 3rd - Final Warning → 4th - Termination

Configuring Escalation Rules

To add or modify escalation rules:

  1. Navigate to Settings tab
  2. Select Escalation Rules section
  3. Click Add Escalation Rule
  4. Configure the following:
    • Violation Type: Select applicable violation type
    • Number of Violations: Threshold count (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.)
    • Action Type: Warning level or termination
    • Deduction Type: Financial penalty if applicable
  5. Click Save to apply the rule

Deduction Types

When configuring escalation rules, you can specify financial deductions:

Deduction Type Description Use Case
No Deduction No financial penalty applied First warnings, minor violations
Percentage of Monthly Salary Deduct a percentage from monthly pay Moderate penalties (e.g., 5%, 10%)
Custom Amount Specify a fixed monetary deduction Specific fine amounts per policy
⚠

Legal Compliance

Ensure all escalation rules and deductions comply with UAE labor law and your company's employment contracts. Consult with your legal team before implementing financial penalties.

9Troubleshooting & Edge Cases

Common Issues

Issue Possible Cause Solution
Cannot log violation Missing required fields or permissions Ensure all fields are filled and you have appropriate access rights
Employee not in dropdown Employee not active in system Verify employee status in HR system; only active employees appear
Violation not appearing in list Filter settings or loading delay Check filter is set to "All" and refresh the page
Ticket link not working Tab blocked or session expired Allow pop-ups for Bayzat and re-authenticate if needed
Escalation not triggering Violations outside validity period Check if previous violations have expired based on validity rules
Reports showing no data Date range or filter issue Adjust date filters and ensure violations exist in selected period

Known Limitations

⚠

Beta Feature Limitations

  • Violations cannot be deleted once logged (only rejected via workflow)
  • Bulk operations on violations are not yet available
  • Custom violation types cannot be added (26 pre-configured types only)
  • Export functionality may have limited format options
  • Mobile app access may have reduced functionality

Best Practices

  • Document thoroughly: Always include detailed notes when logging violations for future reference
  • Act promptly: Log violations as soon as they occur to maintain accurate records
  • Review regularly: Check pending violations daily to avoid backlogs
  • Configure escalation first: Set up escalation rules before actively logging violations
  • Train managers: Ensure all supervisors understand the violation categories and processes

11Glossary of Terms

Progressive Discipline A system of increasingly severe penalties for repeated violations
