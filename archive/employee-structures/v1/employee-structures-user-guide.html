What is employee-structures?

Overview

Employee Structures lets you create custom groupings of employees based on criteria like department, location, or job title. Access it from Settings > Company > Employee Structures to organize your workforce for targeted policy application and reporting.

Create structures automatically using criteria rules or manually by selecting specific employees. Use these structures to assign policies, generate reports, and configure workflows across defined employee segments.

Key Benefits

  • Automate employee grouping using department, location, role, or custom field criteria
  • Apply leave policies, attendance rules, and payroll settings to entire groups instantly
  • Generate segmented reports without manual filtering
  • Maintain dynamic groups that auto-update as employee data changes
  • Replace spreadsheet-based employee lists with system-managed structures

Product Foundation Overview

How Employee Structures Fits

Employee Structures is a configuration template that defines logical groupings of employees based on shared attributes. It sits in the Company Settings layer and serves as a foundation for role-based access control and reporting.

Mental model: Employee Structure (template) ‚Üí Assigned to employees via criteria ‚Üí Groups employees ‚Üí Enables targeted access control and reporting

Once created, structures apply organization-wide and can be referenced by Role Management, Leave Policies, and other HR modules that require employee segmentation.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating employee structures:

  • What business logic drives your groupings? ‚Äî Decide whether to group by department, location, job level, or custom criteria

  • Who needs access to which employee groups? ‚Äî Structure assignments directly impact what managers and admins can see and manage
  • Do you need nested hierarchies? ‚Äî Structures support single-level groupings; complex hierarchies may require multiple structures

Initial Setup

First-Time Configuration

Before creating your first employee structure, ensure you have the necessary permissions and employee data in place.

Required Setup Steps

  1. Navigate to Settings > Company
  2. Expand the Employee Structures accordion
  3. Review existing structures (if any)
  4. Click Add New to create your first structure

Verify Prerequisites

Confirm these items are configured before creating structures:

  • Employee Records: At least one active employee must exist in the system
  • Data Points: Employee profiles must have relevant data fields populated (department, location, job title, etc.)
  • Permissions: You must have Super Admin or appropriate role management permissions

Note: Employee Structures can be created using automatic criteria-based grouping or manual selection. Plan your grouping strategy before starting.

Choose Your Grouping Method

Decide how you want to organize employees into groups:

Automatic Grouping

Use when you want to group employees based on shared attributes (department, location, job title, etc.).

  • System automatically assigns employees to groups based on criteria
  • New employees matching criteria are auto-assigned
  • Best for large organizations with consistent data

Manual Grouping

Use when you need custom groups that don't follow standard data patterns.

  • Manually select employees for each group
  • Full control over group membership
  • Best for project teams or special assignments

Business Rules & Limitations

Known Limitations

The following limitations have been identified through user reports and support tickets. Most relate to leave policy management features that interact with employee structures.

‚ö†Ô∏è Leave Policy Changes Can Unassign Employees

Modifying leave policies can unintentionally unassign employees and erase historical leave balance data without providing a recovery method. Always back up employee assignments before making policy changes.

‚ö†Ô∏è Leave Policy Type Cannot Be Changed

Once a leave policy type is set during creation, it becomes permanently locked and cannot be changed, even by backend administrators. Plan policy types carefully during initial setup.

‚ö†Ô∏è Leave Calculation Does Not Account for Shift Days-Off

The platform cannot automatically account for scheduled shift days-off in leave calculations. Employees with complex shift schedules cannot accurately track leave usage using working days leave policies.

‚ö†Ô∏è Changing Leave Cycles Requires Complete Reconfiguration

The platform does not support changing leave cycle types after initial configuration without completely resetting time-off data. Migrating from custom fiscal year to calendar year leave cycles loses all historical leave information.

‚ö†Ô∏è No Automatic Leave Cycle Recalculation on Hire Date Changes

The platform lacks automatic leave cycle recalculation when an employee's hire date is modified. Manual intervention is required to update leave policy assignments and cycle dates for companies using individual leave cycles.

‚ö†Ô∏è Leave Balance Missing for Pre-Hire Policy Configuration

The system cannot automatically calculate leave balance when leave policies are configured before an employee's hire date, especially while the employee is in inactive status. This creates a gap in automatic leave balance initialization.

‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot Change Leave Type Status After Assignment

The platform restricts users from changing leave type status (paid/unpaid) after initial assignment. This prevents flexible leave management and creates a rigid system that does not accommodate dynamic workforce needs.

‚ö†Ô∏è Deleted Leave Policies Cannot Be Fully Restored

The platform lacks a robust data recovery mechanism for deleted leave policies. When a leave policy is deleted, associated leave requests cannot be fully restored, forcing manual recreation of historical data.

‚ö†Ô∏è Automatic Leave Proration Conflicts with UAE Labor Law

The platform automatically prorates leave balances based on hire date, which conflicts with UAE labor law requirements for maternity and sick leave. This prevents HR managers from configuring leave policies that grant full entitlements to employees.

Edge Cases

  • Multi-level policy configurations: Complex setups with policy-level and employee-level settings can create confusion. Always verify which level controls each setting. Manual overrides may not be immediately visible.
  • Leave requests during probation: Ensure probation dates are filled in employee work profiles for restrictions to apply. Policies must explicitly enable probation restrictions.
  • Public holiday recalculations: Changes to public holiday calendars only affect current and future leave cycles. Past cycles are not recalculated. Enable restriction for editing in time-off policy settings.
  • Overlapping leave requests: System allows overlapping requests but does not automatically prevent them. Use Bayzat Insights to identify conflicts before approval.
  • Half-day leave restrictions: Cannot restrict half-day leaves at policy level. Must rely on manual approval process to enforce minimum duration requirements.
  • Leave encashment for different policy types: One-time vs. accrual policies handle encashment differently. Verify encashment behavior before configuring end-of-service calculations.
  • Negative leave balances: If permitted, negative balances can accumulate without automatic alerts. Monitor balances regularly to prevent excessive negative accruals.
  • Calendar vs. working days: Changing calculation method requires rejecting and resubmitting affected leave requests. Plan changes carefully to minimize disruption.
  • Leave salary requests across cycles: Platform cannot link or consolidate related requests. Add detailed comments to maintain context across separate submissions.
  • Shift schedule integration: Leave calculations do not automatically account for shift days-off. Manually verify calculations for employees with non-standard schedules.
  • KSA/UAE compliance: Platform may not natively support all local labor law requirements. Document compliance gaps and implement manual processes where needed.
  • Bulk carry-over updates: No UI support for bulk modifications. Contact support for SQL-based updates or plan individual policy updates during low-activity periods.
  • Leave policy audit trails: No built-in change history. Maintain separate documentation of policy changes for audit and compliance purposes.
  • Zero encashable days: UI may not correctly display when maximum encashable days is set to zero. Verify configuration in backend if display appears incorrect.
  • Automatic recalculations: System may modify leave requests in background without clear notification. Review all affected requests after administrative changes.

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I assign an employee to multiple structures?

Yes. Employees can belong to multiple structures simultaneously based on different criteria (e.g., one structure by department, another by location).

What happens if I delete a structure?

Deleting a structure removes the grouping configuration but does not affect employee records or their assignments to other structures.

How do automatic structures update when employee data changes?

Automatic structures recalculate group membership when the underlying employee data (department, location, etc.) changes, ensuring groups stay current.

Can I convert an automatic structure to manual?

No. Structure types are fixed at creation. To change grouping methods, create a new structure with the desired type.

What's the difference between structures and organizational hierarchy?

Structures are flexible groupings for reporting and policy assignment. Organizational hierarchy (reporting lines) defines approval workflows and management relationships.

How many structures can I create?

There is no hard limit on the number of structures, but create only what you need for clear reporting and policy management.
