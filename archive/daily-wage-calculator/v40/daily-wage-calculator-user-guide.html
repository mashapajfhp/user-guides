Daily Wage Calculator

Calculate accurate daily wages for salary proration, leave encashment, and unpaid leave deductions

What is Daily Wage Calculator?

The Daily Wage Calculator is a flexible and customizable method for calculating daily wages, including salary proration, leave encashment, and unpaid leave deductions. This feature enables HR teams and payroll administrators to ensure accurate compensation calculations based on actual days worked and company-specific policies.

Who Uses This Feature?

Superadmin, Payroll table managers, and transaction processors rely on the Daily Wage Calculator to configure wage calculation methods and manage payroll operations. HR personnel and payroll administrators responsible for managing employee leave and salary calculations use this feature to process unpaid leave requests and generate accurate pay deductions.

Key Benefits

  • Flexible Calculation Methods: Choose from calendar days, working days, or custom days to align with your company's labor law requirements
  • Accurate Salary Components: Select salary components to factor into calculations—basic salary, basic salary plus allowances, or allowances only
  • Automated Deductions: Automatic pay deduction reflected on the payroll table for unpaid leave
  • Compliance Ready: Most UAE and KSA companies use 30 custom days to align with labor law

Understanding Calculation Methods

How Daily Pay is Calculated

Daily pay is calculated by dividing total salary by the number of actual working days.

Formula:

Daily Pay = Total Salary ÷ Number of Working Days

Example:

  • Employee salary: AED 3,000
  • Calculation basis: 30 custom days
  • Daily pay: AED 3,000 ÷ 30 = AED 100 per day
Public Holidays: Public holidays are deducted from working days when calculating daily pay. Ensure your company's public holiday calendar is up to date in the system.

Common Scenarios

Scenario: Employee Takes 5 Days Unpaid Leave

Setup: Daily Wage Calculation configured with 30 custom days, Basic + Allowances

Employee Salary: AED 3,000 basic + AED 500 allowances = AED 3,500 total

Calculation:

  • Daily pay = AED 3,500 ÷ 30 = AED 116.67 per day
  • Unpaid leave deduction = AED 116.67 × 5 days = AED 583.35
  • This amount is automatically deducted from the employee's monthly salary

Scenario: New Employee Joins Mid-Month

Setup: Salary Proration configured with Working Days calculation

Employee Salary: AED 2,500

Calculation:

  • If the month has 22 working days and employee joins on day 12
  • Working days for employee = 11 days
  • Prorated salary = (AED 2,500 ÷ 22) × 11 = AED 1,250
  • System automatically applies this proration to the first salary

Troubleshooting & Edge Cases

Common Issues and Solutions

Issue: Unexpected Daily Wage Amounts After Configuration Change

Cause: Different calculation methods can impact final wage calculations. Changing from calendar days to working days, or vice versa, will produce different daily rates.

Solution:

  • Verify which calculation base is currently active in Settings > Payroll > Daily Wage Calculation
  • Review the number of days being used (30 for calendar, 22 for typical working days, or your custom setting)
  • Recalculate expected daily wage manually using the formula: Total Salary ÷ Number of Days
  • Compare with system-calculated amount to identify discrepancies

Issue: Prorated Salary Transactions Not Updating After Configuration Change

Cause: When payroll month is open and prorated salary transactions exist, changes to proration settings require manual transaction updates.

Solution:

  • Navigate to the Payroll table for the affected month
  • Locate all prorated salary transactions (typically in "Unpaid" status)
  • Reject each transaction individually
  • The system will automatically recalculate based on new proration settings
  • Review updated amounts before approving payroll

Issue: Inconsistent Daily Wage Calculations Across Months

Cause: When using calendar days, calculation varies depending on month length (e.g., 31 days in January, 28 days in February).

Solution:

  • If consistent daily rates are required, switch to Working Days or Custom Days calculation base
  • If calendar days must be used, document and communicate that daily rates will vary by month
  • Review payroll reports to confirm month-specific calculations are correct

Issue: Leave Encashment Amounts Don't Match Expected Values

Cause: Leave encashment calculations depend on correctly configured salary components and calculation methods.

Solution:

  • Verify End of Service Eligibility settings: Settings > Payroll > End of Service Eligibility
  • Confirm that salary components (Basic Only vs. Basic + Allowances) match your policy
  • Check that the calculation method (calendar/working/custom days) is correct
  • Verify that the leave type is configured as eligible for encashment
  • Recalculate manually: (Daily Wage × Eligible Leave Days)

Issue: Unpaid Leave Deductions Appear Incorrect

Cause: Unpaid leave deductions depend on the selected calculation method and day calculation type in leave policy settings.

Solution:

  • Navigate to Settings > Leaves > Leave Policies
  • Select the unpaid leave policy and verify the calculation method (Basic Only vs. Basic + Allowances)
  • Confirm the day calculation type (calendar days, working days, or custom days)
  • Calculate expected deduction: Daily Wage × Number of Unpaid Leave Days
  • Compare with actual payroll deduction

Issue: Public Holidays Not Reflected in Daily Wage Calculation

Cause: Public holidays must be considered and subtracted from working days when calculating daily pay. If public holidays are not configured, they won't be deducted.

Solution:

  • Verify your company's public holiday calendar is up-to-date
  • Confirm that the calculation base is set to Working Days (not Calendar Days)
  • Check that all applicable public holidays for the month are marked in the system
  • Recalculate: Working Days in Month - Public Holidays = Days Used in Calculation

Edge Cases

Edge Case: Employee Joins Mid-Month

Scenario: An employee's first salary needs to be prorated based on actual days worked.

Handling: Salary proration automatically adjusts employee compensation based on the selected calculation method. The system will calculate the daily wage and multiply by actual days worked in the month.

Edge Case: Employee Takes Unpaid Leave in First or Last Month

Scenario: Unpaid leave deductions need to be calculated for an employee's first or last month when salary is already prorated.

Handling: The daily wage is calculated based on the prorated salary amount, and unpaid leave deductions are applied to this reduced amount. Ensure that both proration and leave deductions are correctly reflected in the final payroll.

Edge Case: Multiple Unpaid Leave Policies in Single Month

Scenario: An employee takes multiple types of unpaid leave in the same month, each with different calculation methods.

Handling: Each unpaid leave type is calculated daily and set off against the employee's monthly pay. The system will apply each policy's calculation method independently and aggregate the total deduction.

Edge Case: February with Fewer Days

Scenario: February has 28 or 29 days, affecting calendar day calculations.

Handling: The system automatically accounts for month length variations. Daily wage calculations will use the actual number of calendar days in February. Consider using Working Days or Custom Days for consistent monthly calculations.

Edge Case: End-of-Service with Unused Leave Encashment

Scenario: Employee leaves the company with accrued but unused leave that must be encashed.

Handling: Leave encashment for end-of-service is calculated based on configured salary components and calculation methods. Verify that the leave type is marked as eligible for end-of-service encashment before processing the final settlement.

Feature Gaps & Known Limitations

Known Limitations

Month-to-Month Calculation Variation

When using calendar days as the calculation base, daily wage calculations vary depending on month length (e.g., 31 days in January, 28 days in February). This can result in different daily rates and leave deduction amounts each month, which may complicate payroll planning and employee communication.

Workaround: Consider using Working Days or Custom Days (typically 22 or 30) for more consistent monthly calculations.

Manual Transaction Updates Required During Open Payroll

When payroll month is open and prorated salary transactions exist, super admin must manually reject and resave transactions after changing proration settings. This adds manual steps to the payroll process and increases the risk of errors.

Workaround: Plan configuration changes before opening the payroll month, or close the payroll month before making changes to proration settings.

Public Holiday Configuration Dependency

Public holidays must be considered and subtracted from working days when calculating daily pay. If your company's public holiday calendar is not properly configured or updated, daily wage calculations using working days will be inaccurate.

Workaround: Maintain an up-to-date public holiday calendar and verify it before each payroll cycle.

Limited Salary Component Options

Daily wage calculations are limited to two salary component options: Basic Only or Basic + Allowances. If your compensation structure requires more granular component selection, you may need to use custom calculations or workarounds.

Workaround: Contact Bayzat support to discuss custom calculation requirements.
