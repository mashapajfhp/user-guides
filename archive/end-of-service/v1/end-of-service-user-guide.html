End of Service

Calculate and settle final payments for departing employees in compliance with UAE labor law

What is End of Service?

Feature Description

End of Service is a feature that calculates and settles the final payment for employees when they leave the company, including gratuity, leave encashments, and other financial components according to UAE labor law. This comprehensive settlement process ensures accurate compliance with regulatory requirements and transparent financial handling of employee departures.

Who Uses This Feature?

HR Administrators or Payroll Managers who manage employee departures and final settlements are the primary users of this feature. These professionals are responsible for processing employee exits and ensuring all financial obligations are correctly calculated and documented.

Key Benefits

  • Automated calculation of gratuity according to UAE labor law requirements
  • Comprehensive settlement breakdown including leave encashments and additional payments
  • Flexible management of additions and deductions for customized settlements
  • Compliance with MOHRE regulations and local labor standards
  • Printable settlement documents for employee records and audit trails
  • Reduced manual calculation errors and processing time

Product Foundation Overview

How End of Service Fits

End of Service is a UAE labor law-compliant settlement calculator that computes gratuity, leave encashments, and final payments when an employee departs. It pulls data from the Payroll table, employee profile, and time-off records to generate accurate, auditable settlements.

Mental model: Select departing employee → Enter departure date and reason → System calculates gratuity based on tenure → Adds leave encashment → Review additions/deductions → Submit for processing.

Key Decisions Before Setup

  • Is employee contract type (Limited/Unlimited) correctly recorded?
  • Are all salary components up-to-date for accurate gratuity calculation?
  • Have leave balances been reconciled before processing?
  • What additional deductions (loans, advances) need to be included?

Related Features

  • Finance Module: Processes final settlement payments
  • Payroll Table: Source of employee salary and compensation data
  • Time Off Management: Provides leave balances for encashment calculation
  • Employee Profile: Contains contract type and service dates for gratuity

Business Rules & Limitations

Core Business Rules

End of Service calculations follow specific rules and constraints based on UAE labor law and contract type. The following table outlines the core business rules:

Rule Description Impact
Minimum Service Duration Minimum 1 year of continuous service required for gratuity eligibility Employees with less than 1 year of service receive no gratuity
Gratuity Calculation Formula Different calculation formulas apply for service periods under 5 years versus over 5 years Gratuity amount varies based on length of service
Gratuity Cap Maximum gratuity capped at two years' basic salary Gratuity cannot exceed two years of basic salary regardless of service duration
Basic Salary Only Gratuity calculation based solely on basic salary Allowances, bonuses, and variable pay are excluded from gratuity calculation
Non-AED Currency Non-AED employees receive 0 gratuity Only AED-denominated salaries qualify for gratuity calculation
Contract Type Handling Different handling for limited and unlimited contract types Contract type affects gratuity calculation methodology
Unpaid Leave Exclusion Unpaid leave days are excluded from service calculation Service duration is reduced by unpaid leave periods
Leave Encashment Unused leave days are converted to monetary value based on daily rate Leave encashment is added to final settlement amount

System Constraints

The End of Service feature has specific limitations regarding data visibility and calculation scope:

  • Variable pay and work expenses are not shown for future departure dates
  • Gratuity calculations are based on historical data and may not reflect future salary changes
  • The system calculates gratuity based on the departure date selected; retroactive adjustments require manual intervention
  • Leave encashment calculations depend on accurate leave balance data in the system
  • Custom deductions and additions must be manually entered; they are not automatically calculated

Compliance Requirements

End of Service calculations must comply with UAE labor law and Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE) regulations:

  • Gratuity calculations must comply with the new UAE labor law
  • All calculations must use the correct daily rate derived from basic salary (monthly salary divided by 30)
  • Service duration must be calculated accurately, accounting for unpaid leave and other absences
  • Final settlement documents should be retained for audit and compliance purposes
  • All deductions must be legally permissible under UAE labor law

Edge Cases

The following edge cases require special attention when processing End of Service:

Important: Gratuity can be edited and leave encashments can be modified. Any manual adjustments should be documented and reviewed for compliance with UAE labor law.
  • Leap Year Calculations: Leap years require calculation using 366 days instead of 365. Ensure the system correctly accounts for leap years in service duration calculations.
  • Mid-Month Departures: When an employee departs mid-month, the daily rate calculation must account for the partial month worked. The system will prorate the monthly pay accordingly.
  • Multiple Contract Changes: If an employee's contract type changed during employment, gratuity calculation may need to account for different service periods under different contract types.
  • Unpaid Leave Periods: Unpaid leave days are excluded from service calculation. Ensure all unpaid leave periods are accurately recorded in the system.
  • Recurring Deductions: The system allows ability to add one-off or recurring deductions. Verify that only applicable deductions are included in the final settlement.
  • Salary Changes During Service: If an employee's basic salary changed during their tenure, the gratuity calculation should use the salary at the time of departure, not historical salary.
  • Partial Year Service: For employees with less than 1 year of service, no gratuity is payable, but leave encashment and other components may still apply.
