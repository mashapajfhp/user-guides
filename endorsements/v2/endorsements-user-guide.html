What is Endorsements?

Overview

Endorsements lets HR admins add or remove employees and dependents from group health insurance policies directly in Bayzat. Access it from Health > Group insurance > Endorsements, where you'll find separate tabs for Additions and Removals.

Submit new additions or removals, upload required documents with AI verification, track status through approval stages, and download insurance cards or COIs when complete.

Key Benefits

  • AI extracts passport, visa, and Emirates ID data automatically from uploaded documents
  • 3-step wizard guides you through required documents, member details, and conditional documents
  • Save drafts and continue later instead of completing submissions in one session
  • Track status in real-time from submission through insurer approval to completion
  • Filter by status, plan, or submission date across paginated lists of all endorsements
  • Download COIs within 30 days and insurance cards directly from the platform

Strategic Context

🎯 What Endorsements Solves

Replaces email-based insurance endorsement requests that cause delays and repeated document requests.

  • Eliminates endorsement@bayzat.com email workflow
  • Standardizes document requirements per insurer and emirate
  • Enables self-service for HR admins to manage policy changes

💰 Why It Matters

Insurance endorsements are the most common admin task, with approximately 2600 processed monthly.

  • Reduces turnaround time for adding employees to insurance plans
  • Prevents late addition penalties through faster processing
  • Decreases endorsement team time spent giving feedback on missing documents

🏗️ How It Connects

Transactional feature that modifies employee insurance policy assignments.

  • Submitted for specific employees or dependents on specific policies
  • References group insurance policies and employee profiles
  • Affects billing, claims eligibility, and insurance card issuance

Product Foundation Overview

How Endorsements Fits

Endorsements are transactional requests that modify active health insurance policies by adding or removing employees and dependents. Each endorsement represents a change event that flows through document submission, verification, insurer processing, and completion stages.

Mental model: Employee/Dependent → Endorsement Request (Addition/Removal) → Document Verification → Insurer Processing → Policy Updated

One endorsement affects one member's insurance status but may require documents for multiple related individuals (e.g., removing an employee also removes their dependents).

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before processing endorsements:

  • Which insurance providers are enabled? — Only providers with endorsement configuration allow online submissions
  • What documents are required per provider and emirate? — Requirements vary by insurer and location (Abu Dhabi vs Dubai)
  • Are AI document verification features enabled? — AI can auto-extract passport, visa, and Emirates ID data
  • What are the turnaround times (TAT) per policy type? — Expected processing times must be configured per insurer and policy

Related Features

These Bayzat features work together with Endorsements. Where available, links point to the published user guide.

  • Group Insurance Policies — Active policies determine which employees are eligible for endorsements and which insurers' configurations apply
  • Employee Profile — Source of employee data that auto-populates endorsement forms; health insurance tab shows policy status and removal actions
  • Document Management — Stores uploaded endorsement documents and enables Certificate of Insurance (COI) and insurance card downloads
  • Admin Panel Provider Configuration — Defines required documents, conditional questions, templates, and TAT settings per insurer and emirate
  • Offboarding — Integrates with removal endorsements when employees depart; offboarded employees can be marked as already removed

Initial Setup & Configuration

Before You Begin

Endorsements does not require dedicated settings configuration. The feature is ready to use once you have active health insurance policies. Ensure the following prerequisites from the product-foundation section are met before submitting your first endorsement request.

First Addition Request

To add an employee to health insurance for the first time:

  1. Navigate to Health > Group insurance > Endorsements
  2. Click New addition (purple button, top-right)
  3. Select the employee from the list of uninsured employees
  4. Choose the insurance policy from the available plans

The system opens a 3-step wizard: Required documents, Member details, Additional documents.

Step 1: Upload Required Documents

The first step collects mandatory documents for the insurance provider.

Each document section displays a status:

  • Not complete — Document not yet uploaded
  • Complete — Document uploaded and verified
  • Attention required — Document has issues (expired, wrong type)

Expand each section and upload the required documents. Common sections include:

  • Establishment card (for sponsor matching)
  • Passport
  • Emirates ID
  • Visa
  • Change Status form

AI Document Verification

When you upload passport, visa, or Emirates ID documents, AI automatically extracts and validates data.

AI verification outcomes:

  • Verified — Document marked complete, component turns green
  • Failed — Options to re-upload, verify manually, or ignore and continue
  • Unreachable — System defaults to manual verification

Manual verification option: If AI cannot read the document, click Verify manually to enter data yourself. AI-extracted values pre-fill the form.

Step 2: Member Details

The second step collects employee information across three sections:

  • Employee Personal Details — Auto-populated from employee profile
  • Employee Document Details — Pre-filled by AI from uploaded documents (Emirates ID, Passport No, UID, Resident file number)
  • Work Details — Auto-populated from employee profile

Review each section, complete any missing fields, and verify AI-detected values.

Step 3: Additional Documents

The final step handles conditional and optional documents.

  • Conditional documents — Appear based on employee conditions (age, pregnancy, gender). Answer yes/no questions to determine if upload is required.
  • Optional documents — Not required but can be uploaded if available
  • Anything else? — Free-text field for special notes or additional context

Conditional logic: If you answer "Yes" to any conditional question, that document becomes mandatory. If all answers are "No", the upload section hides.

Save as Draft

At any step, click Save as draft to pause and return later.

Draft endorsements appear in the Additions tab with status "Draft". Click Continue submission to resume.

First Removal Request

To remove an employee from health insurance:

  1. Navigate to Health > Group insurance > Endorsements
  2. Click the Removals tab
  3. Click New removal (purple button, top-right)
  4. Select the employee from the list of insured employees

The removal form includes:

  • Removal Date (required) — Date picker + Reason for removal dropdown
  • Visa cancellation document — Upload visa cancellation proof
  • Insurance Card (conditional) — Upload card photo if returning physical card
  • Anything else? — Free-text field for notes

Removal reasons:

  • Transfer to new policy
  • Termination
  • Resignation with notice
  • Resignation without notice
  • End of contract

Click Save as draft or Submit removal request.

Adding Dependents

To add employee dependents to insurance:

  1. Navigate to the employee's profile
  2. Go to Dependents section > Insurance tab
  3. Click Insure Now
  4. Follow the same 3-step wizard as employee additions

Required documents for dependents:

  • Health card (if dependent is sponsored by employee)
  • Marriage certificate (for spouse)
  • Birth certificate (for children)

Document reuse: If documents were uploaded during employee profile creation, no need to re-upload.

Tracking Endorsements

After submission, track request status in the Endorsements table.

Table columns:

  • Name — Employee name (click to view profile)
  • Plan — Insurance policy name
  • Endorsement ref no. — Unique reference ID
  • Submitted — Submission date
  • Effective date — When coverage starts (info icon if date changes)
  • Status — Current stage (Draft, Under review, With insurer, Addition completed, Cancelled)
  • Actions — Available actions per status

Status-specific actions:

  • Draft — Continue submission, Delete draft
  • Under review — Cancel request
  • Addition completed — Download COI, Download Insurance card

Resubmitting Documents

If the endorsement team requests additional documents:

  1. Check email notification or Attention Required alert
  2. Click View Details on the endorsement
  3. Review feedback from the team
  4. Upload the required documents
  5. Click Submit documents

⚠️ Complete all requested documents: You must upload all documents specified in the feedback before resubmission.

Offboarded Employees

If an employee is offboarded but still appears on the insurance policy:

  1. Click the alert banner on the Removals tab: "You have offboarded employees who are still insured"
  2. View the list of offboarded employees
  3. Click Remove from policy to start a removal request
  4. Or click Mark as already removed if removal was handled outside the system

Marking Employees as Already Insured

If an employee is already insured but the policy is not assigned in Bayzat:

  1. Go to the employee's profile
  2. Click on Health Insurance
  3. Click Marked as already insured
  4. Select the relevant insurance plan
  5. Click Continue to assign the policy

Key Tasks

Task: Add Employee to Health Insurance

Submit a new employee addition request to assign an uninsured employee to a health insurance policy.

Subtask: Select Employee and Policy

  1. Navigate to Health → Group insurance → Endorsements
  2. Click "New addition" button (purple, top-right)
  3. Search or browse the list of uninsured employees
  4. Click "Add to insurance policy" for the target employee
  1. Select the appropriate policy from available plans (radio buttons show insurer and plan name)
  2. Click "Continue"

Subtask: Upload Required Documents (Step 1)

The 3-step wizard loads. Step 1 displays all required document sections with completion status indicators.

  • Document sections: Establishment, Insurance effective date, Visa Sponsor, Passport, Emirates ID, Visa, Change Status form
  • Status indicators: Complete (green), Not complete (grey), Attention required (red)
  • Each section is collapsible — expand to upload documents
  1. Expand each required document section
  2. Upload the relevant document file
  3. Verify status changes to "Complete"

AI Document Verification: When you upload a passport, visa, or Emirates ID, the system automatically extracts key data (passport number, expiry date, UID, file number). Review the extracted values before proceeding.

  1. Review AI-extracted data for accuracy
  2. Correct any extraction errors manually if needed
  3. Click "Continue" when all required documents are complete

⚠️ Expired Documents: If a document is expired, the section shows "Attention required" status. You can still proceed, but the endorsement team will flag this during review.

Subtask: Complete Member Details (Step 2)

Step 2 displays three sections with auto-populated data from the employee profile:

  • Employee Personal Details: Name, date of birth, gender, nationality, marital status
  • Employee Document Details: Emirates ID, passport number, UID, resident file number (pre-filled via AI extraction)
  • Work Details: Job title, department, hire date, salary
  1. Review auto-populated fields
  2. Fill in any missing required fields
  3. Verify all sections show "Complete" status
  4. Click "Continue"

Subtask: Add Additional Documents (Step 3)

Step 3 displays conditional and optional documents based on employee attributes and insurer requirements.

  • Conditional documents appear if the employee meets specific criteria (e.g., pregnancy, age, gender)
  • Optional documents can be uploaded if relevant
  • "Anything else?" section allows free-text notes and file uploads
  1. Answer any conditional questions (Yes/No format)
  2. If "Yes" to any question, upload the required document
  3. Add optional documents as needed
  4. Add notes in "Anything else?" if needed
  5. Click "Submit addition request"

Save as Draft: At any step, click "Save as draft" to pause and return later. Drafts appear in the Additions tab with a "Continue submission" button.

Task: Add Dependent to Health Insurance

Submit a dependent addition request to add a spouse or child to an employee's health insurance policy.

Subtask: Navigate to Dependent Insurance

  1. Navigate to the employee's profile
  2. Click the "Dependents" tab
  3. Select the dependent to insure
  4. Click the "Insurance" sub-tab
  5. Click "Insure Now"

The same 3-step wizard loads as for employee additions. Follow the steps above for uploading documents, completing details, and submitting.

Note: Dependent additions are NOT initiated from the main Endorsements page. You must access them via the employee's profile → Dependents tab.

Subtask: Upload Dependent-Specific Documents

Required documents for dependents vary by relationship type:

  • Spouse: Marriage certificate, passport, visa, Emirates ID, health card (if sponsored by employee)
  • Child: Birth certificate, passport, visa, Emirates ID (if applicable), health card (if sponsored by employee)

If documents were previously uploaded during employee profile creation, they auto-populate — no need to re-upload.

Task: Track Addition Request Status

Monitor the progress of a submitted addition request from submission through completion.

Subtask: View All Addition Requests

  1. Navigate to Health → Group insurance → Endorsements
  2. The Additions tab loads by default
  3. View table with columns: Name, Plan, Endorsement ref no., Submitted date, Effective date, Status, Actions

Status types:

  • Draft: Incomplete request (Continue submission / Delete draft buttons)
  • Under review: Submitted and being processed by endorsement team (Cancel request button)
  • With insurer: Sent to insurance provider for processing
  • Attention required: Additional documents or corrections needed (View Details button)
  • Addition completed: Employee added to policy (Download COI / Download Insurance card buttons)
  • Cancelled: Request cancelled by admin or system

Subtask: Search and Filter Requests

  1. Use the search bar to find requests by member name or endorsement reference number
  2. Click "Filters" to open the filter panel
  3. Filter by Status (Approved, Cancelled, Draft, Under Review, With Insurer)
  4. Filter by Submitted On date range
  5. Filter by Plan (dropdown shows all available policies)
  6. Click "Apply" to filter results

Subtask: Download Completed Addition Documents

Once an addition is completed, download the Certificate of Insurance (COI) and Insurance Card:

  1. Locate the completed addition in the Additions tab
  2. Click "COI" button to download the Certificate of Insurance
  3. Click "Insurance card" button to download the insurance card

COI Availability: Certificates of Insurance can be downloaded within 30 days of completion.

Subtask: View Employee Insurance Details

  1. Click the employee name link in any endorsement row
  2. The employee profile loads on the Health Insurance tab
  3. View policy name, key benefits (Annual limit, Out-patient coinsurance, Prescriptions), and effective dates
  4. Available actions: Download insurance card, Mark as incorrect, Remove from policy

Task: Remove Employee from Health Insurance

Submit a removal request to remove an employee from their health insurance policy.

Subtask: Initiate Removal Request

  1. Navigate to Health → Group insurance → Endorsements
  2. Click the "Removals" tab
  3. Click "New removal" button (purple, top-right)
  1. Search or browse the list of employees with active policies
  2. Click "Remove from insurance policy" for the target employee

Note: To remove a dependent, go to the employee's profile → Dependents tab → select dependent → Insurance → Remove from policy.

Subtask: Complete Removal Form

The removal form displays four sections:

  • Removal Date (Required): Date picker and reason dropdown
  • Visa cancellation document: Upload visa cancellation proof
  • Insurance Card (Conditional): Return physical card if applicable
  • Anything else: Free-text notes and optional file uploads
  1. Expand the "Removal Date" section
  2. Select the removal effective date
  3. Choose a reason from the dropdown

Removal reasons:

  • Transfer to a new policy
  • Termination
  • Resignation with notice
  • Resignation without notice
  • End of contract
  1. Upload visa cancellation document if required by insurer
  2. If the employee has a physical insurance card, mark "Insurance Card" section and upload proof of return
  3. Add any notes or special instructions in "Anything else?"
  4. Click "Submit removal request" or "Save as draft"

⚠️ Removing Employees with Dependents: When removing an employee who has dependents on the same policy, you must upload documents for both the employee and all dependents. Dependents cannot remain on a policy after the principal member is removed.

Approval Flow

How Endorsements Requests Route

Addition and removal requests go through an internal review process before reaching the insurer. HR administrators submit requests, which are reviewed by the HR team for completeness and accuracy before being forwarded to the insurance provider.

Types Requiring Approval

Both addition and removal requests require internal review before submission to the insurer. Requests with incomplete or expired documents trigger an "Attention required" status, requiring correction before the review process can continue.

Routing Examples

How different endorsement requests route through approvers:

Example 1: New Employee Addition

HR admin submits addition with all required documents → HR team reviews for completeness → Approved request moves to "Under review" status → Insurer processes addition.

Example 2: Removal Request with Missing Documents

HR admin submits removal without insurance card → System flags as "Attention required" → HR admin uploads missing document → HR team reviews → Request proceeds to insurer.

Example 3: Dependent Addition

HR admin submits dependent addition via employee profile → HR team verifies relationship documentation → Approved request routes to insurer → Certificate of Insurance generated upon completion.

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

Document Requirements

Document requirements vary by insurance provider, policy type, and emirate location. Each configuration determines which documents are mandatory, conditional, or optional.

Configuration Aspect Rule Impact
Document Order Standardized sequence: 1. Passport 2. Visa 3. Emirates ID 4. Others Consistent user experience across all providers
Provider Configuration Only one configuration per policy type per insurer Cannot create duplicate configurations
Document Type Usage Each document type can only be used once per configuration Prevents duplicate document requests
Conditional Documents Conditional documents make related detail fields optional Optional passport document = optional passport number field
Predefined Sections Fixed sections: Passport, Passport Photo, Visa, Change Status, Emirates ID Cannot modify predefined section names

AI Verification Rules

AI document verification automates data extraction but has specific fallback behaviors and known limitations.

⚠️ Syrian Passport MRZ Issues

Syrian passports have known MRZ (Machine Readable Zone) issues where passport number appears in unusual location. AI may fail to extract correctly. Use manual verification option if extraction fails.

Scenario System Behavior User Action Required
Claude/AI Unreachable Auto-defaults to manual verification method Complete document details manually
Wrong Document Type Shows "Verify manually" option Re-upload correct document or verify manually
Name/Sponsor Mismatch Shows "Ignore and continue" option with delay warning Correct data or proceed with warning (may cause processing delays)
Unreadable Document Prompts re-upload or manual verification Upload clearer image or enter data manually
Document Pre-uploaded AI verification starts on page load (except Visa component) No action needed unless verification fails

Sponsor Matching: For employee additions only (not dependents), system compares establishment card sponsor with visa sponsor. Mismatch triggers warning but allows continuation.

Conditional Document Logic

Documents can become mandatory based on employee conditions. Questions determine whether conditional documents are required.

Rule Constraint Behavior
Question Eligibility Questions only for "Not Required" documents Cannot add conditional questions to documents already marked "Required"
Answer Format Yes/No answers only Binary responses determine document requirement
Mandatory Trigger One "Yes" answer = document becomes mandatory User must upload document if any condition is met
Gender-based Visibility Questions assigned to Female only visible for Female employees Male employees do not see Female-specific questions
Status Display Conditional documents show "Not Complete" status when questions present Optional documents (no conditions) show no status

Date Field Validation

Date fields have specific validation rules to prevent invalid endorsement submissions.

Field Validation Rule Constraint
COC Date Today or past date only Cannot select future dates
Visa Issue Date Today or past date only Cannot select future dates
Date of Marriage Today or past date only; only displays if member is married Field visibility depends on marital status
Date Entered Country Today or past date only Cannot select future dates
Date of Hire Within 30 days future or past Limited range to prevent data entry errors

Date of Hire Behavior: Field shows prefilled value from employee profile but changes do not overwrite database value.

Troubleshooting & Edge Cases

Common Issues

Issue Cause Resolution
AI Verification Fails Document unreadable, wrong document type, or AI service timeout Click "Verify manually" to open document upload component with pre-filled AI data. Review and complete missing fields manually.
Cannot Create Configuration Policy type already has configuration for this provider Edit existing configuration or delete it first (deletion requires confirmation dialog).
Document Status Not Changing Document removed after upload, or upload incomplete Status reverts to "Not Complete" when document removed. Re-upload document to mark complete.
Conditional Document Required Unexpectedly User answered "Yes" to at least one conditional question Review answers to conditional questions. If "Yes" is correct, upload required document. If incorrect, change answer to "No".
Endorsements Menu Not Visible Company has no relevant insurance policies, or broker field not set to Arya/Prime Verify company has active policies with Arya Insurance Brokerage or Prime Insurance Brokers.

Edge Cases

  • Document Pre-uploaded: If document already exists in employee profile, AI verification starts automatically on page load (except for Visa component). No user action needed unless verification fails.
  • All Conditional Questions Answered "No": If user answers "No" to all conditional questions, document upload component hides (document not required). No "Not Complete" status shown.
  • Multiple Conditional Documents: Each conditional document may have different questions and conditions. Answer questions independently for each document.
  • Conditional Text Without Questions: If document has conditional guidance text but no questions, guidance displays but no "Not Complete" status appears (document remains optional).
  • Document Removal After Upload: When uploaded document is removed, status must revert to "Not Complete" immediately. Re-upload required to mark complete.
  • Provider Configuration Variations: Required document list varies by insurance provider configuration. Same document may be required for one provider but optional for another.
  • Employee Profile Gaps: Not all fields may be available for auto-population from employee profile. Missing fields must be completed manually during endorsement submission.
  • OCR Detection Failure: Fields not detected by OCR remain empty. User must enter data manually. Pre-filled AI data appears in manual verification form for review.
  • Step Navigation: Users must complete each step before proceeding to next. Incomplete steps prevent continuation.
  • Date of Marriage Visibility: Field only displays if member marital status is set to married. Not visible for single employees.
  • Predefined Section Marked Conditional: If predefined section (Passport, Visa, Emirates ID, etc.) is marked conditional but user expects it to be required, review provider configuration. Admin may need to update requirement status.
  • Cut Card Copy Upload: Uploading cut card copy automatically affects "Card collected" field value. Field updates without explicit user action.
  • Date of Hire Changes: Date of hire field shows prefilled value from employee profile, but changes made during endorsement do not overwrite the database value. This is display-only behavior.

Configuration Edge Cases

  • Deleting Configuration: Deletion requires warning dialog confirmation. Cannot be undone. Ensure configuration is no longer needed before deleting.
  • Document Set as Conditional But Field Still Required: If document is conditional but related detail field remains required, review configuration logic. Conditional documents should make related fields optional.
  • New Configuration Disabled by Default: When creating new endorsement configuration, it is disabled by default. Must explicitly enable after setup to make it active.
  • Default Configuration Handling: All providers have default config called "UAE - Dubai, RAK, Al Fujeirah, Sharjah". Existing settings preserved in this default when creating new emirate-specific configurations.
  • Multiple Configurations Per Provider: Same provider may need different requirements for Abu Dhabi vs Dubai. Each emirate can have separate configuration.

Error Messages

Error Message Principle: All error messages must explain that not correcting the issue will cause processing delays. Users need to understand consequences of proceeding with warnings.

  • Name Mismatch: "Name on document does not match employee profile. Proceeding without correction may delay endorsement processing."
  • Sponsor Mismatch: "Visa sponsor does not match establishment card. Proceeding without correction may delay endorsement processing."
  • Unreadable Document: "Document quality is too low for automatic verification. Please re-upload a clearer image or verify manually."
  • Wrong Document Type: "Uploaded document does not match expected type. Please upload the correct document or verify manually."

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I resubmit documents after receiving feedback?

Check your email for the notification, locate the 'Attention Required' alert in Endorsements, click 'View Details', review the feedback, upload the required documents, and submit.

Can I save a draft endorsement request to complete later?

Yes, you can save endorsement requests as drafts at any stage and return to complete the submission when ready.

What happens to dependents when I remove an employee from insurance?

Dependents must be removed along with the principal employee. Upload all required documents for both the employee and their dependents when submitting the removal request.

How do I track the status of my endorsement request?

Navigate to Endorsements, select the Additions or Removals tab, and hover over the request status to view progress through the stages: Under Review, With Insurer, Attention Required, or Completed.

Can I add employee dependents directly from their profile?

Yes, go to the employee's Dependents section, navigate to Insurance, click 'Insure Now', upload required documents, add member details, and submit the endorsement request.

What should I do if the effective date changes?

An info icon will appear next to the date. Hover over it to see the reason for the change. If the turnaround time is exceeded, contact endorsements@bayzat.com with the employee and company name.

Do I need to re-upload documents if they were already provided during employee onboarding?

No, if documents were uploaded during employee profile creation, you do not need to re-upload them for endorsement requests.

What is the difference between removing an employee and offboarding them?

Removing an employee takes them off the insurance policy. Offboarding additionally removes their Bayzat access after the departure date. Offboarding is available when the removal reason is not 'Transferring to New Policy'.

How long does it take to download a Certificate of Insurance?

Certificates of Insurance can be downloaded within 30 days of the endorsement being completed.

Can I assign an employee to an existing policy if they're already insured?

Yes, go to the employee profile, click Health Insurance, select 'Marked as already insured', choose the relevant insurance plan, and click Continue to assign the policy.
