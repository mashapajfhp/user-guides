What is Air Ticket?

Overview

Air Ticket is a comprehensive feature within Bayzat that enables organizations to manage employee air ticket allowances efficiently. It provides a flexible system for creating customizable policies, tracking requests, and offering multiple redemption options—whether through reimbursement, company-booked tickets, or encashment of allowances.

Air Ticket simplifies the management of employee travel allowances by enabling organizations to create and manage air ticket policies with customizable eligibility criteria, allowance amounts, and redemption options. Employees can request air tickets through the Bayzat mobile app using three different methods: reimbursement for tickets they've already purchased, ticket booking where HR/Admin handles the purchase, or encashment of their air ticket allowance.

Key Benefits

  • Flexible redemption options that give employees choice in how they use their allowances
  • Customizable policies tailored to different employee levels and regions
  • Mobile-first request submission through the Bayzat app
  • Automated workflows for encashment processing
  • Transparent control over travel allowance spending
  • Reduced administrative overhead through clear approval processes

Important Constraints

Policy Enablement Required: Air ticket request options must be explicitly enabled by the admin before employees can submit any requests. Without this enablement, the 'Request Air Ticket' option will not appear in the mobile app.

Encashment Leave Restriction: Employees cannot request encashment of their air ticket allowance if they are currently taking leaves. Plan encashment timing accordingly.

One Policy Per Employee: Each employee can be assigned to only one air ticket policy. Ensure your policy structure accounts for different employee groups before assigning.

Tip: Define your policy structure (by seniority, location, role, etc.) before creating policies. This prevents the need to reassign employees later, which requires creating new policies.

Setup Process

Initial Configuration

Before employees can request air tickets, HR administrators must create and configure air ticket allowance policies. This involves defining policy parameters, eligibility criteria, and redemption options.

Step-by-Step Setup

1

Navigate to Air Ticket Policy Settings

From the main Settings menu, select Company, then click Air ticket policies. Click 'Add New' to start creating a policy.

2

Enter Basic Policy Details

Enter a descriptive policy name and define the allowance amount per cycle. Specify the number of eligible people (employee only or including family members).

3

Configure Policy Behavior

Define request timing (when employees can request tickets), set policy renewal cycle (annual, bi-annual, etc.), and specify the number of allowed requests per cycle.

4

Choose Redemption Options

Select which redemption methods employees can use:

  • Reimbursement: Employees submit receipts for reimbursement
  • Ticket Booking: HR/Admin books tickets on behalf of employees
  • Encashment: Employees receive cash equivalent (only if not taking leaves)
5

Select Eligible Employees

Click on 'Select Eligible Employees' section. Choose individual employees or use filters (department, location, seniority level). Note: Each employee can only be assigned to one policy.

6

Review and Finalize Policy

Review all settings including policy name, allowance amount, behavior settings, redemption options, and eligible employees. Click 'Save' or 'Activate' to finalize the policy.

Feature Usage

Common Workflows

Air ticket management involves three primary workflows: employees requesting tickets through the mobile app, administrators managing policies and approvals, and automated encashment processing through workflows.

Reimbursement Request

Employees who have purchased tickets submit details and receipts through the mobile app. HR verifies the receipt and amount before processing reimbursement payment.

Ticket Booking Request

Employees request HR/Admin to book tickets on their behalf. They share travel dates, destination, and passenger details. HR coordinates and completes the booking.

Encashment Request

Employees convert their allowance to cash. Must confirm they are not taking any leaves. Encashment is processed through Bayzat Payroll.

Usage Example: Request Air Ticket as Employee

To submit an air ticket request through the mobile app:

  1. Open Bayzat mobile app
  2. Navigate to Work section
  3. Click 'Request Air Ticket' button
  4. Choose request type: Reimbursement, Ticket Booking, or Encashment
  5. For Reimbursement: Provide ticket details and upload receipt
  6. For Ticket Booking: Share relevant travel details with HR team
  7. For Encashment: Confirm you are not taking any leaves
  8. Submit request for approval
  9. Track request status on the second screen

Usage Example: Set Up Automatic Encashment Workflow

To configure an automated workflow for air ticket encashment:

  1. Log in to Bayzat admin dashboard
  2. Navigate to Workflows in Settings
  3. Click 'Create New Workflow'
  4. Select 'Air Tickets Auto Encashment' recipe
  5. Define workflow trigger date and time (based on UAE or KSA account location)
  6. Set encashment criteria
  7. Choose application: Bayzat Payroll
  8. Select encashment request type (fully approved or requiring approvals)
  9. Assign employee for the request
  10. Save workflow and verify activation in activity log

Edge Cases

Important: When changing policies mid-cycle, employees may have existing requests or partially used allowances. Communicate changes clearly and plan transitions during renewal periods when possible.

  • New employee mid-cycle: Assign to policy immediately; allowance will be available for the remaining cycle period
  • Employee transfer between departments: May require policy reassignment if allowances differ by department
  • Leave overlapping with encashment: Encashment cannot be processed; employee must complete or cancel leave first
  • Multiple dependents: Remember allowance is collective, not individual per dependent

Support Resources

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the three ways employees can request air tickets?

Employees can request air tickets through three methods: (1) Reimbursement - submit receipts for reimbursement of ticket costs, (2) Ticket Booking - request HR/Admin to book tickets on their behalf, or (3) Encashment - receive cash equivalent of their air ticket allowance.

Can an employee be assigned to multiple air ticket policies?

No, employees cannot be assigned to multiple air ticket policies. Each employee can only have one active policy. To change an employee's policy, remove them from the current policy before assigning to a new one.

What happens if a ticket request exceeds the allowance?

The system will not allow employees to request tickets exceeding their allotted allowance. Employees must reduce the ticket amount or contact HR to discuss allowance adjustments.

When can employees request encashment?

Encashment is only possible if the employee is not taking any leaves. Ensure no active or pending leave requests before submitting an encashment request.

Can I edit a policy after requests are submitted?

No, active policies cannot be edited once ticket requests are raised. To make changes, create a new policy with desired modifications and assign it to employees going forward.

How do I set up automated encashment?

Use Settings > Workflows to create a new workflow. Select the 'Air Tickets Auto Encashment' recipe, define trigger time and criteria, and activate. Requests will be generated automatically based on your schedule.
