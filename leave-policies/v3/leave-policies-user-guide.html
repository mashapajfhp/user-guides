What is Leave Policies?

Overview

Leave Policies define how employees accrue, request, and consume time off. Configure paid, partially paid, and unpaid leave types with automatic payroll integration. Accessed via Settings → Leaves → Leave Policies.

Create policies from scratch or use UAE/KSA labor law templates. Assign to employees individually or in bulk. Edit allowances, pay rates, and restrictions per policy.

Key Benefits

  • Automatic tenure-based allowance adjustments eliminate manual policy switching
  • Conditional pay rates (full/partial/unpaid) trigger automatic payroll deductions
  • Pre-built UAE and KSA templates ensure labor law compliance
  • Monthly accrual pausing during unpaid leave maintains accurate balances
  • Calendar vs working day calculations match company work week configuration
  • Leave salary and encashment toggles enable advance payments and end-of-service calculations

Strategic Context

🎯 What Leave Policies Solves

Eliminates manual tracking of accrued leave days and spreadsheet-based payroll deductions for unpaid leave.

  • Replaces Excel-based leave allowance calculations and manual policy updates when employees cross tenure thresholds
  • Standardizes leave entitlements across departments and employee types with reusable policy templates
  • Enables automatic salary deductions when employees take unpaid or partially paid leave

💰 Why It Matters

Leave policies are the foundation for time-off management, payroll accuracy, and labor law compliance.

  • Prerequisite for leave requests, leave salary payments, and leave encashment at end-of-service
  • Drives payroll deductions for unpaid leave and calculates prorated salaries during leave periods
  • Controls employee leave balances, carryover rules, and negative balance permissions

🏗️ How It Connects

Configuration template assigned to employees that governs leave request behavior and payroll integration.

  • Assigned via employee profile Leaves tab or bulk upload during onboarding
  • Referenced by leave requests, approval flows, payroll adjustments, and end-of-service calculations
  • One policy can affect hundreds of employees across multiple leave cycles

Product Foundation Overview

How Leave Policies Fits

Leave Policies are configuration templates that define time-off rules for your organization. Each policy acts as a reusable blueprint specifying allowances, accrual rates, pay rules, and eligibility conditions.

Mental model: Leave Policy (template) → Assigned to Employees → Governs leave requests → Drives payroll deductions and balances

One policy can affect hundreds of employees across departments, making it a foundational configuration layer for workforce management.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating leave policies:

  • What leave types does your organization need? — Annual, sick, maternity, unpaid, or custom types
  • How will allowances accrue? — Upfront allocation vs monthly accrual vs tenure-based tiers
  • What are the pay rules? — Fully paid, partially paid (conditional rates), or unpaid with automatic deductions
  • Who is eligible? — All employees, specific departments, probation restrictions, or tenure thresholds

Initial Setup & Configuration

First-Time Setup Checklist

Complete these foundational steps before creating your first leave policy. Skipping prerequisites leads to incorrect calculations and broken approval routing.

1. Configure Company Work Week

Define your organization's working days to enable accurate working-day calculations for leave policies.

  • Go to Settings → Company → Work Week
  • Select which days are working days (e.g., Sunday to Thursday)
  • Save configuration

Why this matters: Leave policies can calculate days as calendar days or working days. Without a defined work week, working-day policies cannot function correctly.

2. Set Up Public Holiday Calendar

Create a holiday calendar to exclude public holidays from leave calculations when needed.

  • Navigate to Settings → Company → Holiday Calendars
  • Add public holidays for your region
  • Name the calendar (e.g., "UAE Public Holidays 2026")
  • Assign calendar to relevant employees

3. Define Organizational Hierarchy

Set up reporting lines to enable leave approval workflows.

  • Go to each employee's profile → Work tab
  • Set the "Reports to" field to assign their line manager
  • Alternatively, use bulk upload via Excel for multiple employees

⚠️ Required for approvals: Leave requests cannot be approved without defined reporting lines. Managers must have Line Manager permissions assigned in Role Management.

4. Configure Leave Approval Flow

Define who approves leave requests and in what order.

  • Navigate to Settings → Approval Flows → Leaves
  • Add approval steps (e.g., Line Manager → Department Head → HR)
  • Choose whether approvers act sequentially or in parallel
  • Save the approval flow

5. Review Leave Cycle Settings

Decide whether your organization uses calendar-year or individual (hire-date-based) leave cycles.

  • Calendar year: All employees' leave resets on January 1st
  • Individual cycle: Each employee's leave resets on their hire anniversary

⚠️ Cannot change easily: Switching leave cycle types after setup requires configuration team intervention and results in historical data loss. Choose carefully before creating policies.

What Not to Configure Yet

The following configurations are conditional and only needed for specific sub-features:

  • Payroll Module: Only required if you plan to use unpaid leave deductions. Not needed for basic paid leave tracking.
  • Employee Salary Configuration: Only required for unpaid leave policies that automatically calculate payroll deductions.
  • Leave Encashment Settings: Only required if employees can request to encash unused leave. Configure this later when setting up encashment policies.
  • Leave Salary Settings: Only required if you pay employees in advance for leave periods.

Start simple: You can create and use basic paid leave policies (e.g., annual leave, sick leave) with just the required configurations above. Add conditional features later as your needs grow.

Policy/Template Setup

Create leave policies to define entitlements, pay rates, and rules for different leave types.

Settings → Leaves → Leave Policies → Add New

Choose Starting Point

Select how to create your policy:

  • Start from scratch: Build a custom policy with full control over all settings
  • Use UAE templates: 8 pre-configured policies compliant with UAE labor law (Annual Leave, Sick Leave, Maternity, etc.)
  • Use KSA templates: 10 pre-configured policies compliant with KSA labor law

Step 1: Setup - Policy Details

  • Policy Name: Internal name for the policy (e.g., "Annual Leave UAE")
  • Description: Explain what this policy covers and who it applies to
  • Leave Type: Select from predefined types or create custom

Step 1: Setup - Leave Allowance

Define how many days employees receive and when they receive them:

  • Allowance Type: Annually recurring (resets each cycle) or One-time (does not renew)
  • Annual Days: Total days per year (e.g., 30 calendar days or 22 working days)
  • Day Type: Calendar days (includes weekends/holidays) or Working days (business days only)
  • Accrual Method: Full allowance at start of cycle or Monthly accrual (days/12 per month)

Step 1: Setup - Conditional Allowances

Set different allowances based on employee tenure or probation status:

  • Probation Restriction: Block leave requests during probation period
  • Tenure-Based Tiers: Increase allowance after X months/years of service

Step 1: Setup - Leave Pay Rate

Define whether employees receive salary during leave:

  • Paid Leave: Full salary paid during leave
  • Partially Paid: Percentage of salary paid (e.g., 50% for days 16-45 of sick leave)
  • Unpaid Leave: No salary paid, automatic payroll deduction created

For conditional pay rates (e.g., sick leave fully paid for 15 days, half pay for next 30 days), add up to 5 pay rate conditions based on total days taken.

⚠️ Payroll Module Required: Unpaid and partially paid leave policies require the Payroll module to be enabled. Employee salaries must be configured for automatic deductions to work.

Step 1: Setup - Leave Salary & Encashment

Enable optional features for paid leave policies:

  • Leave Salary: Allow employees to request advance salary for upcoming leave (common in UAE/KSA for blue-collar workers traveling home)
  • Leave Encashment: Allow employees to cash out unused leave days instead of taking time off

These toggles are only available for Paid leave policies. Unpaid leave cannot have salary or encashment enabled.

Step 1: Setup - Policy Restrictions

  • Negative Balance: Allow employees to take leave before it accrues (goes into negative balance)
  • Carryover: Allow unused days to roll over to next cycle (with optional maximum carryover limit)
  • Public Holiday Exclusion: Automatically exclude public holidays from leave day count when they fall within leave period
  • Restrict Editing: Prevent employees from manually changing computed leave days in their requests
  • Pause Accrual: Stop monthly accrual during specific leave types (e.g., pause accrual during unpaid leave exceeding 21 days)

Step 2: Add Employees

Assign the policy to employees who should receive this leave entitlement:

  • Select individual employees from the list
  • Use "Move All" to assign all employees at once
  • Filter by department, office, or employment status to assign in bulk

Step 3: Employee Details

Review and adjust individual employee allowances:

  • Allowance Override: Manually set different allowance for specific employees
  • Pro-rated Days: Adjust for mid-cycle hires or partial year eligibility
  • Used Days: Enter historical leave already taken if migrating from another system
  • Carryover Days: Add unused days from previous cycle

Step 4: Review & Save

Confirm all policy details and employee assignments. Policies are saved as Draft status initially.

  • Save as Draft: Policy is saved but not active. Employees cannot see or use it yet.
  • Publish: Activate the policy immediately. Employees can now request leave under this policy.

Assignment & Activation

After creating a policy, assign it to employees and activate it for use.

Manual Assignment

Assign policies individually through employee profiles:

  1. Navigate to Company → All Employees → [Employee Name] → Leaves tab
  2. Click "Setup" next to the policy you want to activate
  3. Confirm allowance details and save

Bulk Assignment

Assign policies to multiple employees at once:

  1. Edit the policy from Settings → Leaves → Leave Policies
  2. Go to Step 2: Add Employees
  3. Select employees or use "Move All"
  4. Save changes

Publishing Policies

Policies must be Published for employees to see and use them:

  • Draft policies: Visible only to admins, can be edited or deleted freely
  • Published policies: Active and visible to assigned employees, can be edited but not deleted if employees have taken leave

To publish a Draft policy, click the Edit icon and change status to Published.

What Happens After Publishing: Assigned employees immediately see the policy in their Bayzat app under Time → My Leaves. They can view their balance and submit leave requests. If monthly accrual is enabled, the system begins accruing days automatically.

Task: Create and Configure Leave Policies

Set up leave policies that define allowances, pay rates, and rules for employee time off.

Subtask: Create Leave Policy from Template

  1. Navigate to Settings → Leaves → Leave Policies
  2. Click Add new button at bottom of policies table
  3. Select Start from a template
  4. Choose region: UAE (8 templates) or KSA (10 templates)
  5. Select template matching your leave type
  6. Review pre-filled configuration and adjust as needed
  7. Proceed through 4-step wizard: Setup → Add employees → Employee details → Review & save

Subtask: Create Leave Policy from Scratch

  1. Click Add new → Start from scratch
  2. Enter policy name and description
  3. Configure leave allowance: annual days, calculation method (calendar/working days)
  4. Set leave pay rate: Paid, Partially paid, or Unpaid
  5. Enable optional features: Leave salary toggle, Leave encashment toggle
  6. Configure policy restrictions: Restrict during probation, Restrict editing of leave days
  7. Assign employees to policy
  8. Review and save

Subtask: Configure Paid Leave Policy

Employees receive full salary during leave.

  • Set Leave pay rate: Paid
  • Define annual allowance (e.g., 30 calendar days or 22 working days)
  • Enable carry over if unused days roll to next cycle
  • Set maximum carry over days

Subtask: Configure Partially Paid Leave Policy

Employees receive reduced salary based on days taken.

  1. Set Leave pay rate: Partially paid
  2. Add pay rate conditions (up to 5 conditions supported)
  3. For each condition, specify: number of days taken, pay rate percentage
  4. Example: First 15 days = 100% pay, Next 30 days = 50% pay, Remaining days = 0% pay
  5. Set daily wage calculation: Basic only OR Basic + Allowances

Subtask: Configure Unpaid Leave Policy

Employees receive no salary during leave. Payroll deductions are automatic when Payroll module is enabled.

  • Set Leave pay rate: Unpaid
  • Configure daily wage calculation for deduction: Basic salary ÷ 30 days OR (Basic + Allowances) ÷ 30 days
  • System automatically creates payroll deductions when leave requests are fully approved
  • Deductions appear in Finance → Adjustments tab

Subtask: Configure Accrual Leave Policy

Leave days accumulate monthly based on annual allowance.

  • Enable monthly accrual in policy setup
  • Set annual allowance (system divides by 12 for monthly accrual)
  • Configure accrual pausing: select leave types that pause monthly accrual (e.g., unpaid leave)
  • Set minimum days threshold for pausing (e.g., pause if unpaid leave exceeds 21 days)
  • Enable negative balance if employees can request more than accrued

Subtask: Configure Tenure-Based Leave Allowance

Leave allowance adjusts automatically based on employee total job tenure.

  • Enable dynamic allowance conditions
  • Add tenure tiers: Probation, 1-3 years, 3-5 years, 5+ years
  • Set allowance per tier (e.g., 22 days for 1-3 years, 30 days for 5+ years)
  • System auto-adjusts when employee crosses tenure threshold

Workflow Integration

Real-World Workflow Examples

Automate leave-related actions when specific events occur:

Example: Auto-notify HR when leave balance depletes

  • Trigger: Leave request approved
  • Condition: Remaining leave balance falls below 2 days
  • Action: Send email notification to HR admin
  • Result: HR proactively manages employee leave planning before balance exhaustion

Example: Payroll deduction for unpaid leave

  • Trigger: Unpaid leave request approved
  • Condition: Leave type is marked as unpaid
  • Action: Create pay adjustment entry in payroll table
  • Result: Salary deductions calculated automatically without manual payroll edits

Example: Manager alert for team coverage gaps

  • Trigger: Leave request submitted
  • Condition: 3+ team members already on leave during requested dates
  • Action: Send alert to line manager
  • Result: Manager reviews coverage before approving additional leave

Scenarios Solved by Workflows

Scenario Without Workflow With Workflow
Employee takes unpaid leave HR manually calculates deduction, adds adjustment to payroll table, risks calculation errors System auto-creates pay adjustment when unpaid leave approved
Leave balance expires at year-end HR manually reviews balances, sends individual emails, employees lose days without warning System sends reminders 30 days before expiry to employees and HR
Maternity leave approved HR manually updates payroll, benefits, and leave accrual settings across multiple screens System pauses leave accrual, adjusts salary components, notifies benefits admin
Leave request conflicts with public holiday Manager manually checks calendar, recalculates days, requests employee to resubmit System auto-adjusts leave days to exclude public holidays, notifies employee of updated duration
Employee exceeds negative balance limit Approval goes through, HR discovers overage during payroll, manually corrects records System blocks request or routes to HR for exception approval
Leave encashment request submitted HR manually verifies eligibility, calculates amount, creates payroll addition System validates eligibility, calculates encashment value, creates pay adjustment

Additional Workflow Opportunities

If you find yourself thinking "I wish the system would automatically do X when Y happens," that is a workflow opportunity:

Opportunity 1: Auto-update employee records during long-term leave

When extended leave (maternity, sabbatical) is approved, automatically update employee status to "On Leave," pause performance review cycles, and notify department head.

Opportunity 2: Cascade leave policy changes to active employees

When a leave policy is updated mid-cycle, trigger notifications to all employees assigned to that policy, explaining balance adjustments and new rules.

Opportunity 3: Sync leave data to external systems

When leave is approved, call webhook to update external workforce planning tools, time tracking systems, or ERP platforms.

Approval Flow

How Leave Policies Requests Route

Leave requests, leave salary requests, and leave encashment requests require approval before processing. Default approvers include Super Admins, Line Managers, Payroll admins, and Transaction processing admins, depending on request type. Advanced flows allow conditional routing based on employee attributes, leave duration, or policy type.

Types Requiring Approval

Three leave-related request types have approval flows configured:

  • Leave Requests — Standard time-off requests submitted by employees
  • Leave Salary Requests — Requests for salary payment during leave periods
  • Leave Encashment Requests — Requests to convert unused leave days to cash payment

Routing Examples

How different leave requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Standard leave request

Employee submits annual leave request → Routes to Line Manager OR Super Admin (1 approval required) → If approved, leave balance deducted and calendar updated

Example 2: Leave salary request

Employee requests salary payment during unpaid leave → Routes to Payroll admin OR Super Admin OR Transaction processing admin (1 approval required) → If approved, pay adjustment created in payroll table

Example 3: Leave encashment request

Employee requests to encash 10 unused leave days → Routes to Payroll admin OR Transaction processing admin OR Super Admin (1 approval required) → If approved, encashment amount calculated and added to next payroll cycle

Example 4: Advanced flow with conditions

Employee submits leave exceeding 5 days → Routes first to Line Manager → If duration >10 days, escalates to Department Head → If approved at all levels, HR admin receives notification for workforce planning

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

Policy Configuration Constraints

⚠️ Leave pay rate cannot be changed for active policies

Once a leave policy is active and assigned to employees, the pay rate classification (Paid/Unpaid/Partially Paid) becomes locked. To change the pay rate, create a new policy and migrate employees.

By Design: This restriction prevents unintended changes to employee leave balances and payroll calculations mid-cycle.

⚠️ Accrual Frequency Cannot Be Modified After Creation

After creating a leave policy, you cannot change its accrual frequency (e.g., from monthly to upfront allocation). To implement a different accrual schedule, create a new policy and migrate employees.

⚠️ Conditional Pay Rate Requires Employee Removal

Enabling conditional pay rate features on existing policies requires removing all assigned employees first. Plan the migration carefully to avoid disrupting leave balances.

⚠️ Leave Cycle Type Change Causes Data Loss

Switching between calendar-year and hire-date-based leave cycles requires configuration team intervention and results in loss of historical leave data. Export all leave balance reports before requesting a cycle type change.

Leave Balance & Data Integrity Risks

⚠️ Data Loss Risk When Unassigning Policies

Removing employees from a policy and reassigning them can erase historical leave balances. The system lacks safeguards against this data loss. Always export leave balance reports before making policy changes.

⚠️ No Mid-Month Prorated Accrual

Leave accrues only for complete months worked. Employees leaving mid-month do not receive prorated accrual for partial months, even if they work most of the month.

Edge Cases

Multi-Month Unpaid Leave Deductions

When unpaid leave spans multiple calendar months, system creates separate deductions per payroll month. Email notifications delayed until each month opens.

Example: Employee takes unpaid leave from June 25 to July 5. System creates two deductions: one for June (6 days) and one for July (5 days). July deduction notification sent when July payroll month opens.

Sick Leave Partial Pay Calculation

UAE and KSA sick leave policies require different pay rates based on cumulative days taken in the leave cycle.

Example: Employee takes 5 days sick leave (full pay), then 10 more days later in cycle. First 10 total days at full pay, next 5 days at half pay. System automatically calculates applicable rate based on policy configuration.

Leave Spanning Public Holidays

When public holidays fall within leave period, system behavior depends on policy configuration (include/exclude holidays from leave balance).

Note: If public holidays updated after leave approval, system may not automatically recalculate leave days. HR receives email notification of affected requests.

Tenure-Based Allowance Threshold Crossing

When employee crosses tenure threshold mid-cycle (e.g., 1 year to 5 years), allowance adjusts automatically based on policy dynamic allowance configuration.

Limitation: No automatic notification sent to employee or HR when allowance changes. HR must manually track tenure milestones.

Negative Balance Requests

If policy allows negative balance, employees can request leave exceeding current balance. Future accruals will offset negative balance.

⚠️ Offboarding Risk: Offboarding employees can exploit negative balance to take leave beyond their accrued entitlement. HR must manually monitor and reject such requests.

Carry-Over Expiration Without Notification

If policy limits carry-over days (e.g., max 5 days carried to next cycle), excess days expire at cycle end without automatic notification.

Workaround: HR must manually generate reports before cycle end to identify employees at risk of losing leave days. Encourage employees to use or encash excess leave before expiration.

Leave Salary with Prorated Monthly Salary

When employee takes leave in same month as hire/termination, leave salary calculation uses prorated monthly salary based on days worked.

Example: Employee hired June 10, takes leave June 20-25. Leave salary calculated using prorated salary for June 10-19 and June 26-30 only.

Partial Day Leave with Attendance Integration

Half-day leave may not reconcile correctly with attendance check-in/check-out records.

⚠️ Known Issue: Employees who take half-day leave cannot check in for remaining hours. Attendance system treats entire day as leave. Manual adjustment required for accurate work hour tracking.

Historical Date Leave Requests

Leave requests with past dates may be restricted based on company policy or require special permissions.

Note: Validation rules for historical dates not fully documented. Test in your environment to determine allowed backdating window.

Bulk Leave Balance Corrections

System does not support bulk update of leave balances for manual corrections (e.g., after migration or policy changes).

Workaround: Adjust balances individually via employee profile, or contact support for assistance with bulk corrections.

Leap Year Hire Dates

Employees hired on February 29 have their leave cycle start date set to February 28 in non-leap years, causing a 1-day discrepancy in conditional leave allowance calculations.

Workaround: Manually adjust leave balances at cycle start to account for the missing day, or configure policy with 1 additional day allowance for affected employees.

Shift-Based Working Days

Working days leave policies cannot automatically exclude scheduled shift days-off. Employees with rotating shifts may see incorrect leave day counts.

Workaround: Use calendar days policy instead, or manually adjust leave balances to account for shift patterns. Document shift schedules for reference during leave approval.

Accrual Pausing During Extended Unpaid Leave

When configured, the system automatically pauses monthly leave accrual when employees take extended unpaid leave exceeding the configured threshold (e.g., 21 days for KSA compliance).

How it works: System tracks total days of specified leave types taken in a month. If total exceeds minimum threshold, monthly accrual is paused. Leave allowance is prorated: (Days worked ÷ Total days) × Monthly accrual.

⚠️ Important: Accrual pausing configuration cannot be edited once set. It only applies to new leave requests, not retroactively to existing ones.

Retroactive Salary Changes

Unpaid leave deductions use salary at approval time. Salary increases after approval do not trigger deduction recalculation.

Workaround: Process salary adjustments before approving unpaid leave requests. For retroactive salary changes, manually adjust deductions in payroll.

Probation Period Transitions

Employees transitioning out of probation mid-cycle may have leave restrictions that don't automatically lift.

Workaround: Review and update leave policy assignments when probation ends. Communicate new leave allowances to employees. Process any pending leave requests that were blocked by probation restrictions.

Deleted Policy Recovery

No self-service mechanism exists to recover accidentally deleted leave policies.

⚠️ No Recovery: Contact support immediately for backend recovery. Maintain documentation of all policy configurations as backup.

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change the leave pay rate after a policy is active?

No. Once a policy is active and assigned to employees, the pay rate (paid/unpaid/partially paid) cannot be changed. You must create a new policy with the desired pay rate and reassign employees.

Why don't employees see all company leave policies in their app?

Policies must be individually activated for each employee. Navigate to the employee's profile, go to the Leaves tab, and select which policies to activate.

How do I handle leave requests that span two leave cycles?

Leave requests cannot span multiple cycles. Employees must submit separate requests for each cycle. The system validates leave requests against cycle boundaries.

What happens if I change a public holiday date after leaves are approved?

The system recalculates affected leave requests and moves them to Pending status. You can optionally send an email notification to impacted employees. Recalculation only applies to current or future cycles.

Can I bulk approve or reject multiple leave requests?

No. The system currently requires individual approval/rejection actions for each leave request. Use the filters panel to narrow down pending requests by department, date range, or leave type.

How does unpaid leave deduction work with payroll?

When an unpaid leave request is fully approved, the system automatically creates a payroll deduction. If the leave spans multiple months, separate deductions are created for each month. For closed payroll months, contact your Payroll Manager.

What's the difference between calendar days and working days?

Calendar days count all days including weekends and holidays. Working days count only business days based on your company's work week configuration. Choose the calculation method when creating the policy.

Can employees request half-day leaves?

Yes. When requesting leave, employees select the same date for both 'From' and 'To' fields, then enable the 0.5 days toggle to convert it to a half-day request.

How do I restrict leave requests during probation?

Edit the leave policy and toggle 'Restrict during probation' ON. This only works if the employee's probation end date is filled in their work profile.

Why are my leave calendar changes not showing for employees?

Check Settings > Leaves > Leave Calendar and ensure the employee view toggle is ON. Employees will not see messages, attachments, or comments on leave requests—only the request details.

How do I configure leave salary policies?

First, enable leave salary settings in the leave policy. Then navigate to Payroll Settings > Leave Salary Policy to create a policy, select applicable leave types, configure the calculation formula, and add eligible employees. See the Leave Salary guide for details.

Can I pause monthly leave accrual for certain leave types?

Yes. When creating or editing a policy, enable monthly accrual pausing and select which leave types trigger the pause. Set the minimum days requirement (e.g., 21 days for KSA compliance). This only applies to new leave requests after configuration.

Why doesn't my leave policy account for scheduled shift days-off?

The platform cannot automatically account for scheduled shift days-off in working-day leave calculations. Employees with rotating shift schedules may see incorrect leave day counts. Use calendar days policy as an alternative, or manually adjust balances to account for shift patterns.

Does the platform support hourly leave requests?

No. The platform currently only supports full-day or half-day leave applications. Hourly leave requests are not available.

How does leave encashment work for employees leaving mid-month?

The platform cannot automatically calculate pro-rated leave encashment for employees leaving before month-end. Manual intervention is required to ensure employees receive accurate compensation for accrued leave days.

What happens to leave balances if I unassign and reassign employees to a policy?

Unassigning and reassigning employees can reset leave balances and erase historical data. The system lacks safeguards against this data loss. Always export leave balance reports before making policy changes.

Can employees request leave for periods before their policy was assigned?

No. The platform does not allow creating leave requests for periods before a leave policy is assigned to an employee. To handle historical leave, assign the policy with a retroactive start date first, then create the leave request.

How does the daily wage divisor affect unpaid leave deductions?

The system uses a fixed divisor of 30.42 days for daily wage calculations, not actual calendar days. This means deduction amounts may not precisely match monthly salary expectations. Formula: Daily Pay = (Basic Salary + Allowances) ÷ 30.42. Multiply by leave days for total deduction.
