What is Leave Policies?

Overview

Leave Policies define how employees accrue, request, and consume time off. Configure policies in Settings ‚Üí Leaves ‚Üí Leave Policies to control paid leave, unpaid leave, accrual rates, and encashment rules.

Create policies from templates or scratch, assign to employees, and track balances automatically. Policies integrate with Payroll for unpaid leave deductions and End of Service for encashment calculations.

Key Benefits

  • Automate monthly leave accrual instead of manual spreadsheet updates
  • Enforce probation restrictions and tenure-based allowances
  • Calculate unpaid leave deductions automatically in payroll
  • Track leave balances in real-time across calendar or working day cycles
  • Configure conditional pay rates (full, partial, unpaid) based on days taken
  • Support leave encashment with customizable calculation methods

Product Foundation Overview

How Leave Policies Fits

Leave Policies are configuration templates that define time-off rules and entitlements. They exist in Settings ‚Üí Leaves ‚Üí Leave Policies and serve as the foundation for all employee leave tracking.

Mental model: Leave Policy (template) ‚Üí Assigned to Employees ‚Üí Governs leave requests ‚Üí Drives payroll deductions and balances

One leave policy can affect hundreds of employees. Changes to accrual rates, carry-over rules, or payment types impact all assigned employees and future leave cycles.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating leave policies:

  • What leave types do you need? ‚Äî Annual, sick, unpaid, maternity, work-from-home, or custom types
  • How will leave accrue? ‚Äî Monthly accrual, one-time allocation, or conditional based on tenure/probation
  • What's the calculation basis? ‚Äî Calendar days vs. working days affects how weekends and holidays count
  • Will unpaid leave trigger payroll deductions? ‚Äî Requires payroll module enabled and salary configurations in place
  • Do you need conditional pay rates? ‚Äî Sick leave that transitions from paid to half-pay to unpaid based on days taken
  • What carry-over rules apply? ‚Äî Maximum days that roll to next cycle, expiry policies

Related Features

  • Payroll Module ‚Äî Automatically calculates unpaid leave deductions and processes leave salary payments
  • Time-Off Requests ‚Äî Employees submit requests against assigned leave policies; balances update upon approval
  • End of Service ‚Äî Leave encashment calculations reference policy settings for unused leave compensation
  • Approval Flows ‚Äî Leave requests route through configured approval hierarchies based on policy type
  • Public Holiday Calendars ‚Äî Determines whether holidays are excluded from leave calculations (working days policies only)

Initial Setup

Before You Begin

Complete these prerequisite steps before creating your first leave policy:

  • Enable Payroll Module ‚Äî Required for unpaid leave deductions and leave salary calculations
  • Configure Employee Salaries ‚Äî Set up basic salary and allowances for accurate deduction calculations
  • Set Up Holiday Calendar ‚Äî Define public holidays under Settings ‚Üí Company ‚Üí Holiday Calendars
  • Define Work Week ‚Äî Configure working days to support working-day-based leave calculations
  • Assign Line Manager Permissions ‚Äî Enable managers to approve leave requests through Role Management

First-Time Configuration Steps

Step 1: Access Leave Policies

Settings ‚Üí Leaves ‚Üí Leave Policies

Click "Add New" to create your first policy.

Step 2: Choose Policy Creation Method

  • Use Template ‚Äî Pre-configured policies for common leave types (Annual Leave, Sick Leave, Maternity Leave)
  • Create from Scratch ‚Äî Custom policies for organization-specific needs (Work from Home, Days in Lieu, Study Leave)

Step 3: Configure Core Policy Settings

Define these essential parameters:

  • Policy Name ‚Äî Clear identifier visible to employees (e.g., "Annual Leave - UAE Labor Law")
  • Leave Type ‚Äî Paid, Partially Paid, or Unpaid
  • Calculation Method ‚Äî Calendar days (includes weekends) or Working days (excludes weekends)
  • Annual Allowance ‚Äî Total days per year (e.g., 30 calendar days or 22 working days)
  • Accrual Frequency ‚Äî Annually recurring or One-time allocation

Step 4: Set Leave Cycle

Choose how leave resets:

  • Calendar Year ‚Äî Resets January 1st for all employees
  • Individual (Hire Date) ‚Äî Resets on each employee's anniversary

‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot Change After Assignment: Leave cycle type cannot be modified once employees are assigned to the policy. Changing requires creating a new policy and reassigning employees, which resets all leave balances.

Step 5: Configure Advanced Options

Enable these features based on organizational needs:

  • Conditional Leave Allowance ‚Äî Different allowances during probation vs. post-probation (e.g., 0 days during probation, 30 days after)
  • Carry Over Allowance ‚Äî Maximum days employees can roll to next cycle (e.g., 5 days max)
  • Restrict During Probation ‚Äî Prevent leave requests until probation ends
  • Exclude Public Holidays ‚Äî Public holidays don't count against leave balance
  • Enable Negative Balance ‚Äî Allow employees to request leave beyond available balance
  • Pause Monthly Accrual ‚Äî Stop accruing leave during extended unpaid leave (KSA compliance)

Step 6: Configure Unpaid Leave Deductions (If Applicable)

For unpaid or partially paid policies, set deduction calculation:

  • Salary Components ‚Äî Basic Only or Basic + Allowances
  • Daily Rate Calculation ‚Äî Calendar days (√∑30), Working days (√∑22), or Custom days (√∑30.42)

Daily Rate Formula: (Basic Salary + Allowances) √∑ [Days in Month] = Daily deduction amount. System automatically creates payroll deductions when unpaid leave is approved.

Step 7: Configure Conditional Pay Rates (Optional)

Set up to 5 pay rate conditions for policies like sick leave:

  • Example: First 15 days = 100% paid, Next 30 days = 50% paid, Remaining days = Unpaid
  • Each condition specifies number of days and pay percentage
  • System applies conditions sequentially based on cumulative days taken

‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot Enable After Assignment: Conditional pay rate feature cannot be toggled on for existing policies with assigned employees. Employees must be removed before enabling this feature.

Step 8: Assign Employees to Policy

Select which employees receive this policy:

  • Search and select individual employees
  • Use "Move All" to assign entire workforce
  • Set initial balances if employees have existing leave from previous system

Step 9: Configure Leave Encashment (Optional)

Allow employees to convert unused leave to cash:

  • Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Payroll ‚Üí Leave Encashment Policy
  • Define minimum accrued days required (e.g., 10 days)
  • Set minimum job tenure (e.g., 1 year)
  • Specify maximum encashment limit per request (e.g., 15 days)
  • Choose salary components for calculation (Basic or Basic + Allowances)

Step 10: Set Up Approval Flow

Define who approves leave requests:

  • Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Approval Flows ‚Üí Leaves
  • Add approval steps (Line Manager, Department Head, HR Manager)
  • Configure sequential or parallel approvals
  • Assign backup approvers for delegation scenarios

Key Tasks

Task: Create Leave Policy

Set up a new leave policy to define time-off rules for employees.

Subtask: Create Policy from Template or Scratch

Steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Leaves ‚Üí Leave Policies
  2. Click 'Add New'
  3. Choose template (Annual Leave, Sick Leave, Work from Home) or create from scratch
  4. Enter policy name and description

Subtask: Configure Leave Allowance

Steps:

  1. Set leave type (Paid, Partially Paid, or Unpaid)
  2. Define annual allowance (e.g., 30 calendar days or 22 working days)
  3. Choose calculation method: Calendar days or Working days
  4. Configure conditional allowances based on probation or tenure
  5. Enable or disable negative balances

Subtask: Configure Accrual Settings

Steps:

  1. Select accrual frequency (Monthly, Annually, or One-time)
  2. Set carry-over allowance for unused days
  3. Configure maximum encashable days
  4. Enable pause accrual during specific leave types (e.g., unpaid leave)

Steps:

  1. Toggle 'Unpaid Leave' option ON
  2. Select daily wage calculation: Basic Only or Basic + Allowances
  3. Choose day calculation type: Calendar days, Working days, or Custom days
  4. System will auto-create payroll deductions when leave is approved

Subtask: Configure Conditional Pay Rates

Steps:

  1. Enable 'Conditional Pay Rate' feature
  2. Add up to 5 pay rate conditions
  3. For each condition, set: number of leave days, pay rate percentage
  4. Example: First 15 days = 100% paid, Next 30 days = 50% paid, Remaining = Unpaid
  5. Set default pay rate for days exceeding all conditions

Subtask: Assign Policy to Employees

Steps:

  1. Click 'Assign Employees' or 'Move All'
  2. Select individual employees or bulk assign
  3. Review and adjust pro-rated days if needed
  4. Update 'Days used before setup' for existing leave taken
  5. Confirm and save policy
Expected Outcome

Policy is created and assigned. Employees can now submit leave requests. System tracks balances and applies deductions automatically.

Task: Configure Leave Encashment Policy

Set up rules for employees to convert unused leave days into cash payments.

Business Rules & Limitations

Core Business Rules

Leave Calculation Methods

Calculation Type Calendar Days Working Days
Annual Leave 30 days (includes weekends and holidays) 22 days (excludes weekends and holidays)
Leave Deduction Counts all days in leave period Counts only business days
Public Holidays Included in leave count unless excluded Automatically excluded from count

Unpaid Leave Deduction Rules

  • Daily Rate Formula: Salary Amount √∑ Total days (calendar or working based on policy)
  • Salary Components: Can include Basic Only or Basic + Allowances
  • Multi-Month Leave: Creates separate deductions per calendar month
  • Closed Payroll Months: Cannot create deductions; requires manual email notification to Payroll Manager
  • Deduction Timing: Created automatically upon full approval of leave request

Leave Accrual Rules

  • Monthly Accrual: Annual allowance √∑ 12 months
  • Proration: Based on complete months worked; mid-month departures do not accrue partial month
  • Accrual Pausing: Can pause monthly accrual during specific leave types (e.g., unpaid leave exceeding threshold)
  • Carry-Over: Unused days can carry forward to next cycle if enabled in policy
  • Negative Balances: Can be enabled to allow leave requests exceeding current balance

Conditional Pay Rate Rules

  • Sick Leave Example: First 15 days fully paid, next 30 days half pay, remaining 45 days unpaid
  • Maximum Conditions: Up to 5 pay rate conditions per policy
  • Allowance Tracking: Leave allowance considered used when request is approved
  • Daily Calculation: Pro-rata calculation based on actual leave duration

Configuration Limitations

‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot Change Payment Type After Assignment

Leave policies cannot be changed from paid to unpaid (or vice versa) after employees are assigned. Employees must be removed before changing payment type due to impacts on payroll calculations and End of Service duration tracking.

‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot Enable Conditional Pay Rate on Active Policies

Conditional pay rate features cannot be enabled on existing policies with active employee assignments. Employees must be removed from the policy before the feature can be toggled, creating a complex migration process.

‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot Modify Accrual Frequency After Creation

The accrual frequency of a leave policy cannot be changed after initial creation. Users must create an entirely new policy instead of editing the existing one.

Calculation & Processing Limitations

‚ö†Ô∏è Unpaid Leave Deduction Uses Fixed Daily Wage Divisor

The payroll system uses a custom daily wage divisor (30.42 days) instead of calendar days, which can result in deduction amounts that don't precisely match the monthly salary. This creates potential user confusion about calculation methods.

‚ö†Ô∏è No Real-Time Synchronization Between Time Off and Payroll

When public holidays are added after leave requests are approved, the system cannot automatically recalculate daily wage deductions. This creates inconsistent calculations that cannot be retroactively corrected.

‚ö†Ô∏è Static Salary Reference for Unpaid Leave Deductions

The payroll system uses a static salary reference point instead of a dynamic, time-sensitive approach. This prevents accurate prorated salary deductions based on actual leave duration and corresponding salary at the time leave was taken.

No Shift Days-Off Integration: Leave calculations cannot automatically account for scheduled shift days-off. Employees with complex shift schedules cannot accurately track leave usage using working days policies.

No Automated Leave Request Timing Validation: The platform lacks automated validation for leave request timing relative to employee absences. Managers must manually review leave history and enforce time-range policies.

Reporting & Visibility Limitations

Unpaid Leave Count Not in Payroll Templates: The current payroll template lacks complete visibility into unpaid leave tracking and comprehensive leave salary reporting. Users cannot easily view total unpaid days and corresponding salary implications in a single template view.

No Configurable Leave Expiry Notifications: The time-off management system has a fixed, non-configurable notification period for leave expiry. Users cannot customize the advance notice period or enable automatic notifications for line managers.

Generic Disclaimer Messages for Conditional Policies: The system lacks flexibility in generating policy-specific disclaimer messages for conditional leave policies. Generic messages do not adapt to nuanced pay structures of different leave policies, leading to potential misunderstandings.

Edge Cases

February 29 Hire Dates

Employees hired on February 29 (leap year) have their leave cycle start from February 28 in non-leap years, causing potential 1-day discrepancy in conditional leave allowance calculations.

Overlapping Leave Requests

When enabling automatic public holiday deductions or changing policy settings, affected leave requests are moved to Pending state and require re-approval.

Leave Encashment for Mid-Month Departures

Employees leaving before month-end lose accrued leave days for that partial month. Pro-rated leave encashment must be calculated manually as the system only accrues for complete months worked.

Conditional Pay Rate Mid-Cycle Changes

The system can track conditional leave policies but lacks nuanced handling when conditions change mid-cycle. Balances do not automatically carry forward or adjust retroactively.

Unpaid Leave Exceeding Threshold

When unpaid leave exceeds configured threshold (e.g., 21 days in KSA), monthly accrual pausing applies. Adjusted leave days use the original request start date, not the recalculated date.

Deleted Policy Recovery

Accidentally deleted leave policies cannot be recovered through self-service. Backend intervention is required, creating significant operational complexity and potential data loss risks.

Sick Leave Certificate Denial

If online doctor determines employee is not eligible for Sick Leave Certificate after AED 50 consultation payment, the certificate is not issued. Employee must dispute through HAH platform or visit hospital.

Payroll Cycle Modification vs Normal Opening

When a payroll cycle is modified instead of opened normally, automatic unpaid leave deduction calculations may fail across month boundaries. Deductions must be created manually.

Same Employee in Multiple Leave Salary Policies

Employees cannot be added to multiple leave salary policies simultaneously. Attempting to do so will result in validation error.

Advance Leave Salary Payment Tracking

When leave salary is paid in advance (before actual leave period), precise tracking and adjustment in future payroll months is required. System does not automatically reconcile advance payments.

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change a leave policy from paid to unpaid after employees are assigned?

No. The platform prevents changing payment type (paid/unpaid) once employees are assigned to the policy due to impacts on payroll calculations and End of Service tracking.

How does unpaid leave deduction calculate daily wage?

The system uses a divisor of 30.42 days (not calendar days) to calculate daily wage. This can result in deduction amounts that don't precisely match monthly salary division.

Can I modify carry-over allowance for individual leave cycles?

No. The platform cannot modify carry-over allowance for companies using individual leave cycles. This requires backend intervention.

Why can't I see audit logs for leave policy changes?

The platform lacks a user-accessible interface for viewing leave policy modification history. You must contact support to access audit logs via backend database queries.

Can employees request leave retroactively before their policy assignment date?

No. The platform does not allow creating leave requests for periods before a leave policy is assigned to an employee, even if the payroll cycle remains open.

What happens if I add public holidays after unpaid leave is approved?

The platform lacks real-time synchronization between Time Off and Payroll modules. Public holidays added after leave approval will not trigger automatic recalculation of deductions.

Can I enable conditional pay rates on existing leave policies with assigned employees?

No. Employees must be removed from the policy before the conditional pay rate feature can be enabled, creating a complex migration process.

Does the system support hourly leave requests?

No. The platform only supports full-day or half-day leave applications. Hourly leave requests are not available.

Can I change the accrual frequency of an existing leave policy?

No. The platform does not support modifying accrual frequency after policy creation. You must create a new policy instead.

How do I handle leave requests spanning multiple payroll months?

The system automatically creates separate deductions for each calendar month. Leave requests for closed payroll months require manual email notification to the Payroll Manager.

Why are leave balances incorrect after policy changes?

The platform lacks safeguards against data loss when modifying policy configurations. Unassigning and reassigning employees can erase historical leave balances.

Can I recover a deleted leave policy?

No. The platform lacks a self-service undelete mechanism. You must contact support for backend intervention to restore accidentally deleted policies.
