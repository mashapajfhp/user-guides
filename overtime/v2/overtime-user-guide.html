üìã Feature Overview

What is Overtime Management?

The Overtime feature enables organizations to systematically track, approve, and compensate employees for hours worked beyond their regular schedule. It provides configurable policies that define how overtime is calculated, requested, and processed through payroll or converted to time off.

Who Uses This Feature?

Primary Users:

  • HR Administrators: Configure overtime policies and calculation rules

  • Line Managers: Schedule overtime, approve employee requests, and review time adjustments

  • Employees: View scheduled overtime, request extra hours compensation, and track approval status

  • Payroll Administrators: Process approved overtime for payment

    • Key Capabilities


  • Policy Configuration: Create overtime policies with Single Rate or Custom Rate calculations for weekdays, weekends, public holidays, and days off

  • Advance Scheduling: Managers can schedule overtime hours in advance with employee acceptance workflow

  • Employee Requests: Employees can request compensation for extra hours worked after checkout

  • Flexible Compensation: Support for both payroll-based overtime pay and time-off-in-lieu

  • Approval Workflow: Multi-level approval process with pending, approved, and processed statuses

  • Time & Pay Adjustments: Centralized view of all overtime entries with filtering and bulk actions

    • Business Value


  • Compliance: Ensure accurate tracking and compensation according to labor law requirements

  • Transparency: Employees can see scheduled overtime and request status in real-time

  • Efficiency: Automated calculations eliminate manual overtime tracking and reduce payroll errors

  • Flexibility: Support multiple compensation methods and custom calculation formulas per policy

  • Control: Managers maintain visibility and approval authority over overtime costs

Task 1: Create a New Overtime Policy

Purpose: Set up a policy to track and calculate overtime hours for employees

Prerequisites:

  • Super Admin or HR Manager access

  • Understanding of your organization's overtime compensation rules

  • Knowledge of which employees should be included

    • Steps:

  • Navigate to Policy Creation

  • Go to Settings > Attendance > Overtime Policy

  • Click Add New button

  • Screenshot: 03-overtime-policies-list.png

  • Configure Policy Name and Behavior (Step 1 of 3)

  • Enter a descriptive Policy Name (e.g., "OT Schedule", "Standard Overtime")

  • Allow employees to request extra hours: Toggle ON to let employees submit overtime requests after working beyond scheduled hours

  • Allow managers to see net amount: Toggle ON to let managers without payroll access view overtime payment amounts

  • Screenshot: 04-overtime-policy-configuration-step1.png

  • Select Rate Calculation Type

  • Choose between:

  • Single Rate: One multiplier applies to all overtime hours

  • Custom Rate: Different multipliers for weekdays, weekends, public holidays, and days off

  • Screenshot: 05-overtime-rate-calculation-options.png

  • Configure Rate Calculation Formula

    • For Single Rate:

  • Choose Type: Select "Unit" or "Split"

  • Multiplier: Enter rate multiplier (default: 1.5, minimum: 0.1, no maximum)

  • Salary Component: Choose "Basic Salary" or "Basic Salary + Allowances"

  • Working Days: Select calculation basis:

  • Custom Days (default: 30)

  • Actual Days

  • Working Days

  • Hours: Set standard working hours per day (default: 8)

    • Formula Display: ((Unit x [Multiplier]) x [Salary Component]) / ([Working Days] x [Days]) x Hours ([Hours]) x Extra hours duration in hours

    For Custom Rate:

  • Configure separate formulas for:

  • Weekdays: Regular working day overtime

  • Weekends: Weekend overtime (based on work week settings)

  • Public Holidays: Holiday overtime

  • Day Offs: Scheduled day off overtime (shift employees only)

  • Each category has the same configuration options as Single Rate

  • Screenshot: 06-overtime-formula-configuration.png

    • Important Notes:

  • The formula display may appear complex. The system calculates: (Hourly Rate √ó Multiplier) √ó Extra Hours

  • Decimals are rounded to 2 decimal places

  • Known Issue: Formula display lacks clear visual grouping (TSSD-2256). The calculation logic is correct, but the UI presentation may be confusing.

  • Assign Employees (Step 2 of 3)

  • Search for employees by name or use filters

  • Move employees from Excluded list to Included list

  • Note: Employees already assigned to another overtime policy will show as "Already on another policy" and cannot be selected

  • Screenshot: 07-overtime-employee-assignment.png

  • Review and Save (Step 3 of 3)

  • Review all policy settings in read-only summary view

  • Verify:

  • Policy name and behavior settings

  • Rate calculation formulas

  • List of assigned employees (with pagination)

  • Click Save to create the policy

  • Screenshot: 08-overtime-policy-review-save.png

    • Expected Outcome: Policy is created and assigned employees can now have overtime tracked according to the configured rules.

    Limitations:

  • Only ONE overtime policy can be assigned to an employee at a time

  • Cannot select specific allowance components (TSSD-294) - must choose either Basic Salary only or Basic Salary + All Allowances

  • Complex formula configuration may require testing to ensure correct calculations (TSSD-3821)

    Configuration Troubleshooting Tasks

    Task 11: Troubleshoot Overtime Calculation Issues

    Common Issue: Overtime starting at unexpected hours

    Example: Overtime calculated at 8 hours instead of configured 8.30 hours (TSSD-3821)

    Root Cause: Break time inclusion flag affecting actual work hours

    Diagnosis Steps:

  • Check Work Timing Configuration

  • Navigate to Settings > Attendance > Work Timings

  • Open the work timing assigned to the employee

  • Note the configured hours (e.g., 8:30 hours)

  • Check break duration (e.g., 30 minutes)

  • Verify Break Inclusion Flag

  • Look for "Include break time in work hours" setting

  • If FALSE: System subtracts break time from work hours

  • Example: 8:30 hours - 0:30 break = 8 hours actual work

  • Overtime starts after 8 hours of actual work

  • If TRUE: Break time is included in work hours

  • Example: 8:30 hours includes break

  • Overtime starts after 8:30 hours

  • Solution Options:

  • Option A: Set work timing to 9 hours with break inclusion TRUE

  • Option B: Enable break inclusion flag for existing 8:30 hour schedule

  • Option C: Adjust overtime policy formula to account for break deduction

    • Testing Recommendation: Create test scenarios with different break inclusion settings to understand impact on overtime calculations.

    Task 12: Understand Work Week and Weekend Overtime

    Common Issue: Confusion about weekend overtime calculations (TSSD-2984)

    Steps:

  • Check Work Week Configuration

  • Navigate to Settings > Attendance > Work Week

  • Verify which days are designated as weekends

  • Note any Days in Lieu policy assignments

  • Understand Weekend Overtime Logic

  • For employees with Days in Lieu policy: ALL hours worked on weekends count as overtime

  • No comparison to scheduled hours on weekend days

  • Weekend designation is based on work week settings

  • Impact of Configuration Changes

  • Changes to work week settings can affect historical overtime calculations

  • Example: Changing weekend designation may retroactively impact past shifts

  • Recommendation: Document work week changes and communicate to managers

    • Note: This is expected system behavior, not a bug. The complexity arises from interdependent settings (work week, Days in Lieu policy, weekend designation).

    Known Limitations and Workarounds

    Limitation 1: Cannot Select Specific Allowance Components

    Issue: System only allows "Basic Salary" or "Basic Salary + All Allowances" (TSSD-294)

    Impact: Cannot selectively include only certain allowances (e.g., Basic Salary + Accommodation Allowance only)

    Workaround:

  • Use "Basic Salary" option if allowances should not be included

  • Or accept all allowances and adjust multiplier to compensate

  • Or calculate overtime manually outside the system for complex scenarios

    • Status: Feature improvement under consideration

    ---

    Limitation 2: Complex Formula Display

    Issue: Formula presentation lacks clear visual grouping (TSSD-2256)

    Impact: Users may misunderstand calculation order of operations

    Workaround:

  • Test policy with known scenarios to verify calculations

  • Document expected results for different hour amounts

  • Use calculator to manually verify formula logic

  • Contact support if calculations appear incorrect

    • Example Formula: ((Unit x 1.5) x Basic Salary) / (Custom Days x 30) x Hours (8) x Extra hours duration

    Actual Calculation: (Basic Salary / 30 / 8) √ó 1.5 √ó Extra Hours

    ---

    Limitation 3: Bulk Approval Discoverability

    Issue: Bulk approval functionality not immediately visible (TSSD-3944)

    Impact: Line managers may not realize they can bulk approve overtime

    Workaround:

  • Select multiple entries using checkboxes in Pending tab

  • Bulk action bar appears at bottom of screen

  • Use bulk Approve or Reject buttons

Screenshot: 15-pending-tab-bulk-approve.png, 16-bulk-selection-action-bar.png

---

Limitation 4: Policy Assignment Timing Uncertainty

Issue: Unclear whether overtime policy applies if assigned after shift publication (TSSD-1544)

Clarification: System evaluates overtime policy at check-in time, NOT at shift publication time

Impact: Order of operations (policy assignment vs. shift publication) does not matter

Best Practice: Assign overtime policies before publishing shifts to avoid confusion, even though timing does not affect functionality.
