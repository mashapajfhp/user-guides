What is Overtime?

Overview

Overtime tracks employee extra hours worked beyond scheduled time and calculates compensation automatically. Access it via Time > Employee attendance > Time and Pay Adjustments, or configure policies in Settings > Attendance.

Admins review pending extra hours, approve compensation as payroll additions or days in lieu, and track processed records. Line managers can schedule extra hours and decide compensation types when enabled.

Key Benefits

  • Automatic rate calculations based on salary components and working hours
  • Dual compensation modes: payroll (AED) or leave (days in lieu)
  • Bulk approval workflows for pending extra hours requests
  • Policy-based assignment with custom rate formulas per employee group
  • Direct integration with payroll table for seamless payment processing
  • Audit trail with activity logs and approval history

Product Foundation Overview

How Overtime Fits

Overtime is a configuration template system that defines how extra hours worked beyond standard schedules are compensated. Policies are created once, assigned to employees, and automatically calculate compensation when extra hours are recorded.

Mental model: Overtime Policy (template) → Assigned to Employees → Extra hours recorded → Compensation calculated (Payroll AED or Days In Lieu) → Flows to payroll or leave balance

One overtime policy can affect many employees. Changes to policy rates or calculation basis immediately impact all assigned employees' future extra hours compensation.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating overtime policies:

  • How will you compensate extra hours? — Payroll additions (AED amounts) or Days In Lieu (time off)
  • What salary components determine the hourly rate? — Basic only, or Basic + Allowances
  • Will rates vary by time period or employee type? — Single unified rate vs custom rates for weekdays/weekends/holidays or different employee groups
  • What is the minimum threshold for extra hours? — Default is 30 minutes; extra hours below this threshold are ignored
  • Can line managers decide compensation type per request? — If enabled, managers choose between payroll or leave when approving extra hours

Initial Setup & Configuration

Configuration Walkthrough

Before employees can request overtime or receive compensation, configure global settings and create compensation policies.

Settings → Attendance → General

Configure baseline overtime behavior for all employees.

  • Minimum extra hours threshold: Set the minimum duration (e.g., 30 minutes) before overtime is recorded. Hours below this threshold are ignored.
  • Line managers can decide compensation types: Toggle ON to allow line managers to choose between payroll compensation (AED) or leave compensation (days in lieu) when approving overtime requests. Toggle OFF to enforce policy-level defaults only.
  • Auto-mark employees absent on missed check-in: Controls automatic absence marking for employees who do not check in.
  • Enable Image Capture / Enable facial recognition: Security settings for attendance verification (not specific to overtime).

Overtime Policy Setup

Create policies that define how overtime hours are compensated in payroll. Each policy calculates an hourly rate based on employee salary and applies a multiplier.

Settings → Attendance → Overtime Policies

Manage all overtime compensation policies for payroll-based compensation.

Click Add new at the bottom of the table to create a policy. The 3-step wizard guides you through configuration:

Step 1: Policy Name & Calculation

  • Policy name: Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "CS Overtime Policy", "Weekend Overtime 1.5x").
  • Rate calculation basis: Choose Single Rate or Custom Rate.
    • Single Rate: One multiplier applies to all overtime hours. Choose Unified (same rate for all employees) or Split (different rates per employee).
    • Custom Rate: Define different multipliers for different hour ranges (e.g., 1.5x for first 2 hours, 2x thereafter).
  • Rate formula: The system calculates hourly rate as: ((multiplier × (Basic + Allowances)) / (Working Days × Hours)) × Extra hours duration. Configure the multiplier value.
  • Employee extra hours requests: Toggle ON to allow employees to submit overtime requests via the platform. Toggle OFF if overtime is scheduled by admins only.

Step 2: Assign Employees

Select which employees this policy applies to. Use the employee assignment interface to add or remove employees. Employees can be assigned to only one overtime policy at a time.

Step 3: Review & Save

Review policy details and employee assignments. Click Save to activate the policy. The policy appears in the Overtime Policies table with an Active toggle.

Managing Policies

  • Edit: Click the pencil icon to reopen the 3-step wizard and modify policy settings or employee assignments.
  • Delete: Click the trash icon to remove the policy. Cannot delete if employees are currently assigned.
  • Active/Inactive toggle: Deactivate a policy without deleting it. Inactive policies do not accept new overtime requests.

Days In Lieu Policy Setup

Create policies that compensate overtime with time off instead of pay. Each policy defines how many overtime hours equal one day of leave.

Settings → Attendance → Days In Lieu Policies

Manage all leave-based compensation policies.

Click Add new to create a policy. The wizard is similar to Overtime Policies but focuses on hours-to-days conversion:

  • Policy name: Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Standard Days In Lieu", "Weekend Leave Compensation").
  • No. of hours for 1 day: Define the conversion rate. For example, if set to 8, every 8 overtime hours worked equals 1 day of compensatory leave.
  • Employee extra hours requests: Toggle ON to allow employees to request overtime with leave compensation. Toggle OFF for admin-scheduled overtime only.
  • Assign Employees: Select employees who can receive leave compensation for overtime.

Days In Lieu policies follow the same Edit/Delete/Active toggle management as Overtime Policies.

Assignment & Activation

After creating policies, assign them to employees:

  • Each employee can be assigned to one Overtime Policy (for payroll compensation) and one Days In Lieu Policy (for leave compensation).
  • If the "Line managers can decide compensation types" toggle is ON in General settings, line managers choose which policy applies when approving each overtime request.
  • If the toggle is OFF, the assigned policy determines the compensation type automatically.
  • Employees not assigned to any policy cannot submit overtime requests and do not appear in the Schedule Extra Hours employee dropdown (shown as "Not assigned to addition policy").

Activation: Policies become active immediately upon saving. Employees can submit requests (if enabled) or admins can schedule overtime as soon as assignment is complete.

Payroll Integration Setup

For overtime amounts to appear in payroll, configure the mass upload mechanism:

Payroll → Payroll table → 3-dot menu → Mass Upload Variable Pays

This interface allows bulk upload of approved overtime amounts via Excel template:

  • Download template: Click to download an Excel sheet pre-populated with employee identifiers, names, and calculated overtime rates per policy.
  • Fill in hours: Enter the number of overtime hours worked per employee in the appropriate policy column.
  • Upload completed sheet: The system calculates amounts (rate × hours) and adds them to the Overtime column in the payroll table.

Amounts uploaded this way appear in the payroll table Overtime column and are included in monthly payroll processing.

Approval Flow

How Overtime Requests Route

Extra hours requests submitted by employees route through the Pending queue in Time and Pay Adjustments. Approvers review requests and choose compensation type (Payroll or Days In Lieu) during approval. Requests move to Approved, Processed, or Rejected tabs based on decision.

Types Requiring Approval

All extra hours requests require approval before processing. Requests can be compensated as:

  • Payroll (AED) — Monetary compensation added to payroll
  • Days In Lieu — Time-off compensation based on hours-per-day policy

Routing Examples

How different overtime requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Standard Extra Hours

Employee works 2 extra hours → Request appears in Pending queue → Line manager reviews attendance details → Approves for Payroll → Amount calculated based on assigned overtime policy → Moves to Approved tab → Processes to payroll

Example 2: Days In Lieu Compensation

Employee works 8 extra hours → Request in Pending queue → Manager reviews → Approves for Leave (Days In Lieu) → System converts hours to days based on policy (e.g., 8 hours = 1 day) → Employee receives time-off credit

Example 3: Bulk Approval

Multiple extra hours requests pending → Approver selects multiple rows → Uses bulk action bar → Chooses "Approve all for payroll" or "Approve all for leaves" → All selected requests route to same compensation type → Moves to Approved tab in batch

Compensation Type Decision

Approvers decide compensation type at approval time:

  • Payroll: Calculates monetary amount using overtime policy rate formula
  • Days In Lieu: Converts hours to leave days using Days In Lieu policy
  • Line Manager Toggle: If enabled in General settings, line managers choose compensation type; if disabled, HR controls decision

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

Policy Configuration Constraints

⚠️ Overtime calculations vary based on policy configuration

Different overtime policies produce different payment amounts for the same extra hours depending on rate type (Single vs Custom), multiplier, and salary components included. Single Rate applies one multiplier to all hours; Custom Rate allows different multipliers for different hour ranges. Salary components (Basic only vs Basic + Allowances) directly affect the hourly rate calculation.

By Design: This is intentional to support diverse company compensation structures.

⚠️ Overtime and Days In Lieu policies are separate configurations

Administrators must configure overtime policies and days in lieu policies separately. There is no unified policy editor for both compensation types. Each requires independent setup in Settings > Attendance.

By Design: Separation allows flexible combinations of pay and time-off compensation.

Global Settings Impact

Setting Scope Impact
Minimum extra hours threshold Global (currently 30 minutes) Applies to all employees regardless of overtime policy. Cannot be set per-policy.
Line manager compensation type decision Global toggle When enabled, all line managers can decide compensation types. No department-level or team-level override available.
Days in lieu hours-per-day Per policy (fixed) Fixed per policy and cannot vary by employee or day type within the same policy. No conditional logic for weekdays vs weekends.

By Design: Global settings ensure consistent policy application across the organization.

Payroll Integration Considerations

No dedicated overtime mass upload option in payroll

There is no separate 'Mass Upload Overtime' option in the payroll 3-dot menu. Overtime must be uploaded through 'Mass Upload Variable Pays' which covers all variable pay types. Navigate to Payroll table > 3-dot menu > Mass Upload Variable Pays.

By Design: Overtime is treated as a variable pay component for payroll processing.

Hourly Rate Calculation Formula

Overtime hourly rate is calculated as:

Rate = Multiplier × ((Basic Salary + Allowances) / Calendar Days) / Working Hours

  • Multiplier: Set in overtime policy (e.g., 1.5x for Single Rate or custom values for different hour ranges)
  • Salary components: Configurable per policy (Basic only or Basic + Allowances)
  • Calendar days: Total days in the month
  • Working hours: Standard daily working hours

Multi-Currency Payroll Constraints

For companies managing multi-currency payroll:

  • Cannot update employee currency if any pay-items have been paid in the active month
  • Cannot update currency if unpaid variable pay (including overtime) exists
  • Cannot update currency if unpaid recurring deductions exist for active or future months
  • Overtime amounts flow into payroll calculations in the employee's base currency

Edge Cases

  • Multiple policies with overlapping employees: Employee can be assigned to only one overtime policy at a time. Last assignment takes precedence.
  • Overtime hours negative or excessive: System may accept invalid values during Excel upload. Validate data before upload to prevent payroll errors.
  • Retroactive attendance corrections: Changes to approved extra hours require re-approval and may affect closed payroll months. Coordinate with finance before making retroactive changes.
  • Partial day overtime: System calculates based on hours, not full days. Fractional hours are supported and calculated according to hourly rate formula.
  • Multi-branch companies: Overtime policies apply organization-wide. Cannot configure different policies per branch or location without separate employee groupings.
  • Overtime exceeding policy limits: No automatic cap enforcement. Admins must manually review uploaded hours against company policy limits.
  • Time off overlapping with scheduled attendance: Approved leave may conflict with extra hours records. System does not automatically resolve conflicts—manual review required.
  • Salary or allowances not set: Hourly rate calculation fails if employee salary data is incomplete. Ensure Basic Salary and Allowances (if included in policy) are configured in employee profiles.

Data Validation Checklist

Before uploading overtime hours or processing payroll, verify:

  • Employee identifiers populated in Work section of all employee profiles
  • Overtime policies fully configured with correct rate type, multiplier, and salary components
  • Days In Lieu policies (if used) have correct hours-per-day setting
  • Excel file format matches downloaded template structure
  • Overtime hours are positive values and within expected ranges
  • No currency changes pending for employees with unpaid overtime
  • Payroll month is open (not closed) before uploading new overtime data

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do different overtime policies produce different payment amounts for the same hours?

Overtime policies use different calculation methods. Single Rate applies one multiplier to all hours, while Custom Rate allows different multipliers for different hour ranges. The salary components included (Basic only vs Basic + Allowances) also directly affect the hourly rate calculation.

How do I upload overtime hours for payroll?

Use 'Mass Upload Variable Pays' from the payroll table 3-dot menu. There is no separate 'Mass Upload Overtime' option — overtime is uploaded through the variable pays option which covers all variable pay types.

Can I set different minimum extra hours thresholds for different employees?

No. The minimum extra hours threshold (currently 30 minutes) is a global setting that applies to all employees regardless of their overtime policy. It cannot be set per-policy or per-employee.

Can line managers decide compensation types for some teams but not others?

No. The 'Line managers can decide compensation types for extra hours worked' toggle is a global on/off setting. It cannot be configured per team or department.

Can I set different hours-per-day values for days in lieu based on weekday vs weekend?

No. The 'How many hours that make a day in lieu' setting is fixed per policy and cannot vary by employee or day type within the same policy.

Do I need to configure overtime policies and days in lieu policies together?

No. Overtime policies and days in lieu policies are separate configurations. You must configure them independently — there is no unified policy editor for both compensation types.
