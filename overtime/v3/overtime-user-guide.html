What is Overtime?

Overview

Overtime tracks employee extra hours beyond scheduled work time and calculates compensation automatically. Access it through Time > Employee Attendance > Time and Pay Adjustments, or configure policies in Settings > Attendance.

Filter extra hours by status (Scheduled, Pending, Approved, Processed, Rejected), view detailed attendance breakdowns, and choose compensation type: payroll (AED amounts) or leave (days in lieu).

Key Benefits

  • Eliminate manual overtime calculation spreadsheets and reduce payroll errors
  • Track extra hours automatically from attendance data with manager approval workflow
  • Offer flexible compensation: cash payments or time-off credits based on company policy
  • Apply different overtime rates per employee group (standard, weekend, holiday multipliers)
  • Integrate overtime amounts directly into payroll table for seamless processing
  • Audit trail of all extra hours with approval history and compensation details

Strategic Context

🎯 What Overtime Solves

Replaces manual tracking of extra hours in Excel, WhatsApp requests, and ad-hoc payroll adjustments with automated calculation and approval.

  • Replaces manual overtime spreadsheets and email approvals
  • Standardizes extra hours calculation across all employees and departments
  • Enables accurate payroll processing without last-minute adjustments

💰 Why It Matters

Overtime is foundational for companies with shift workers, field teams, or project-based work where hours vary beyond standard schedules.

  • Prerequisite for accurate payroll when employees work beyond scheduled hours
  • Drives payroll additions (overtime pay) and time-off credits (days in lieu)
  • Controls labor cost visibility and compliance with overtime regulations

🏗️ How It Connects

Configuration template assigned to employees, generates transactional records that flow into payroll or time-off balances.

  • Overtime policies assigned to employees determine rate calculation and compensation type
  • Attendance system references policies to calculate extra hours from check-in/check-out data
  • One policy can affect many employees; changes apply to future extra hours calculations

Product Foundation Overview

How Overtime Fits

Overtime is a configuration framework that defines how extra hours worked beyond standard schedules are compensated. It consists of policies (templates) that set calculation rules and compensation modes (payroll or leave).

Mental model: Overtime Policy (template) → Assigned to Employees → Extra hours recorded → Compensation calculated → Payroll addition or leave credit applied

One policy can affect multiple employees across departments, standardizing how overtime is tracked and paid company-wide.

Key Decisions Before Setup

Answer these questions before creating overtime policies:

  • How will you compensate overtime? — Choose between monetary payment (added to payroll) or time-off credits (days in lieu)
  • What rate multiplier applies? — Determine if overtime is paid at 1.5x, 2x, or custom rates based on company policy or labor law
  • Which salary components count? — Decide if overtime rates calculate from basic salary only or basic plus allowances
  • What's the minimum threshold? — Set the minimum extra hours (e.g., 30 minutes) before overtime compensation applies

Initial Setup & Configuration

Configuration Walkthrough

Before employees can log extra hours or receive compensation, configure overtime policies and general attendance settings.

Settings → Attendance → General

Set company-wide overtime thresholds and manager permissions.

General Settings Configuration

  • Minimum extra hours threshold: Set the minimum overtime duration to track (default 30 minutes). Hours below this threshold are not recorded.
  • Line managers can decide compensation types: Enable this toggle to allow line managers to choose between payroll (AED) or leave (days in lieu) compensation when approving extra hours. When disabled, compensation type is fixed per policy.
  • Auto-mark employees absent on missed check-in: Automatically create absence records if employees do not check in on scheduled workdays.
  • Enable Image Capture / Enable facial recognition: Require photo verification during check-in/check-out for attendance validation.

Overtime Policy Setup

Create policies that define how overtime hours are calculated and compensated. Each policy applies to a specific group of employees.

Settings → Attendance → Overtime Policies

Admin path: Configure rate formulas and assign employees to overtime policies.

Creating an Overtime Policy

Click Add new at the bottom of the Overtime Policies table. The 3-step wizard guides you through configuration:

Step 1: Policy Name & Calculation
  • Policy name: Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "CS Overtime Policy", "Operations Overtime").
  • Rate calculation basis: Choose Single Rate (one formula for all employees) or Custom Rate (different formulas per employee or group).
  • Rate type: Select Unified (same rate for all hours) or Split (different rates for weekday vs weekend/holiday hours).
  • Rate formula components:
    • Multiplier: Overtime rate multiplier (e.g., 1.5x for time-and-a-half)
    • Salary components: Basic only or Basic + Allowances
    • Working days: Number of working days per month (e.g., 26)
    • Hours per day: Standard working hours per day (e.g., 8)
  • Employee extra hours requests: Toggle ON to allow employees to submit extra hours requests via the mobile app. Toggle OFF if only admins/managers can create extra hours records.

Formula example: If multiplier is 1.5, Basic + Allowances = AED 10,000, working days = 26, hours per day = 8, then hourly rate = (1.5 × 10,000) / (26 × 8) = AED 72.12 per hour. An employee working 5 extra hours earns AED 360.60.

Step 2: Assign Employees
  • Click Manage employee assignment to select which employees this policy applies to.
  • Search by name, department, or office to filter employees.
  • Select employees individually or by group.
  • Employees can only be assigned to one overtime policy at a time.
Step 3: Review & Save
  • Review policy name, rate formula, and assigned employee count.
  • Click Save to activate the policy.
  • The policy appears in the Overtime Policies table with an Active toggle.

Editing and Deleting Policies

  • Click the Edit (pencil) icon to modify policy name, formula, or employee assignments.
  • Click the Delete (trash) icon to remove a policy. Policies with assigned employees cannot be deleted until employees are reassigned.
  • Use the Active/Inactive toggle to temporarily disable a policy without deleting it.

Days In Lieu Policy Setup

Configure policies that compensate overtime with time off instead of pay. Employees earn leave days based on extra hours worked.

Settings → Attendance → Days In Lieu Policies

Admin path: Define hours-per-day conversion rates and assign employees.

Creating a Days In Lieu Policy

Click Add new at the bottom of the Days In Lieu Policies table. The 3-step wizard is similar to overtime policies:

Step 1: Policy Name & Calculation
  • Policy name: Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Standard Days In Lieu", "Shift Worker Comp Days").
  • No. of hours for 1 day: Define how many extra hours equal one full leave day (e.g., 8 hours = 1 day, 10 hours = 1 day). This determines the conversion rate.
  • Employee extra hours requests: Toggle ON to allow employees to request days in lieu compensation via the mobile app.

Conversion example: If policy sets 8 hours = 1 day, an employee working 12 extra hours earns 1.5 days of leave. If policy sets 10 hours = 1 day, the same 12 hours earn 1.2 days of leave.

Step 2: Assign Employees
  • Click Manage employee assignment to select employees.
  • Employees can be assigned to both an overtime policy (for payroll compensation) and a days in lieu policy (for leave compensation) simultaneously. The line manager or admin chooses which compensation type to apply per extra hours record.
Step 3: Review & Save
  • Review policy name, hours-per-day conversion, and assigned employee count.
  • Click Save to activate the policy.

Editing and Deleting Policies

  • Use the Edit and Delete icons to manage policies.
  • Use the Active/Inactive toggle to enable or disable policies.

Assignment & Activation

After creating policies, verify employee assignments and activate policies.

Verify Employee Assignments

  • In the Overtime Policies or Days In Lieu Policies table, check the No. of employees included column to confirm assignments.
  • Click Manage employee assignment in the policy editor to add or remove employees.
  • Employees without an assigned policy cannot log extra hours or receive overtime compensation.

Activate Policies

  • Newly created policies are Active by default.
  • Use the Active/Inactive toggle in the policy table to enable or disable policies.
  • Inactive policies do not calculate overtime or accept extra hours requests.

What Happens After Activation

  • Employees assigned to overtime policies can log extra hours via the mobile app (if employee requests are enabled) or admins/managers can create extra hours records on their behalf.
  • Extra hours records appear in Time > Employee attendance > Time and Pay Adjustments for approval.
  • Approved extra hours flow to the Payroll table as additions (for overtime policies) or to employee leave balances (for days in lieu policies).
  • Line managers with the "decide compensation types" permission can choose between payroll and leave compensation when approving extra hours, if both policy types are assigned to the employee.

Bulk Overtime Upload (Alternative Method)

For companies that calculate overtime outside Bayzat or need to import historical overtime data, use the Mass Upload Variable Pay feature.

Payroll → Payroll table → 3-dot menu → Mass Upload Variable Pays

Admin path: Upload overtime amounts directly to payroll without creating extra hours records.

Upload Process

  • Click the 3-dot menu in the Payroll table header.
  • Select Mass Upload Variable Pays.
  • Download the Excel template. The template includes employee identifiers, names, and columns for each configured overtime policy.
  • Fill in overtime amounts per employee in the policy columns. Leave cells blank for employees with no overtime.
  • Upload the completed Excel file.
  • Amounts appear in the Overtime column of the Payroll table and are included in the next payroll run.

⚠️ Important: Mass upload bypasses the extra hours approval flow. Use this method only when overtime has been pre-approved or calculated externally. Uploaded amounts cannot be traced back to specific extra hours records or attendance data.

Task: Understand Overtime Rate Calculation

Learn how the system calculates hourly overtime rates from employee salary data.

Subtask: Review Rate Calculation Components

Hourly Rate Formula:

Rate multiplier × ((basic salary + allowances) / calendar days) / working hours

Components:

  • Rate multiplier: Set in overtime policy (e.g., 1.5 for time-and-a-half)
  • Basic salary: Employee's base monthly salary
  • Allowances: Optional - include if policy specifies Basic + Allowances
  • Calendar days: Days in the month (e.g., 30, 31) or custom value (e.g., 30.42)
  • Working hours: Standard hours per day (typically 8)

Final Amount: Hourly rate × extra hours duration

Example: Employee with AED 6,000 basic + AED 2,000 allowances, 1.5x multiplier, 30.42 days, 8 hours/day:
Hourly rate = 1.5 × ((6,000 + 2,000) / 30.42) / 8 = AED 49.34/hour
10 extra hours = AED 493.40

Approval Flow

How Overtime Requests Route

Extra hours requests submitted by employees route to line managers for approval. Requests appear in the Pending sub-tab under Time and Pay Adjustments, where approvers review attendance details, compensation type (Payroll or Days In Lieu), and extra hours duration before approving or rejecting.

Approval Queue Interface

Approvers use the Pending tab to review requests, the Approved tab to track processed extra hours, and the Rejected tab to view declined requests.

Request Detail Panel

Each request displays employee attendance data, compensation type selection, and activity comments to support approval decisions.

Routing Examples

How different overtime requests route through approvers:

Example 1: Standard Overtime Request

Employee submits extra hours worked beyond scheduled shift → Line manager reviews attendance details and compensation type → Approves for payroll processing or rejects with comment.

Example 2: Days In Lieu Compensation

Employee requests overtime compensation as leave days → Line manager verifies hours against Days In Lieu policy → Approves conversion to leave balance or rejects if policy threshold not met.

Example 3: Weekend or Holiday Overtime

Employee logs extra hours on non-working day → Line manager confirms work authorization and applies overtime policy rate → Approves with applicable multiplier or rejects if unauthorized.

Compensation Type Decision

If the General settings toggle "Line managers can decide compensation types" is enabled, approvers can switch between Payroll (AED) and Leave (Days In Lieu) during approval. If disabled, the compensation type is determined by the assigned overtime policy.

For detailed approval flow configuration, see the Approval Flow guide.

Business Rules & Limitations

Policy Configuration Constraints

⚠️ By Design: Overtime calculations vary based on policy configuration

Different overtime policies produce different payment amounts for the same extra hours depending on rate type (Single vs Custom), multiplier, and salary components included. Single Rate applies one multiplier to all hours; Custom Rate allows different multipliers for different hour ranges. Salary components (Basic only vs Basic + Allowances) directly affect the hourly rate calculation.

⚠️ By Design: Minimum extra hours threshold applies globally

The minimum extra hours threshold (currently 30 minutes) is a global setting that applies to all employees regardless of their overtime policy. Cannot be set per-policy.

Configuration Item Constraint Impact
Rate Type Choose Single Rate (one multiplier) or Custom Rate (multiple multipliers) Single Rate simplifies payroll but may not reflect policy variations for extended overtime
Salary Components Include Basic only or Basic + Allowances Including allowances increases hourly rate and overtime pay amounts
Days In Lieu Hours Fixed per policy (e.g., 8 hours = 1 day) Cannot vary by employee or day type within same policy
Line Manager Compensation Decision Global toggle affects all managers Cannot enable for some departments and disable for others

Payroll Integration

By Design: No dedicated overtime mass upload option

There is no separate 'Mass Upload Overtime' option in the payroll 3-dot menu. Overtime must be uploaded through 'Mass Upload Variable Pays' which covers all variable pay types including overtime, bonuses, and commissions.

Multi-Currency Considerations

When processing payroll for employees in non-AED currencies:

  • Overtime rates are calculated in the employee's base currency
  • Currency change restrictions apply if unpaid overtime exists
  • Cannot update employee currency if unpaid variable pay (including overtime) exists
  • Excel upload templates show rates in employee currency

Separate Policy Management

By Design: Overtime and Days In Lieu are configured separately

Administrators must configure overtime policies and days in lieu policies separately. There is no unified policy editor for both compensation types. Each has its own accordion section in Settings > Attendance.

Hourly Rate Calculation Formula

Overtime pay is calculated using this formula:

Hourly Rate = Rate Multiplier × ((Basic Salary + Allowances) / Calendar Days) / Working Hours

  • Rate multiplier is set in overtime policy (e.g., 1.5x for first 2 hours, 2x after)
  • Calendar days = days in the month (28-31)
  • Working hours = standard daily hours from work timing schedule
  • Allowances are included only if configured in policy

Edge Cases

  • Overtime spanning multiple months: When extra hours are worked near month-end but approved in the following month, ensure overtime is uploaded to the correct payroll month based on when hours were actually worked, not when approved
  • Employee with multiple overtime policies: If employee's overtime policy changes mid-month, hours before and after the change use different rate calculations - split hours accordingly in upload
  • Negative or excessive hours in upload: System may accept but flag suspicious values - review any overtime exceeding 12 hours per day or negative values before payroll submission
  • Overlapping extra hours and leave: If employee takes leave on a day with recorded extra hours, clarify with line manager which takes precedence based on company policy
  • Policy deleted after hours uploaded: Existing uploaded hours remain but cannot upload new hours under deleted policy - reassign employees to active policy first
  • Custom rate with gaps in hour ranges: If Custom Rate policy defines 0-2 hours at 1.5x and 4+ hours at 2x, hours between 2-4 may default to base rate - define all ranges explicitly
  • Days in lieu balance tracking: Days in lieu granted for overtime are added to employee leave balance but may have expiration dates - confirm balance validity periods with HR
  • Retroactive attendance corrections: If attendance records are corrected after overtime approval, recalculate affected overtime hours and upload adjustment

Data Validation Before Upload

Check these items before uploading overtime to payroll:

  • Employee identifiers match exactly with Work section of employee profiles
  • All overtime hours have been approved by line managers
  • Hours fall within reasonable ranges for your organization (flag anything over 60 hours/month)
  • Correct overtime policy is assigned to each employee
  • Salary components (Basic + Allowances) are up to date in employee records
  • No closed payroll months are being updated
  • Currency matches employee base currency for multi-currency companies

FAQs & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do different overtime policies produce different payment amounts for the same hours?

Overtime calculations vary based on rate type (Single vs Custom), multiplier values, and salary components included (Basic only vs Basic + Allowances). A Single Rate policy uses one multiplier for all hours, while Custom Rate allows different multipliers for different hour ranges.

How do I upload overtime hours in payroll?

Use 'Mass Upload Variable Pays' from the payroll table 3-dot menu. There is no separate overtime upload option—overtime is included with all variable pay types.

Can I set different minimum extra hours thresholds for different policies?

No. The minimum extra hours threshold (e.g., 30 minutes) is a global setting that applies to all employees regardless of their overtime policy.

Can line managers decide compensation types for some teams but not others?

No. The line manager compensation type toggle is global—it applies to all managers or none. Department-level or team-level overrides are not available.

Can the hours-per-day for days in lieu vary by employee or day type?

No. The hours-per-day setting is fixed per policy and cannot vary by employee, weekday vs weekend, or other conditions.

Why are overtime policies and days in lieu policies separate?

By design, administrators configure overtime policies and days in lieu policies separately. There is no unified policy editor for both compensation types.
